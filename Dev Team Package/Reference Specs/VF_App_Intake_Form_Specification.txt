PURPOSE
====================================================================

This document defines the complete intake form for App athletes in
the VirtusFocus direct-to-consumer ecosystem. It is the single
source of truth for what questions are asked, in what format, and
how each answer routes through the pipeline.

The form serves as a UNIFIED INPUT: one question set, answered once
by the athlete, from which the system derives three outputs:

  1. ATHLETE SNAPSHOT — Expanded narrative baseline profile
     (9+ sections, 300-500 words)
  2. PPD TOP 3 — Deterministic ranking of primary problems
     (8 buckets, scored and ranked)
  3. ABI 4-PILLAR SCORES — Quantitative developmental baseline
     (Ownership, Composure, Focus, Structure; each 0-10, total 0-40)

No separate question sets exist. Each question is asked ONCE.
Shared inputs are routed to multiple systems simultaneously.


DESIGN PRINCIPLES
====================================================================

1. UNIFIED INPUT, DERIVED OUTPUTS — One question set produces
   Snapshot + PPD + ABI. No duplication.

2. SIGNAL PER MINUTE — Optimized for maximum coaching signal per
   minute of athlete time. Many structured questions (1-5 scales,
   multi-select, single-select) + few strategic free-text questions.

3. DUAL-USE ARCHITECTURE — This form is for App athletes ONLY.
   Core Foundation athletes retain their existing 22-question form
   and current Snapshot Builder process. The Snapshot Builder
   detects which form was submitted and routes accordingly.

4. LOCKED BASELINE FIELDS — These 4 fields exist in the current
   pipeline and do not change. New fields sit alongside them:
   - baseline_identity_claim
   - baseline_hinge_habit
   - baseline_primary_derailer
   - baseline_growth_phase

5. PPD LONGITUDINAL DESIGN — Self-report PPD is computed at intake
   (this form). Execution-derived longitudinal PPD is a future
   Stage 2 enhancement. The PPD output format supports future
   comparison (PPD Alignment Signal).

SECTION 3 — PERFORMANCE FRICTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Identify the primary problems VirtusFocus must solve.
         This is the most important section for PPD scoring.
Cognitive load: Moderate. Multi-select requires reading options.
Estimated time: ~30 seconds.

Q15. "What usually gets in the way of your best performance?"
     Format: Multi-select, UP TO 3
     Options (8 — one per PPD bucket):
       □ Overthinking
       □ Confidence going up and down
       □ Hard time letting go of mistakes
       □ Losing focus or getting distracted
       □ Pressure in big moments
       □ Staying motivated or disciplined
       □ Lack of routine or structure
       □ Pressure from coaches or parents
     Source: UPGRADED from CF Q12
             (CF had 10 options with overlap; consolidated to 8
              with 1-to-1 PPD bucket mapping, capped at 3)
     Routes to: PPD (selection weight), Snapshot
     Field: performance_friction_selections[]
     PPD Role: Each selection adds +3 to the corresponding PPD bucket.
               Maximum 3 selections = maximum 3 buckets activated.

     PPD BUCKET MAPPING:
       Overthinking                      → Overthinking Loop
       Confidence going up and down      → Confidence Volatility
       Hard time letting go of mistakes  → Mistake Recovery Lag
       Losing focus or getting distracted → Focus Drift / Distraction
       Pressure in big moments           → Pressure Reactivity
       Staying motivated or disciplined  → Discipline Gap
       Lack of routine or structure      → Structure Gap
       Pressure from coaches or parents  → Ecosystem Friction

     NO "Other" option. The 8 options cover the VirtusFocus problem
     taxonomy completely. Free-text questions in Section 4 capture
     anything the structured options cannot express.

Q16. "What situation makes this worse?"
     Format: Multi-select, UP TO 2
     Options (5 — trigger context amplifiers):
       □ Big competitions or high-stakes moments
       □ After making a mistake
       □ When being evaluated (coach, scouts, recruiters)
       □ During busy or stressful life weeks
       □ When expectations from others are high
     Source: NEW
     Routes to: PPD (trigger amplifier), Snapshot
     Field: trigger_context_selections[]
     PPD Role: Each trigger selection adds amplifier weight to
               specific PPD buckets:
       Big competitions     → +2 Pressure Reactivity, +1 Overthinking
       After a mistake      → +2 Mistake Recovery Lag, +1 Confidence Volatility
       Being evaluated      → +2 Overthinking, +1 Confidence Volatility
       Busy/stressful weeks → +2 Discipline Gap, +1 Focus Drift
       High expectations    → +2 Pressure Reactivity, +1 Confidence Volatility
     Exact amplifier weights confirmed in PPD Scoring Logic (Task 2).

SECTION 4 — IDENTITY & PRESSURE
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Capture the athlete's core identity language and pressure
         narrative. These are the highest-value coaching inputs.
Cognitive load: High. Free-text requires genuine reflection.
Estimated time: ~90 seconds (3 questions × ~30 sec each).

Q17. "At my best, I compete like someone who..."
     Format: Free-text sentence completion
     Source: NEW
     Routes to: Snapshot
     Field: identity_sentence_completion
     Pipeline Role: Primary input for baseline_identity_claim
                    derivation. The Snapshot Builder synthesizes
                    this with Q19 (competitor aspiration) to produce
                    the identity claim anchor.
     Examples athletes might write:
       "stays calm under pressure"
       "attacks every rep"
       "trusts my training"
       "doesn't back down"

Q18. "Before a big moment, what thought shows up most often?"
     Format: Free-text, short answer
     Display hint: Examples: "Don't mess this up" / "Let's go" /
                   "I need to prove myself"
     Source: UPGRADED from CF Q13 (reworded for precision —
             CF asked "What usually goes through your mind before
             a big moment?" which invited narrative; this asks for
             the single dominant thought)
     Routes to: PPD (tie-breaker), Snapshot
     Field: pressure_thought_text
     PPD Role: Used for tie-breaker classification when two or
               more PPD buckets have equal scores:
       Avoidance framing ("Don't mess up", "What if I fail") →
         +1 Overthinking Loop, +1 Pressure Reactivity
       Proving framing ("I need to prove myself") →
         +1 Confidence Volatility
       Obligation framing ("I can't let them down") →
         +1 Pressure Reactivity, +1 Ecosystem Friction
       Approach framing ("Let's go", "Trust the process") →
         No tie-breaker adjustment (positive indicator)
     Classification logic confirmed in PPD Scoring Logic (Task 2).

Q19. "What kind of competitor do you want to become?"
     Format: Free-text, short answer
     Source: NEW
     Routes to: Snapshot, Longitudinal
     Field: competitor_aspiration_text
     Pipeline Role: Identity aspiration anchor. Combined with Q17
                    (identity sentence) to build the Snapshot's
                    identity profile. Longitudinal because the
                    aspiration at intake can be compared against
                    identity language that emerges in weekly data
                    over time.

SECTION 5 — BEHAVIORAL PATTERNS
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose: Capture behavioral response and identity self-perception
         under adversity. Fast single-selects with high signal.
Cognitive load: Low-moderate. Single-select, clear options.
Estimated time: ~15 seconds.

Q20. "When something goes wrong in competition, what do you usually
      do first?"
     Format: Single-select
     Options:
       ○ Blame the situation
       ○ Get frustrated
       ○ Try to adjust right away
       ○ Reset and refocus
     Source: NEW (from High-Value Intake Additions)
     Routes to: Snapshot
     Field: adversity_response_pattern
     Pipeline Role: Captures behavioral response vs. reaction
                    pattern. Distinct from Q9 (reset speed) which
                    measures HOW FAST — this measures WHAT HAPPENS
                    FIRST. Directly feeds Snapshot Section 4 (Known
                    Derailers) and Section 7 (Stress & Trigger
                    Profile). Also informs baseline_hinge_habit
                    derivation (if answer is "Reset and refocus,"
                    the hinge habit may already be present).

Q21. "After a bad performance, how do you usually describe yourself?"
     Format: Single-select
     Options:
       ○ I question my ability
       ○ I get frustrated but move forward
       ○ I analyze what happened and adjust
       ○ I stay confident in myself
     Source: NEW (from High-Value Intake Additions)
     Routes to: Snapshot
     Field: adversity_self_description
     Pipeline Role: Captures identity self-perception under adversity —
                    a different dimension from Q20 (behavioral response).
                    Feeds Snapshot Section 5 (Confidence Pattern) and
                    Section 6 (Identity Friction). Reveals whether
                    identity is stable or outcome-reactive under
                    performance pressure.

====================================================================
DERIVED OUTPUTS (computed by downstream systems)
====================================================================

These are NOT stored as raw form answers. They are computed from
the raw fields above by their respective systems.

1. PPD TOP 3
   Computed by: PPD Scoring Logic (Task 2)
   Inputs: performance_friction_selections, trigger_context_selections,
           abi_composure_q1 (reset speed amplifier),
           abi_structure_q1 (routine amplifier),
           home_conversation_pattern (ecosystem amplifier),
           pressure_thought_text (tie-breaker)
   Output: Ranked list of top 3 Primary Problems with scores,
           trigger context, and coaching implication line.
   8 PPD Buckets:
     1. Overthinking Loop
     2. Confidence Volatility
     3. Mistake Recovery Lag
     4. Focus Drift / Distraction
     5. Pressure Reactivity
     6. Discipline Gap
     7. Structure Gap
     8. Ecosystem Friction

2. ABI 4-PILLAR SCORES
   Computed by: ABI Scoring Logic (Task 3)
   Inputs: abi_ownership_q1, abi_ownership_q2,
           abi_composure_q1, abi_composure_q2,
           abi_focus_q1, abi_focus_q2,
           abi_structure_q1, abi_structure_q2
   Output:
     Ownership:  0-10
     Composure:  0-10
     Focus:      0-10
     Structure:  0-10
     Total:      0-40
     Primary Emphasis: lowest 1-2 dimensions
   Formula: pillar_score = round((Q_a + Q_b) / 2 * 2)
   Bands: 0-16 Needs Foundation / 17-28 Developing /
          29-36 Consistent / 37-40 Leadership

3. EXPANDED ATHLETE SNAPSHOT

FORM SEQUENCING RATIONALE
====================================================================

The form follows a deliberate cognitive load progression:

  Section 1 (Context): START EASY — demographic dropdowns and short
  text. Zero self-reflection required. Gets the athlete moving
  through the form with quick completions.

  Section 2 (Scales): BUILD TO SELF-ASSESSMENT — 1-5 scales require
  moderate self-awareness but are fast to complete. The scale format
  feels objective ("rate yourself") rather than vulnerable.

  Section 3 (Friction): PEAK DIAGNOSTIC — multi-select forces the
  athlete to identify their top problems. This is the most
  diagnostically valuable section but requires honest self-assessment.
  Placed after scales have warmed up the athlete's self-reflection.

  Section 4 (Identity): PEAK DEPTH — free-text identity and pressure
  questions require the most genuine thought. Placed at the peak of
  the form when the athlete is fully engaged but not yet fatigued.

  Section 5 (Behavioral): QUICK STRUCTURED — single-selects that
  capture behavioral patterns without requiring deep reflection.
  Provides a cognitive break after the free-text peak.

  Section 6 (Ecosystem): EXTERNAL FOCUS — shifts attention outward
  to family and support dynamics. Lower cognitive load because the
  athlete is describing their environment, not evaluating themselves.

  Section 7 (Goals): END FORWARD-LOOKING — closes on goals,
  commitment, and an optional catch-all. Ends the form on an
  energizing, positive note rather than a self-critical one.
