Grace —

Forty-nine straight Win days. Seven consecutive Growth weeks. And this week brought something none of the previous six did — your first competitive setback. Monday you stepped in as a pinch-hitter, you struck out, and the first thing you wrote was "that sucks." By Wednesday, you wrote "I just need to reset and I'll be fine." That two-day sequence is the most important thing that has happened in this program.

Last week, the commitment was to write one specific action you wanted to execute before the game — something you could picture doing. No evidence of the written commitment showed up in the recap. Your warmup preparation stayed consistent, but the specific one-sentence pre-game action remains an open loop. That commitment carries forward — it is designed for exactly the moments you faced this week.

Here is what earned recognition this week.

You handled a competitive failure the way someone who controls her confidence handles it. Brief disappointment. No spiral. No multi-day disruption. Reset language within 48 hours. That is your hinge habit — the one you have been building since Week 1 — working under the exact conditions it was designed for. When the outcome went against you, you came back to your center ring and kept going. That is not something most people do after a strikeout in a game that mattered to them.

Saturday you did not play and your response was to be loud, present, and prepared. That is a choice. "When I don't I'm still up and loud and prepared" — that is you naming your own controllable on the days when you have no competitive opportunity. That composure is what keeps you in position for the next chance.

Here is what needs attention. Goal-setting has now been at its lowest point for two consecutive weeks — "Keep working hard" and "Same thing." Your execution has never been stronger, but when asked what you want to accomplish, the answer has become automatic. You have proven you can show up every day. The next edge is showing up with a specific target every game day. Your philosophy works. Putting a name on the action before each moment keeps it sharp.

You came into this program wanting to stay confident during competition. This week you proved something bigger — that your confidence survives failure, not just success. That is the version of you that can handle anything the season throws at you.

YOUR ACTION — AFTER EACH GAME DAY THIS WEEK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

After each game day this week — whether you play or not — write one sentence naming something you controlled well that day.

Not a result. Not playing time. Not what the coach did. One controllable action you owned.

If you played: name the action. If you did not play: name how you showed up.

At the end of the week, you should have one sentence per game day — each naming something you controlled. If every sentence is about something in your center ring, the commitment is working.

Control what is yours.
— Coach Arron
