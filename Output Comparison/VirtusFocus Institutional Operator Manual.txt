1. VirtusFocus Identity Doctrine

VirtusFocus was built on a simple truth: athletes don’t fail because they’re weak; they fail because they don’t have structure.
The program solves the gap left by traditional mindset coaching approaches (long lessons, motivational talks, one-time sessions) by operationalizing daily and weekly behavior into a system athletes can run themselves.

3. The Three-Loop Behavioral Engine (Core Differentiator)
VirtusFocus runs on three integrated loops: daily execution, day-to-day identity calibration, and weekly coaching integration—all athlete-led.
3.1 Intra-Day Loop — Commit → Execute → Confirm
Purpose: transform intention into execution and close the loop the same day.
Field	Definition / Requirements
Inputs	Daily Tune-Up commitment; daily context; athlete intention
Outputs	Execution behavior; evening confirmation; WTD score
Success Signal	Athlete follows through on one controllable behavior and confirms it
3.2 Inter-Day Loop — Reflect → Recalibrate → Recommit
Purpose: stabilize identity across days and prevent reset-based thinking.
Field	Definition / Requirements
Inputs	Yesterday’s outcomes; WTD scores; journaling; Bullseye entries
Outputs	Adjusted next-day focus; simplified commitments after misses; standards after wins
Success Signal	Athlete returns to system without shame; learning carries forward
3.3 Weekly Loop — Review → Coach → Integrate
Purpose: pattern recognition + course correction; convert week into insight and direction.
Field	Definition / Requirements
Inputs	7 days WTD; 7 days journaling; 7 days Bullseye; weekly recap; motivation inventory; goals; coaching history
Outputs	Weekly coaching analysis; next-week focus areas; targeted challenges; flags
Success Signal	One clear coaching priority and an integrated plan that shapes the next week
4. Core Modules and Operating Specifications
4.1 Daily Mindset Tune-Up (Morning)
Definition: a quick, intentional mindset check-in to start the day with clarity and control (under 5 minutes; ideally 1–3 minutes).
Required elements
•	Identity anchor (who the athlete is choosing to be)
•	Controllable focus (Bullseye center ring language)
•	One micro-commitment / promise for the day
•	Simple language, no overwhelm
Coaching quality ladder (used for evaluation)
Field	Definition / Requirements
Level 1 — Passive	Generic: 'Be positive.'
Level 2 — Habit-focused	Specific habit: 'Stay composed in practice.'
Level 3 — Identity-based	Value/identity anchored: 'Lead with calm presence.'
Level 4 — Integrated	Scenario + tools: 'When I feel nervous, breathe, set posture, focus on next rep.'
4.2 Win the Day (Evening — Step 1)
Definition: five yes/no questions, each worth 1 point. The anchor of the Evening Review workflow.
The five questions (intent)
Field	Definition / Requirements
Q1 — Act with intention	Did I follow a plan or was I reactive today?
Q2 — Keep the AM promise	Did I complete the commitment set in the Tune-Up?
Q3 — Respond to adversity	Did I respond (not react) when things got hard?
Q4 — Progress over perfection	Did I show up and adjust instead of expecting perfect?
Q5 — Gratitude / self-compassion	Did I end grounded rather than self-critical?
Scoring interpretation
•	4–5 = Win the Day (aligned)
•	2–3 = Partial Win (mixed)
•	0–1 = Missed the Mark (drift)
Weekly categorization (0–35)
•	28–35 = Growth Week
•	14–27 = Mixed Week
•	0–13 = Reset Week
Operational coaching rule
Coaches evaluate patterns over time, not single-day scores. A low score is a mirror, not a grade.
4.3 Daily Journaling (Evening — Step 2)
Definition: the narrative component of the Evening Review where athletes reflect in their own words across three life environments.
Required fields
•	Work / School
•	Practice / Competition
•	Home / Life
Experience requirements
•	Simple, safe, quick (~60–90 seconds)
•	Non-judgmental microcopy
•	Auto-save to prevent loss
AI analysis responsibilities (minimum)
•	Detect emotional tone and shifts
•	Identify recurring stressors and triggers
•	Link context to WTD scoring patterns
•	Identify identity language vs outcome fixation
•	Flag burnout/overwhelm indicators and sudden changes in reflection behavior
4.4 Bullseye Method (Evening — Step 3, plus optional real-time)
Definition: the control framework that classifies focus into Control / Influence / Can't Control.
Daily integration
•	Occurs after WTD + Journaling; athlete must list at least one item in each ring.
AI coaching responsibilities (minimum)
•	Assess where the athlete ‘lived’ (center/middle/outer) daily and weekly
•	Identify drift into outer ring and redirect to controllables
•	Detect misclassification (e.g., trying to control uncontrollables)
•	Deliver a Bullseye-based action instruction for the next week
Optional real-time Bullseye (future)
•	Quick-access: 'Use Bullseye Now'
•	Minimal inputs: what happened, 3 ring fields, optional tag (competition/school/home/etc.)
•	Included in weekly review and trend flags
4.5 Weekly Recap & Motivation Inventory (Sunday)
Definition: a structured weekly reflection module capturing achievements, resonance, goals, context, and competitions.
Required Motivation Inventory questions
•	What did you achieve this week?
•	Favorite Mindset Challenge from Tune-Ups?
•	Goals for the upcoming week?
•	Anything else relevant?
•	Competitions scheduled?
AI uses this to
•	Evaluate goal alignment vs drift
•	Assess identity language strength
•	Detect readiness for deeper challenge or intervention
•	Select one core coaching priority for next week
4.6 Deep Dive Coaching Analysis (Weekly Output)
Definition: professional-grade weekly analysis in VirtusFocus Full Review format.
Mandatory structure
1.	1) Weekly Overview (Status + Theme)
2.	2) Strengths & Wins (4–6 sections; each includes headline, evidence, Coach Insight/Cue)
3.	3) Opportunities & Growth Areas (3–5 sections; coaching input + action)
4.	4) Mindset Summary (one paragraph identity trajectory)
5.	5) Coaching Focus Areas (Next Week) (4–5 bullets)
Style constraints
•	Concise, professional, emotionally intelligent language
•	No fluff or filler; every paragraph must add insight
•	Calm, grounded, confident tone; never overhype or criticize
•	Use 'Coach Insight', 'Coach Cue', or 'Coaching Input' anchors in sections
•	Readable formatting with whitespace, subheadings, numbering

5.4 Scenario templates (institutional default)

Template

Template

A5. Deep Dive Coaching Prompt Spec (excerpted)
VirtusFocus Deep Dive Coaching Analysis System Prompt File
------------------------------------------------------------

SYSTEM IDENTITY:
You are Coach Arron, the lead VirtusFocus Mindset Coach. Your purpose is to review each athlete’s weekly recap and generate a professional-grade Deep Dive Coaching Analysis in the exact format and tone used in the VirtusFocus Full Reviews.

Each output must reflect the athlete’s emotional state, mindset trends, and alignment with their VirtusFocus goals while offering high-level professional coaching insight.

------------------------------------------------------------
OUTPUT STRUCTURE (MANDATORY):

1. Weekly Overview
   - Status: Gold Streak / Silver Streak / Reset Week, etc.
   - Theme: 1–2 sentences summarizing emotional tone and focus of the week.

2. Strengths & Wins
   - Provide 4–6 numbered sections.
   - Each section includes:
     a. Headline (short phrase, e.g., "Emotional Composure Under Pressure")
     b. Description (observations, quotes, or behaviors from recap)
     c. Coach Insight or Coach Cue (italicized line giving coaching perspective)

3. Opportunities & Growth Areas
   - Provide 3–5 numbered sections.
   - Each section includes:
     a. Headline
     b. Explanation (cause/effect logic, connection to goals)
     c. Coaching Cue or Coaching Input (italicized actionable guidance)

4. Mindset Summary
   - One concise paragraph summarizing the athlete’s phase of growth or transition.
   - Example: “Athlete has moved from reactive confidence to identity-based consistency.”

5. Coaching Focus Areas (Next Week)
   - 4–5 bullet points summarizing the main coaching priorities or behavioral focuses.

------------------------------------------------------------
STYLE GUIDELINES:

• Match tone and structure exactly as seen in the VirtusFocus Full Review examples.
• Use concise, professional, emotionally intelligent language.
• No fluff or filler — each paragraph must provide measurable insight.
• Use subheadings, indentation, and white space for clarity.
• Maintain calm, grounded, confident tone — never overhype or criticize.
• Always use “Coach Insight,” “Coach Cue,” or “Coaching Input” to anchor advice.
• Avoid repetitive phrasing — vary sentence rhythm.
• Each section should be formatted for readability in exported PDF reports.

------------------------------------------------------------
ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK:

When reading a recap:
1. Identify mindset strengths and behavioral patterns.
2. Locate key emotional markers (language shifts, tone, fatigue, confidence).
3. Map observations to the Bullseye Method:
   • Center: controllables
   • Middle: influence
   • Outer: uncontrollables
4. Reframe all feedback toward controllable actions.
5. Emphasize identity-based growth (“I am becoming…” vs “I want to…”).
