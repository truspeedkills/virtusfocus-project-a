============================================================
1. WHAT IS THE FORWARD ANCHOR?
============================================================

A single-line text input on the Weekly Recap screen with the
prompt: "One thing you can control next week."

The athlete writes one short, controllable focus they carry
into the next week. Not a goal list, not a wish — one
specific thing within their control.

Examples (not shown to athlete):
  - "Respond calmly to mistakes."
  - "Complete my morning challenge every day."
  - "Bring energy to practice."

The Forward Anchor is the psychological bridge between weeks.
The Weekly Recap closes the current week — the Forward Anchor
is the one piece that points forward. By the time the athlete
taps Submit, the feeling should be: "Last week is closed. I
know what I'm carrying into next week."

============================================================
3. HOW IT DIFFERS FROM EXISTING FIELDS
============================================================

Three related but distinct concepts already exist:

MICRO_COMMITMENT (Stage 3 output — coach-assigned)
  - Generated by the Coaching Output Engine in the ONE Action
    section of each weekly Coaching Message
  - Coach Arron assigns a specific behavioral action
  - Tracked via prior_commitment_result the following week
  - Has modality rules (Written, Behavioral, Verbal, Oral)
  - The athlete does NOT author this — the coach does

Q3_UPCOMING_GOALS (Motivation Inventory — athlete-authored)
  - Free text: "What are your goals for the upcoming week?"
  - Open-ended, no controllability constraint
  - Can contain multiple goals, outcome-oriented wishes, etc.
  - Routes to upcoming_context.athlete_stated_next_goal
  - No alignment tracking against subsequent behavior

FORWARD ANCHOR (proposed — athlete-authored)
  - Single-line, constrained to ONE controllable focus
  - Explicitly framed around controllability ("one thing you
    can control"), not goals or outcomes
  - Creates a perception-reality comparison: what the athlete
    says they will focus on vs. what they actually do
  - Week-to-week consistency signal (stable focus vs. scattered
    attention)
  - Bridges the weekly close to the next week's daily actions

The Forward Anchor fills a gap none of the existing fields
cover: a constrained, controllable, athlete-authored weekly
intention with behavioral alignment tracking.

============================================================
4. SIGNAL VALUE FOR THE PIPELINE
============================================================

The Forward Anchor produces three distinct analytical signals:

SIGNAL 1: CONTROLLABILITY CLASSIFICATION
  Does the athlete think in controllable vs. outcome terms?
  - Controllable: "Bring energy to practice every day"
  - Outcome-Oriented: "Win my next match"
  - Mixed: "Play confident in the big moments"
  Stage 2 classifies each week. Longitudinal pattern reveals
  whether the athlete's thinking shifts over time (outcome →
  controllable is a growth indicator aligned with the Bullseye
  Method's center-ring philosophy).

SIGNAL 2: WEEK-TO-WEEK FOCUS CONSISTENCY
  Compare current forward_anchor to prior week(s):
  - Consistent: same theme or related controllable focus
  - Shifting: different focus area each week
  - Scattered: no coherent thread across 3+ weeks
  Consistent focus is not always better — it depends on
  context. But scattered attention across many weeks with no
  follow-through is a coaching signal.

SIGNAL 3: FORWARD ANCHOR ALIGNMENT
  Does what the athlete anchored on show up in their actual
  behavior the following week? Tracked via:
  - Morning Tune-Up focus words (does focus_word relate?)
  - Bullseye entries (does center-ring activity align?)
  - Journal reflections (does the athlete reference it?)
  - WTD question patterns (do yes-rates in related areas
    shift?)

  Classification:
  - Aligned: behavioral evidence matches stated anchor
  - Partial: some evidence but inconsistent
  - Misaligned: no behavioral evidence of stated focus
  - Insufficient Data: not enough daily data to assess

  This mirrors the self_ratings perception-reality alignment
  pattern — but at the intention level rather than the
  self-assessment level.

  (new version)
  Change: Forward Anchor integration rules. Key design
    decision: how does the coach-assigned micro_commitment
    interact with the athlete-authored Forward Anchor?

  THREE OPTIONS TO RESOLVE IN DESIGN SESSION:

  OPTION A — Independent Tracks
    micro_commitment stays coach-assigned, Forward Anchor is
    acknowledged but separate. Coach may reference the
    athlete's anchor ("You said you wanted to bring energy
    to practice — here's how to make that real this week...")
    but the micro_commitment is still coach-directed.

  OPTION B — Anchor-Informed Commitment
    The micro_commitment builds ON the athlete's Forward
    Anchor when alignment is strong. If the athlete's anchor
    is controllable and specific, the coach uses it as the
    foundation and adds coaching precision. If the anchor is
    outcome-oriented or vague, the coach redirects toward a
    controllable alternative.

  OPTION C — Anchor Replaces Commitment (Premium only)
    For Premium athletes with established controllability
    patterns, the Forward Anchor IS the commitment — the
    coach validates and sharpens it instead of assigning a
    new one. Base tier and early-program athletes still get
    coach-assigned micro_commitments.

  This is the biggest design decision in this proposal.
  Estimated scope: ~60-100 lines depending on option chosen

============================================================
6. DESIGN QUESTIONS TO RESOLVE
============================================================

Q1: MICRO_COMMITMENT INTERACTION MODEL
  How does the coach-assigned micro_commitment interact with
  the athlete-authored Forward Anchor? (Options A/B/C above)
  This affects Stage 3 routing and the entire coaching
  relationship between coach direction and athlete autonomy.

Q2: ALIGNMENT TIMING
  Forward Anchor Alignment is inherently a NEXT-WEEK signal
  (you state your anchor this week, alignment is measured
  next week). This means:
  - Week 1 forward_anchor_alignment = always "No Prior Week"
  - The signal is always one week behind
  - Same timing pattern as prior_commitment_result
  Confirm this is acceptable or explore alternatives.

Q3: CONTROLLABILITY CLASSIFICATION METHOD
  Pure keyword-based (deterministic) or agent-classified
  (judgment-based)? Controllability is nuanced:
  - "Play with confidence" — is this controllable?
  - "Be a good teammate" — controllable but vague
  - "Win my match" — clearly outcome
  Recommendation: agent-classified with guided examples,
  similar to PPD tie-breaker classification. But this adds
  LLM judgment where the pipeline prefers deterministic
  rules. Design session should resolve.

Q4: BASE TIER FORWARD ANCHOR
  Base tier athletes submit the Weekly Recap (which includes
  the Forward Anchor) but do NOT receive a Coaching Message
  with a micro_commitment. The Forward Anchor may be their
  ONLY forward-looking element. Does this change how Stage 6
  uses it for Base tier snippets?

Q5: Q3_UPCOMING_GOALS REDUNDANCY
  With the Forward Anchor added, q3_upcoming_goals becomes
  partially redundant. The Forward Anchor is more constrained
  and analytically useful. Options:
  - Keep both (q3 stays open-ended for context, Forward
    Anchor is the structured signal)
  - Retire q3 and let Forward Anchor + q5_competition_schedule
    cover the forward-looking space
  - Merge: reframe q3 to be broader context only ("Anything
    coming up next week?") and let Forward Anchor own the
    intention space

Q6: COMPLIANCE CLASSIFICATION
  Is the raw Forward Anchor text athlete-private (like journal
  content — never shown to coaches) or athlete-visible (like
  WTD scores — can inform coach-facing output)?
  Recommendation: raw text stays private (inform-only for
  Stage 4), but the controllability classification and
  alignment signal are compliance-safe for coach-facing
  output in translated form.
