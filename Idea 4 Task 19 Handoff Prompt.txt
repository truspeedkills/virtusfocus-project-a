TASK 17 — PARENT CM DESIGN (Session 2):

  SECTION STRUCTURE (4 sections):
  1. Weekly Context
     What the athlete is building this week. High-level
     theme, tier-level framing without naming the tier.
     Source: weekly_tier, core_pattern, consistency_profile

  2. What Stood Out
     One specific strength or development signal the parent
     can recognize and reinforce at home. Behavioral, not
     technical.
     Source: coach_insights.key_habit_signal, control_quality,
     athlete_voice (if natural fit)

  3. What's Ahead
     Forward-looking context so the parent can create the
     right environment. Not the micro_commitment itself
     (that is athlete-facing). The theme of next week's work.
     Source: upcoming_context, micro_commitment.action
     (thematic reference only), narrative_arc.open_thread

  4. How You Can Support
     One concrete, actionable support suggestion for the
     parent. Drawn from the week's data, not generic. This
     is the anchor section and differentiator.
     Source: Ecosystem fields (Q22-Q25), growth_phase,
     emotional_intensity, core_pattern

  DESIGN PRINCIPLE: Every section helps the parent support
  the athlete's mental performance growth. No surveillance.
  No monitoring. No reporting on behavior.

  LENGTH: 150-250 words

  CM REFERENCE: Thematically aligned, never directly
  references the Athlete CM. Parent never knows what's in
  the athlete's coaching message.

  PARENT DEEP DIVE: No. Parent Insights serves that function.

  VOICE: Coach Arron third-person register. Informational +
  support guidance. Does NOT coach the parent. Updates and
  suggests how to support.

  Voice calibration:
    Dimension          | Athlete CM              | Parent CM
    Person             | Second ("You showed")   | Third ("Grace showed")
    Action orientation | Athlete acts            | Parent supports
    Micro-commitment   | Directed at athlete     | NOT included
    Accountability     | Direct (per comp level) | Absent
    Recognition        | Behavioral              | Contextual
    Tone               | Coaching (challenge +   | Informational +
                       |   support)              |   guidance

  WHAT IS NOT IN THE PARENT CM:
  - No prior_commitment_result bridge
  - No drift identification section
  - No Trendline Decision
  - No Weekly Action section
  - No Identity Thread
  - No micro-commitment assignment to the parent
  - No accountability language directed at the parent

DESIGN QUESTIONS TO RESOLVE DURING SPECIFICATION
============================================================

DQ1. Ecosystem Field Calibration Depth
  The intake form captures 4 ecosystem fields (Q22-Q25):
  Q22: Who do you live with during your season?
  Q23: How do people at home talk about your sport?
  Q24: Who is your main support person outside of coaching?
  Q25: Does your support person know about VirtusFocus?

  These directly inform the "How You Can Support" section.
  Question: How detailed should the calibration be? Options:
  (a) Simple: Q23 value determines general tone (Supportive
      → reinforce, Pressure → buffer, Don't discuss → open
      door). Q22/Q24/Q25 provide context but no formal rules.
  (b) Detailed: Per-combination rules for Q22+Q23+Q24+Q25
      producing specific support suggestion templates.
  (c) Moderate: Q23 as primary calibration with Q25 as
      secondary modifier (aware vs. not aware of VirtusFocus
      changes what you can reference).

DQ2. Emotional Intensity Handling
  When emotional_intensity = High in the JSON:
  - Athlete CM leads with empathetic acknowledgment
  - Should Parent CM also shift? Options:
  (a) Yes: "How You Can Support" becomes warmer and more
      protective. "What Stood Out" may acknowledge the
      emotional weight without naming it.
  (b) Light touch: Only "How You Can Support" adjusts.
      Other sections stay informational.
  (c) No change: Parent CM stays informational regardless.
      The Parent Insights narrative handles emotional context.

DQ3. Derailer-Activation Weeks
  When the athlete's primary derailer activates (baseline_
  alignment.alignment_status = "Partially Aligned" or
  "Not Aligned"):
  - Athlete CM addresses drift directly
  - Should Parent CM acknowledge this? Options:
  (a) Yes, indirectly: "What's Ahead" notes the athlete is
      working on something specific. "How You Can Support"
      adjusts to provide space/stability.
  (b) No: Parent CM stays positive/neutral. Parent Insights
      Section 8 ("Something to Be Aware Of") handles this.

DQ4. Growth Phase Advancement
  When growth_phase advances (e.g., Developing -> Consistent):
  - Athlete CM celebrates the milestone
  - Should Parent CM also celebrate? Options:
  (a) Yes: "Weekly Context" or "What Stood Out" names the
      advancement in parent-appropriate language.
  (b) Subtle: Phase name not used, but tone reflects the
      milestone ("Grace has reached a new level of
      consistency in her routine").

DQ5. Reference Examples
  How many reference Parent CM examples should be embedded
  in the specification? Minimum 3 proposed:
  (a) Growth week, Low EI, no competition upcoming
  (b) Mixed week, High EI, derailer activated
  (c) Growth week, upcoming competition, phase advancement
  More examples improve consistency. Fewer keep the spec
  lighter. The Athlete CM spec has 5 reference snippets in
  the Master Prompt.
