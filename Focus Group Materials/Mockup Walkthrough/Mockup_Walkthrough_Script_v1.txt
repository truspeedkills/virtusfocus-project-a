CRITICAL DELIVERY NOTES
=======================
- ZERO em dashes anywhere in this script (project convention).
  Use period, comma, or colon. Never substitute em dash on the fly.
- "VirtusFocus" already established in pitch Section 1. Plain
  VirtusFocus throughout this script.
- Athletes are anonymized as Athlete A / B / C from the stage,
  even when on-screen mockup data shows real names. The Inbox
  (Feed) screen contains real Wk12 content from Athlete A's
  actual program with her name visible in the rendering. Refer
  to her as "Athlete A" or "this athlete" from the stage.
- Tag-team structure: Arron drives screens 1 through 9
  (athlete-side flow). Single hand-off seam after Inbox (Feed)
  to Nicole, who closes with snapshot, coach, parent, team,
  audit, and the lunch transition.
- Smart-board sequence: 9 screens from the filtered
  Mockup_Walkthrough_Gallery.html, then 4 reader pages in this
  order: Snapshot Reader, Coach Reader, Parent Reader, Team
  Snapshot Reader.
- Each segment's printed packet contains the artifact Nicole
  holds up for that subsection. After Phase 7 packet
  symmetrization, Coach Insight, Parent CM, Team Snapshot, and
  Athlete A snapshot are in every packet.

SECTION 1: SCREEN 1 - HOME (MORNING)
Speaker: Arron | ~155 words | ~1.5 minutes
=====================================================

This is what the athlete sees first thing in the morning.
The entire daily surface lives here.

[GESTURE TO SCREEN]

Greeting at the top. The big amber card in the middle is
today's Mindset Tune-Up. The most important current task
always rises to the top of the feed. The focus word is
already on the card, pre-rendered. Below the Tune-Up card,
the Bullseye and the Coaching Feedback Preview, both always
present.

[BEAT]

Tuesday through Sunday, there's also a yesterday-to-today

SECTION 2: SCREEN 2 - TUNE-UP (ACTIVE)
Speaker: Arron | ~215 words | ~2.5 minutes
=====================================================

[CLICK TO SCREEN 2: TUNE-UP (ACTIVE).]

When the athlete taps Start, this is the Morning Tune-Up.
About one to three minutes for the athlete to interact with.

[GESTURE TO SCREEN]

Single scrollable screen. Mindset Statement at the top in

SECTION 3: SCREEN 3 - BULLSEYE (MID-ENTRY)
Speaker: Arron | ~225 words | ~2.5 minutes
=====================================================

[CLICK TO SCREEN 3: BULLSEYE (MID-ENTRY).]

This is the Bullseye method. The athlete uses this any time
something happens during the day they want to sort. It's
also part of the end-of-day review, but it stands alone
here.

[GESTURE TO SCREEN]

Three labels on screen. Can Control. Can Influence. Can't
Control. They correspond to the rings of a bullseye. The
athlete writes what happened at the top, then categorizes
pieces of it into the right ring. We let them tag the
event. In this case, Practice slash Training is selected.
Earlier moments from this same day are saved as cards
below.

[PACE SHIFTS. DELIBERATE.]

This is where the pipeline reads what the athlete is
focusing on versus what they're owning. If the Can't Control
ring is filling up faster than the Can Control ring across
a week, that's a coaching signal. If the athlete is properly
categorizing the controllable, recognizing what they
actually can act on versus what is noise, that's also a
coaching signal. The system reads both directions. The
athlete never sees the signal. The system uses it, along
with hundreds of other signals, to write the next coaching
message and deep dive.

[PAUSE]

If you're a parent in this room reading those ring labels
and feeling protective, hold that feeling. The parent layer
never sees this content directly. The coaching staff never
sees this content directly. The athlete's privacy is
non-negotiable for us. We'll come back to that.

SECTION 4: SCREEN 4 - JOURNAL (ACTIVE)
Speaker: Arron | ~250 words | ~2.5 minutes
=====================================================

[CLICK TO SCREEN 4: JOURNAL (ACTIVE).]

Journaling. Three life domains. Used during the day in the
moment or as part of the evening review.

[GESTURE TO SCREEN]

School slash Work. Sport. Homelife. All on one scrolling
screen. School slash Work has one locked entry from earlier
this morning. A sport entry is being written right now.
Homelife is empty. Journal entries auto-save and lock after
a five-minute edit window.

[PACE SHIFTS. DELIBERATE.]

Three separate domains, not one. The athlete sees today as
a whole person, not just as a sport athlete in isolation.
The pipeline reads across all three. If sport is going well
and homelife or social life shifts, even slightly, that's
caught and named. If homelife is calm and sport is the
source of real friction, that's caught too. The system
reads small ripples and real friction as coaching signal.
This isn't a system that only works when things are Great
or Terrible. It works in real life.

[PAUSE. EYE CONTACT WITH THE ROOM.]

This is one of many input layers this app is built around
protecting. Athlete input has to be private or the
journaling won't be honest. Parents never see this. Coaches
never see this. This app is not surveillance. If your
instinct is wanting to read a kid's journal, hold it.

Tap Reflect and you land here. Step one of the evening

Five Yes-or-No questions. Three answered, two remaining.

CONTINGENCY NOTES
=================

If running long (over 30 min):
  Trim Section 7 (Home Sunday Recap) to a single beat.
  "Sunday evening. Same hub. The weekly recap card just
  lit up. Sunday is the only day this card appears."
  That's ~25 words / 15 sec. Saves ~45 sec on the floor.

If running short (under 27 min):
  Add ~30-60 seconds to Screen 9 (Inbox Feed) by walking
  the Deep Dive preview card more deliberately. Note the
  Read Full Analysis CTA, mention the long-form reader is
  the only full-screen reader pattern in the entire app
  (deliberate design choice for the depth moment).

If a savvy attendee asks during the walkthrough "what
happens if the audit catches a problem?" or "what does
regeneration look like?":
  Suggested answer for Arron: "Hold that for the breakout
  or for synthesis. Short answer: the audit either auto-
  corrects or rejects and regenerates. The athlete never
  sees the failure. The pipeline log catches it for our QA
  monitoring." Then redirect.

If a parent in the room asks during 10C "but what if I
just want to know how my kid is doing?":
  Suggested answer for Nicole: "That is exactly what the
  Parent Coaching Message gives you. How the week was, what
  stood out, how to support. What it does not give you is
  surveillance of the journal. We made that choice on
  purpose. We will get into the why in your breakout."
  Then redirect.

If asked about pricing during the walkthrough:
  Hold for the pricing and deployment block at 2:15-2:45.
  "Pricing comes up after the breakouts. We'll put it all
  on the table then."

If the smart-board fails or a screen does not load:
  Pivot to the printed packet immediately. "Open your
  packet to the Coach Insight" or equivalent. The packet
  is the redundancy layer. Keep talking, do not call
  attention to the tech failure.
