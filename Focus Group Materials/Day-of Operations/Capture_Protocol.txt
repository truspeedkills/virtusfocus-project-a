PURPOSE
=======
Defines the day-of capture, recording, and post-session
synthesis plan for the focus group. The point is to make
sure the value extracted from this room (which we cannot
get anywhere else) is preserved cleanly and synthesized
into actionable findings within seven days.

There are three capture surfaces: written worksheets,
audio recording, and live note-taking. Each captures a
different layer. All three feed into a single Synthesis
Report.


THREE CAPTURE SURFACES
======================

1. WRITTEN WORKSHEETS (athlete-filled)
   - One per attendee per segment
   - 7 pages: cover + 5 segment-specific questions + 1
     free-form page
   - Filled in real-time during the 1:30-2:15 breakout block
   - NAME field on cover is OPTIONAL (anonymous responses
     allowed; segment is what matters)
   - Collected by note-taker at the END of breakout, before
     the room reconvenes for pricing/deployment block
   - Storage: physical originals in labeled segment folder,
     scanned to PDF same evening for synthesis

2. AUDIO RECORDING (verbal discussion)
   - One device per breakout room
   - Recommended: dedicated voice recorder (better fidelity
     than phone) or two phones running simultaneously as
     backup
   - Recording confirmed VERBALLY at start of session
     (covered by NDA; legally required to confirm regardless)
   - Welcome script Section 4 includes the recording
     announcement (see Welcome_and_NDA_Flow.txt)
   - Files transferred to secure storage same evening,
     transcribed within 48 hours via Otter or comparable
     transcription service

3. NOTE-TAKER OBSERVATIONS (sentiment + themes)
   - One note-taker per breakout
   - Captures what the audio cannot: body language,
     engagement level, who deferred to whom, when the
     room agreed without saying so, when somebody pushed
     back politely on a question that should be reopened
   - Uses the shorthand convention below
   - Notes feed into synthesis as the interpretive layer
     on top of the audio transcript

NOTE-TAKER SHORTHAND CONVENTION
================================
Note-takers use a single notebook or laptop with this
template per page:

  ----------------------------------------------------------
  SEGMENT:        [Institutional | D2C | GTM]
  BREAKOUT TIME:  [start - end, e.g., 1:30-2:15]
  NOTE-TAKER:     [Name]
  ATTENDEES:      [First names, e.g., Rick, Bret, Bob]
  ----------------------------------------------------------

  Q1: [Question text or short ref, e.g., "Compliance balk?"]
  ----------------------------------------------------------
  [TIMESTAMP] [INITIALS] [SENTIMENT-CODE] [verbatim or paraphrase]
  ----------------------------------------------------------

SENTIMENT CODES (single letter):
  P  = Positive / agreement / engaged
  N  = Negative / disagreement / pushback
  M  = Mixed / middle / "yes but"
  Q  = Question raised by attendee (worth following up)
  *  = Cross-lens insight (attendee bringing secondary
       perspective into a primary-segment question)

VERBATIM CAPTURE RULES:
  - Use double quotes for exact words: "We would never
    deploy this without legal review."
  - Use parens for paraphrase: (paraphrased: legal review
    is the gate)
  - Mark unclear or partially-heard with [unclear]:
    "We would [unclear] without legal."
  - Always include attendee initials so synthesis can
    attribute correctly

EXAMPLES:

  1:42  RK  N  "Position coaches won't read it. They'll
              skim the color and move on."

  1:45  BR  *  Brings in his Saint Vincent rebuilding
              experience. Says: (paraphrased: institutional
              adoption depends on whether the dashboard
              shows what the head coach asked them to
              measure, not what we think matters)

  1:47  BC  P  "The clinical line in the language is
              exactly right."

  1:52  RK  Q  Asks whether parent layer is on or off by
              default. Worth following up in synthesis.

   left it blank.
4. Worksheets stay with the note-taker until end of day.
5. Same evening: photograph or scan all worksheets into
   secure shared storage. Originals to a sealed envelope
   (one per segment) and into Arron + Nicole custody.

CONTINGENCY: ATTENDEE OBJECTS TO RECORDING
============================================
If an attendee objects to recording in the welcome block
(unlikely given NDA, but possible):
  - Acknowledge the concern: "Understood, that's fair."
  - Offer two options:
    a) Their breakout uses note-taker only (no audio)
    b) Their specific responses are flagged and excluded
       from any clip-sharing or transcript distribution
  - Note-taker for that breakout switches to detailed
    verbatim mode to compensate for missing audio
  - Document the objection in synthesis report ops notes


CONTINGENCY: NOTE-TAKER UNAVAILABLE
====================================
If a designated note-taker can't make it:
  - Facilitator note-takes during slow moments
  - Backup recorder runs continuously to catch what
    facilitator misses
  - Synthesis weighs that breakout's findings slightly
    lower in cross-segment confidence scoring


CONTINGENCY: TWO ROOMS NOT ENOUGH
==================================
The Hampton Inn room is reserved all day. If breakouts
need physical separation (audio bleed risk):
  - Confirm with venue ahead of time about partition or
    secondary space access
  - Worst case: stagger breakouts (one at a time) but
    this triples the time, not viable
  - Best case: Hampton has flexible event space, all
    three breakouts in same room with chair-grouping
    separation. Audio bleed is acceptable for this
    purpose.
