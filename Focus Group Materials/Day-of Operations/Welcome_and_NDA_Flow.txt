SCRIPT (Arron, ~700 words / ~10 min spoken)
====================================================

[ARRON STEPS UP. WAIT FOR ROOM TO SETTLE.]

1. OPENING GREETING + HOSTS INTRO

Hi everyone. Thanks for being here on a Saturday
afternoon. Some of you traveled to get here. All of
you signed an NDA on the way in. We do not take that
lightly.

I'm Arron. For those who do not know me, I have spent
the last decade plus running a strength and conditioning
business and coaching mental performance. This is the
project I've been quietly working on for over a year
that pulls those two threads together.

[GESTURE TO NICOLE]

This is Nicole. My good friend and business partner in this and other ventures. We are
co-founding "VirtusFocus" together along with our development partner Scott Dix. Nicole is going to
take over the front of the room at 12:15 to walk you
through the pitch portion of today, and you'll hear from
her again at the end.

2. NDA + RECORDING CONFIRMATION

[PACE SHIFTS - DELIBERATE FOR THIS BLOCK]

Quick thing I need to land before we start. You all
signed an NDA at the door. That covers everything you
see, hear, and read in this room over the next three
hours. The product, the system, the mockups, the athlete
materials, the pricing direction, the strategic
questions we are asking you. It all stays in this room
until we say otherwise.

We will also be recording the breakouts after lunch.
Audio only, internal use only, covered by the NDA you
signed. We will never share identified clips externally
without explicit permission. The recording is so we can
synthesize what you tell us properly. By participating
in the breakouts, you're consenting to the recording.

[PAUSE, SCAN ROOM]

If anybody has a concern about that, please flag it now
or pull either of us aside before the breakouts start
at 1:30. We can accommodate.

3. WHY THIS ROOM

[PACE LIFTS, WARMER TONE]

The reason you specifically are here. We had to be very
deliberate about who we put in this room. Every one of
you was selected because you bring a perspective we
need and could not buy or replicate.

We've put you into three rough groups for the breakouts
later. Coaches and program-builders on one side. Parents,
athletes, and youth-program operators on another. Market, 
strategy, and content people on the third. The packets in front
of you indicate which group you're in.

But several of you bring more than one perspective into
your seat, and we are going to ask about both. We will
get specific about that in the breakouts.

For now, just know that we did not invite anybody who
does not have something specific to teach us today.

4. TODAY'S GOAL: EXTRACT, NOT VALIDATE

This is the part where I want to be clear and direct.

This is a focus group. It is not a pitch. We are not
trying to convince you to invest or purchase anything. 
We are about to put a real product, a real system, and 
the real shape of our business in front of you, and we
are going to ask you to find the cracks.

If you walk out of here later today telling us
everything was great and we should ship it tomorrow,
we have failed. The most useful thing you can do for us
in the next three hours is push back, tell us what is
wrong, name what would not work in your world, and
explain why.

Don't be polite. Be specific. Nicole will repeat that
in different words at the end of her talk, and we will
say it again before the breakouts start. We mean it.

5. HOW TODAY FLOWS

Here is how the next three hours go.

Right now we'll go over some logistics. Then
Nicole and I are going to do a thirty-minute foundations guided tour
together. We split it. She opens, I take the middle, she
closes. You'll see how it works.

After the tour, I'll walk you through the app mockups
for thirty minutes so you can see what an athlete and a
parent and a coach would actually see. The mockups are
in active design, not final, but they are far enough
along that they're useful for what we're going to ask
you in the breakouts.

Then we eat. Lunch will be right here in the room.

After lunch, you'll split into your three groups for
forty-five minutes of breakout work. Each group has a
worksheet packet. Each group has a facilitator and a
note-taker.

Then we come back together for a thirty-minute
conversation about pricing and deployment. That one we
do as a single room. You can press hard there.

Last fifteen minutes is cross-room synthesis. You hear
what came out of the other groups, we react in real
time, and we close.

Three hours, no breaks longer than the lunch transition.
If you need to step out at any point, just step out.
Don't ask permission.

6. QUICK LOGISTICS

Bathrooms are just outside the door to the right.
Coffee, water, and light snacks are on the side table.
Help yourself anytime. WiFi password is on the cards in
front of you if you need it. We're asking you to put
phones away during the pitch and the mockups, but use
them as you need to during the rest of the day.

7. HAND-OFF TO NICOLE

Last thing before I hand it over. Some of you know each
other already, some of you don't. Don't worry about
introducing yourselves to the whole room right now. Use
lunch for that. Sit with people in your breakout group
when we eat so you can get to know them before you
have to argue with them productively at 1:30.

[BEAT]

Ok. Nicole is going to take it from here.

[ARRON STEPS BACK. NICOLE STEPS UP.
PITCH BEGINS AT 12:15.]

CHECK-IN LEAD ROLE
==================
The check-in lead is the first face attendees see. They
own the door from 11:30 until 12:00 sharp. Their job is
to:

1. Greet each attendee warmly by name as they arrive
2. Confirm NDA signature (QR primary, printed fallback)
3. Mark them on the sign-in roster
4. Hand them a name tag (color-coded by segment)
5. Direct them to coffee, snacks, and seating
6. Flag any unsure attendee or unexpected guest to
   Arron or Nicole

The check-in lead does NOT need to be Arron or Nicole.
In fact, it should not be. Arron is preparing for the
welcome block. Nicole is preparing for the pitch.

OPEN QUESTIONS TO RESOLVE PRE-EVENT:
  - Who is the check-in lead? Same person as a
    note-taker, or different?
  - Backup if check-in lead is delayed?

REQUIREMENTS FOR THE CHECK-IN LEAD:
  - Trusted (handles NDA-signed material)
  - Has signed an NDA themselves
  - Knows the attendee list and faces (or has a printed
    sheet with photos)
  - Can stay calm under last-minute logistical churn
  - Available 11:15-12:15 (arrive early, stay for
    welcome block)


NDA SIGNING - THREE PATHS
==========================

PATH A: QR CODE (PRIMARY, ~80% of attendees)
--------------------------------------------
- QR code printed and posted at the entry table
- QR code already configured in Slack canvas (per pickup)
- Attendee scans, signs digitally on phone, takes ~60
  seconds
- Confirmation goes to Arron / Nicole shared email
- Check-in lead verifies confirmation came through
  before letting attendee in

PATH B: PRINTED COPY (FALLBACK, ~20% of attendees)
--------------------------------------------------
- Stack of printed NDA copies at entry table
  (recommended: 6-8 copies, covers ~50% of attendees
  even if QR fails entirely)
- Attendee signs in pen, hands signed copy to check-in
  lead
- Check-in lead initials and dates the back of each
  signed copy
- Signed copies stored in a sealed folder in
  Arron + Nicole custody at end of event

PATH C: PRE-SIGNED (SOME ATTENDEES, OPTIONAL)
---------------------------------------------
- For attendees who signed NDAs in advance through
  Slack: confirm in the system, mark them on the roster,
  proceed to name tag step
- This is the fastest path if implemented for confirmed
  attendees ahead of time

CONTINGENCIES
=============

UNEXPECTED ATTENDEE (someone who didn't RSVP shows up):
  - Check-in lead pulls Arron or Nicole quietly
  - Decision case-by-case
  - If admitted: NDA signed on the spot (printed copy),
    name tag created if possible, segment assigned
    based on best fit
  - Likely candidates: Curt Smith finally confirms,
    spouse of confirmed attendee, etc.

UNSURE ATTENDEE NO-SHOWS:
  - Mark roster as "no-show"
  - Their packet stays in the segment pile (someone may
    take an extra for review post-event)
  - Synthesis report notes who attended vs RSVP'd

LATE ARRIVAL (attendee shows after 12:00):
  - Check-in lead processes them quietly during the
    welcome block
  - They slip into the back, get oriented during the
    natural transition between welcome and pitch
  - Welcome content can be summarized for them at lunch

TECH FAILURE (QR doesn't work):
  - Pivot 100% to printed NDA copies
  - Have at least 6-8 printed copies on hand for this
  - Keep a paper backup of the sign-in roster regardless
