"VIRTUSFOCUS" FOCUS GROUP: PRICING + DEPLOYMENT BLOCK SCRIPT
=============================================================

Event:        May 9, 2026 (Saturday)

Venue:        Hampton Inn, 1000 Towne Square Dr, Greensburg

Slot:         2:15-2:30 PM (15 min, full-room)

Lead:         Arron Panigall

Support:      Nicole Bohince (chimes in on D2C topic),
              Michele Cribbs (chimes in on white-label topic)

Last updated: 2026-05-04


====================================================
PURPOSE
====================================================

The first focused full-room read on three signal-dense topics
that the breakouts already touched but cannot resolve in
isolation. The room hears each segment's read in real time
and reacts.

Three topics on the table:

  1) D2C subscription pricing. Where is the ceiling. What
     would make somebody cancel.
  2) Institutional contract structure. What does a contract
     have to look like for an AD or a principal to say yes.
  3) White-label versus single-app architecture. Same app
     for every institution, or skinned versions per program.

The block is hybrid in structure. Arron names all three
questions up front. Attendees pick which one to push back
on first. Roughly five minutes per topic. Order is
attendee-driven.


====================================================
STRUCTURE
====================================================

  1)  Reconvene and framing                ~2 min
      (Arron names the three questions
      and invites the room to pick first)

  2)  Topic block 1 (attendee-chosen)      ~5 min

  3)  Transition                           ~30 sec

  4)  Topic block 2 (attendee-chosen)      ~5 min

  5)  Transition                           ~30 sec

  6)  Topic block 3 (by elimination)       ~2 min

  7)  Hand-off to cross-room synthesis     ~30 sec


====================================================
SCRIPT
====================================================


1. RECONVENE AND FRAMING (Arron, ~2 min spoken)

[ROOM RECONVENES FROM BREAKOUTS. NOTE-TAKERS WALK WORKSHEETS
TO THE FRONT AND HAND THEM TO ARRON. ATTENDEES SETTLE.]

[ARRON STEPS UP. WAIT FOR ROOM TO QUIET.]

Welcome back. Worksheets are in. Thank you, note-takers.

[BEAT]

The next fifteen minutes is the only block today where we
hear from the full room together before the close. Three
things on the table.

[GESTURE TO ROOM. PACE DELIBERATE]

One. Pricing on the D2C side. The number we floated in the
pitch was around twenty dollars a month. Where is the
ceiling. Where does it stop being worth it for the athlete
or the parent paying out of pocket.

Two. Institutional contract structure. If we walk into a
school, a college, or an athletic department, what does the
contract have to look like. Annual or multi-year. Per-athlete
or per-team or per-program. What does the budget conversation
actually require for an AD or a principal to sign.

Three. White-label versus single-app architecture. One option
is the same VirtusFocus app for everyone, every institution.
The other option is institutions get a skinned version with
their own brand, their own colors, their own logo. Ohio State
gets "Ohio State Mental Performance." Pitt gets "Pitt Mental
Performance." Same engine underneath. Different face. The GTM
breakout heard this question already. The rest of you are
hearing it for the first time.

[BEAT]

Three questions. About five minutes each. Order is yours.

[OPEN HANDS, INVITE ROOM]

Which one do you want to push back on first?

[PAUSE. ATTENDEE PICKS A TOPIC. ARRON PROCEEDS TO WHICHEVER
TOPIC IS NAMED FIRST. THE THREE TOPIC BLOCKS BELOW ARE
MODULAR. ANY ORDER. ANY DURATION WITHIN THE 5-MIN BUDGET.]


2. TOPIC BLOCK: D2C PRICING (~5 min)

[NICOLE CAN CHIME IN HERE. HER D2C BREAKOUT JUST CLOSED ON
THIS QUESTION. SHE HAS THE FRESHEST DATA.]

ARRON ANCHOR PROMPT:

  "D2C breakout just closed on this question. Nicole,
  give us the headline from your room. Then we open it
  to everyone."

[NICOLE: 30-45 SEC HEADLINE FROM D2C BREAKOUT. SPECIFIC
DOLLAR CEILINGS WRITTEN ON Q5. SPECIFIC CANCEL TRIGGERS.
THEN ARRON OPENS TO ROOM.]

LISTENING FOR:

  1)  Specific dollar ceilings (athletes vs parents)
  2)  Cancel triggers (Q5b answers)
  3)  Cross-segment reaction. Coaches and GTM advisors
      pushing in on whether the price feels right
  4)  Difference between athlete-direct purchase and
      parent-paying-for-kid economics

IF DISCUSSION STALLS, ARRON PROMPTS:

  "What's the price where you say yes without thinking
  about it."

  "What's the price where you walk away."

  "If we charged five dollars less, would you commit
  longer."

WATCH-OUT:

  Do not let the room converge on a single number too
  fast. Each attendee's number is signal. Convergence is
  signal, divergence is more useful.


3. TOPIC BLOCK: INSTITUTIONAL CONTRACT STRUCTURE (~5 min)

ARRON ANCHOR PROMPT:

  "Institutional coaches in the room. Rick, Bret, Bob,
  Gio, Buck, Chris. If you took this to your AD or your
  principal tomorrow, what is the contract structure that
  makes them say yes. Annual. Multi-year. Per-athlete.
  Per-team. Site license. What do they need to see."

[LOOK AT EACH NAMED COACH AS YOU NAME THEM.]

LISTENING FOR:

  1)  Pricing units (per-athlete, per-team, per-program,
      flat fee, site license)
  2)  Term length expectations (annual vs multi-year)
  3)  Budget cycles (when does AD budget close)
  4)  HS-context vs college-context divergence
      (HS principal + AD + state association vs college
      AD + compliance + procurement)
  5)  Bob and Rick on AD-budget mechanics specifically.
      Bob built a program from zero, Rick ran one for
      forty years. Their numbers are not abstract.

IF GTM ADVISORS PUSH IN, ARRON MAKES SPACE:

  "GTM, what does the contract have to look like for the
  institutional sales motion to actually work. Jon, you've
  sold B2B. What's the term and structure that closes."

WATCH-OUT:

  Do not let coaches default to "whatever the AD wants."
  Push for the structure they think actually fits a
  coaching tool, not the structure they assume the AD
  will demand.


4. TOPIC BLOCK: WHITE-LABEL VS SINGLE-APP (~5 min)

[MICHELE CAN CHIME IN HERE. HER GTM BREAKOUT WORKED THIS
QUESTION FOR ABOUT TEN MINUTES.]

ARRON ANCHOR PROMPT:

  "GTM heard this in the breakout already. Michele, give
  us the GTM headline. Then I want coaches and D2C to
  weigh in, because both groups have a stake in the answer
  that GTM does not."

[MICHELE: 30-45 SEC HEADLINE FROM GTM BREAKOUT. WHAT THE
ROOM ARGUED FOR. WHERE THEY DIVERGED. THEN ARRON OPENS TO
ROOM.]

WHY COACHES MATTER ON THIS QUESTION:

  Whether "Ohio State Mental Performance" carries more
  institutional credibility than "VirtusFocus at Ohio
  State" for the AD pitch and for athlete adoption inside
  the program.

WHY D2C MATTERS ON THIS QUESTION:

  Whether brand recognition matters at all for athletes
  or parents paying directly. If parents shop on the
  consumer side and see VirtusFocus, that is the brand.
  If institutions can skin it, the consumer brand is
  effectively invisible inside every institution that
  uses it.

IF DISCUSSION STALLS, ARRON PROMPTS:

  "What does VirtusFocus lose if every institution gets
  to skin it."

  "What does VirtusFocus lose if no institution can skin
  it."

  "If you were Ohio State, would you take the brand or
  the skin."

WATCH-OUT:

  This is the highest-stakes question on the table. The
  answer informs the May 16 synthesis report and the
  product roadmap. Slow the room down. Capture divergence
  verbatim, not consensus.


5. TRANSITION TO CROSS-ROOM SYNTHESIS (Arron, ~30 sec)

[ARRON CHECKS THE CLOCK. AT 2:28, START THE TRANSITION
REGARDLESS OF WHERE THE THIRD TOPIC IS. HARD CUT AT 2:30.]

That's the table.

[BEAT]

Last block of the day. Each breakout shares what came out of
the room. We open it to the full group. Then we close on the
question that's on the back page of every worksheet.

[GESTURE TO NICOLE AND MICHELE]

Nicole and Michele are going to report alongside me.

[STAY UP. NICOLE AND MICHELE COME FORWARD FOR CROSS-ROOM
SYNTHESIS BLOCK AT 2:30.]


====================================================
CONTINGENCIES
====================================================

IF ONLY TWO OF THE THREE TOPICS GET COVERED:

  The third folds into the cross-room synthesis. Arron
  pulls the question into the open Q&A or into the "what
  we missed" block. Note-takers tag worksheets for the
  uncovered topic so synthesis can lean on the written
  answers.

IF ONE TOPIC DOMINATES THE FULL FIFTEEN MINUTES:

  Acceptable. The signal is in the depth, not the
  coverage. The cross-room synthesis can pull the
  under-covered topic forward.

IF AN ATTENDEE NAMES A COMPETITOR OR PROGRAM BY NAME:

  Note-takers capture verbatim. NDA covers it. Do not
  redact, do not echo, do not endorse.

IF A CLINICAL-LANGUAGE SLIP HAPPENS IN DISCUSSION:

  Standard rule. Do not echo. Re-frame the substance in
  performance language and move on.

IF RUNNING SHORT (BLOCK CLOSES BEFORE 2:30):

  Do not extend. Hand to cross-room synthesis early. The
  cross-room block can absorb extra time without damage.

IF RUNNING LONG (STILL IN PRICING+DEPLOYMENT AT 2:30):

  Hard cut. Transition to cross-room synthesis. Topics
  mid-discussion get picked up in the cross-room block
  or in the synthesis report.

IF AN ATTENDEE PRESSES FOR A SPECIFIC PRICE OR CONTRACT
NUMBER FROM US:

  Suggested response: "We're not anchoring you to a
  number today. The number we put in the pitch was a
  starting point so you had something to react to. The
  ceiling is what we want from this room."

IF AN ATTENDEE ASKS WHAT WE WILL DO WITH THEIR ANSWERS:

  Suggested response: "Synthesis report goes to internal
  team and advisors within seven days. Decisions land
  shortly after. Your input lands somewhere concrete."
