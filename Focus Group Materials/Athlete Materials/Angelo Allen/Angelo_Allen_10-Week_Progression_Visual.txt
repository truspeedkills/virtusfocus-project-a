KEY INFLECTION POINTS
─────────────────────

Wk 1      Illness recovery + approach-framed re-engagement.
          Returns to competition. Category-only WTD scoring.

Wk 2      Near-PR Shotput (16.44m vs 16.70m career best) at
          highest-level meet of season. Solo at the meet —
          composure held in isolation.

Wk 3      First Gold Streak. Won both events.
          Identity-level achievement.

Wk 4      PSAC championship retrospective after 2.5-week gap.
          MODERATE EI. Partially Aligned. Multi-domain
          overload derailer activated under championship
          pressure. Outdoor transition begins.

Wk 5      SHIPPENSBURG BREAKTHROUGH — best outdoor opener of
          career. All PSAC auto-qualifiers hit (Discus 2nd,
          Shot 3rd, Hammer 4th). Process-focused goal
          reframing applied. 7/7 Win — second Gold Streak.
          PSAC lesson named in athlete's own words:
            "properly planned, not spontaneous."
          The preparation-quality system proves itself for
          the first time.

Wk 6      First Missed day in 42 program days (Friday illness).
          CMU meet cancelled — athlete supposed to compete
          but got sick. Only 1 practice in 7 days. Moderate
          recommitment (lifted Saturday while sick).
          Reframed the disruption:
            "a blessing"
          (because practice preparation was already
          compromised — PSAC lesson operating at real-time
          decision level).

Wk 7      Easter break recovery week executed to stated
          intention. 7/7 Win — third Gold Streak. Bullseye
          center-ring expanded 1 → 6 items, mapping to
          baseline identity language verbatim. Achievement
          internal: "mental stability and equilibrium."

Wk 8      FIRST MIXED WEEK in program. Mt Union 2-day meet
          under severe weather (rain, cold, wind despite
          sunny forecast). Marks below standard across all
          three events. Practice facility permanently lost.
          Strongest negative language to date — but weekday
          execution held, athlete competed through, brought
          extra clothes the next day despite not needing
          them.

Wk 9      SLIPPERY ROCK PROGRAM-BEST. Outdoor shot put
          school record (over 16m) — exactly the goal the
          athlete had stated. New Discus PR. Hammer
          improvement over Mt Union. 7/7 Win — fourth Gold
          Streak. Preparation-quality system VALIDATED A
          SECOND TIME, this time under MORE constrained
          conditions (continued practice instability +
          no-lift week + post-Wk-8 setback). Athlete's own
          summary:
            "best meet of the season."

Wk 10     ↑ PHASE ADVANCEMENT to Consistent — final week of
          collegiate career. Two real-time strategic
          decisions named in athlete's own voice:
            "I didn't need to be there but I chose to to
             support my team and to keep my competition
             readiness up."
          (Saturday last-chance meet attendance choice. Won
          shot put and discus. Mid-competition decision to
          scratch hammer for injury prevention — both
          decisions process-anchored.)
          Career closure framing introduced for first time:
            "leave colligate sports with gratitude, reverence,
             and peace."


INTAKE → CURRENT
────────────────
   FROM: multi-domain overload derailer (consistency disruption
         when school + work + sport demands compete)
   TO:   preparation-system mastery operating across THREE
         timescales —
            • retrospective lesson extraction (Wk 4)
            • prospective preparation design (Wks 5, 9)
            • in-competition real-time decision-making (Wk 10)

   The same athlete who entered the program with championship
   disappointment and overload-pattern friction now closes his
   collegiate career having demonstrated his preparation
   principle at three distinct timescales — and named the
   close in process language before the final meet was thrown.
