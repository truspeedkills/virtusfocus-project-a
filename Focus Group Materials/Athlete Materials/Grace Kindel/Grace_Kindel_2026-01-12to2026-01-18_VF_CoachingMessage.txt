Grace,

Thursday is what I want to start with. The front toss session went below your standard, and you didn't let it follow you out of the cage. You kept working. You didn't let one rough rep change the rest of your week. The first week of a new program is the hardest place to do that, and you did it without anyone telling you to.

That same instinct showed up everywhere else. Your center ring named the right things. Mindset, daily reset, body health. Not the lineup, not what coaches think, not other people's reps. The work that's actually yours.

You closed the week with this: "Not only did I prove it to others but to myself." That's the line, Grace. The confidence you're describing started with you, not with the result. That's the kind of confidence that holds when the week stops giving back, and that's the kind we're going to build on.

You came into this program wanting to stay confident in your ability during competition. That confidence isn't built in the games. It's built in moments like Thursday, when nobody's watching and a rep didn't go your way and you reset anyway. This week gave you the first real one of those, and you ran it the way the program is designed to make automatic.

Heading into Week 2, second hell week, the test is whether the same reset shows up under fatigue. Right now it's instinct. The next step is making it a structure you can name and reach for.

YOUR ACTION: AFTER ANY ERROR THIS WEEK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Three breaths, one silent statement: "my effort, my reset." Then the next play. Every error or below-standard rep, every practice this week.

End of each practice, one question: did I use the cue every time? That's the measurement. Not how many mistakes. Whether the reset showed up after each one.

What's yours stays in the center ring.
Coach Arron
