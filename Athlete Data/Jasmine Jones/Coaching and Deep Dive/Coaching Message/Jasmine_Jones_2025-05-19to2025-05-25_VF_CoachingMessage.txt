Jasmine —

You wrote that block target down and carried it. Gold Streak — seven for seven. That does not happen by accident.

What made this week work was not just showing up. It was the intention behind how you showed up. You named it yourself: "Setting the tone on Monday, allowed me to carry that motivation into the rest of the week with the same Intention." That is deliberate. You decided what Monday was going to look like, and then you held that standard through Sunday. Below-parallel squats for the first time post-injection. Sled pushing back in the mix. Core circuits, yoga, chiro, rehab — every day had a purpose. And Jade's recital Thursday night was part of a full day, not a break from it.

The biggest moment this week was not a training session. It was how you handled the NBC shoot. You were excluded from the feature. That is the kind of recognition gap that goes straight at your comparison pattern — being left out based on your role, not your effort. And your response was not to withdraw. You said, "Now I'm working on something that's going to make me stand out and be more than just the brakeman behind the pilot." That is an approach-framed response to the exact situation that has pulled you off track before. You turned an exclusion into a standard.

The comparison pattern is still in your Bullseye outer ring, and it should be. It is not gone. But this week you showed it does not have to run the show.

Heading into week two of the block, the initial excitement fades. That is normal.
