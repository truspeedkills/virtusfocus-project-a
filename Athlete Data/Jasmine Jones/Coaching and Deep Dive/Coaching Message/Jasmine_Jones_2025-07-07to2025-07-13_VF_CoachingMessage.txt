Jasmine,

"I achieved to actually cry my emotions out and to not bottle them up." Those are your words. And they tell me more about where you are right now than any training metric could.

This was a full week, Jasmine. You drove back to Lake Placid on Monday and went straight into recovery mode. Tuesday you were in sports med for EHAI testing, and the results confirmed what your training has been building: balanced left-right strength with just a few corrective areas to refine. Wednesday, on your birthday, you were pushing in the icehouse and checking in with your strength coach, who confirmed you held accountability during vacation. Friday was your best session of the week: productive icehouse work with two teammates where you coached each other and reviewed video, then a strong weight room finish. Five Win days, zero Missed, and your seventh consecutive week of showing up and doing the work.

But here's what stood out most. The weekend brought some heavy personal weight, and you handled it with genuine composure. You didn't avoid what you were feeling. You found someone you trusted, let the emotions move, communicated directly, and came back to your day. All within 24 hours. That kind of emotional honesty takes real strength, and the fact that you named it as your achievement this week tells me your definition of strength is expanding beyond the icehouse.

There's something worth noticing in how you framed your controllables this week. For the first time in the program, your center ring wasn't about training at all. It was about showing up as your true self in relationships and letting the other person decide what to do with that. The same internal standard you've been building for seven weeks, applied to a completely different part of your life. You didn't force that transfer. It happened because the work is becoming part of who you are.

With the next training block and performance camp approaching at the end of the month, the opportunity now is to take all that energy you're carrying and aim it somewhere specific on the track.
