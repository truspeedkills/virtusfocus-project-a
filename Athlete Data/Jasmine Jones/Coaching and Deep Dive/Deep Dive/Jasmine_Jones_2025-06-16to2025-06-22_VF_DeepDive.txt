STRENGTH SIGNALS
------------------------------------------------------------

But adding the coach-athlete relationship as a lever is a real development. In the icehouse, you focused on one technical element at a time because that is what your coaches assigned — and that coaching structure became your anchor against the pull to chase what others were doing.
*When you treat coaching instruction as a tool you choose to use, it becomes available in any high-pressure environment — not just camp.*

The 1080 sprint testing produced a result that belongs entirely to you: same speed at a heavier body weight. That is your preparation, your programming, and your process showing up in measurable form. Combined with last week's block-end testing improvements, you now have a growing body of evidence that your approach works — all measured against your own prior data.
*Objective self-referenced evidence is the most durable form of confidence. It does not shift when someone else has a good day.*

You recognized that engaging would pull your focus, and you shut it down before it could.
*The same internal standard that keeps you in your lane during training also works outside the gym. You applied it to both arenas this week without anyone telling you to.*

The development is in how much mental space it occupies versus how much your controllables occupy.

You are acting on your own program, your own coaching, your own process.

That pride you named this week — it is earned. It came from doing the harder thing: staying in your lane when it would have been easier to measure yourself against everyone around you. That is the mindset you said you wanted to build. You are building it.
