MINDSET SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------

This week tested something the program hasn't seen before. Not your composure in the icehouse, not your ability to hold your own lane at camp, but your capacity to manage real emotional weight in a space that has nothing to do with bobsled. And you handled it.

Tuesday you were in sports med for EHAI testing, which confirmed balanced strength with a few corrective exercises to address. Wednesday, your birthday, you were pushing in the icehouse and checking in with your strength coach. Friday was the standout: productive session with two teammates where you coached each other and did video work, followed by a strong weight room finish. Five Win days, zero Missed, seven straight weeks of consistent execution.

But the weekend brought personal difficulty that carried real emotional weight. What matters about how you handled it isn't just that you recovered. It's how you recovered: you felt what you were feeling, found someone you trusted to process with, let the emotions move through you, communicated directly with the person involved, and moved on with your day. All within 24 hours. You named it yourself as your achievement this week: letting yourself feel it and not bottling it up. Your definition of what counts as a win is getting broader and more honest, and that's a sign of genuine growth.

GROWTH AREAS
------------------------------------------------------------

CHANNELING ENERGY TOWARD A SPECIFIC TARGET
You've said it yourself: when things get hard, you level up by pouring that energy into your sport. That pattern has produced some of your best training intensity. This week generated a lot of emotional energy, and heading into the next block with performance camp at the end of the month, the opportunity is to point that energy at something precise. When intensity finds a specific technical target in the icehouse or a specific lift in the weight room, it builds something lasting. When it stays diffuse, it burns hot but doesn't compound.

*Energy is energy. The difference between intensity and progress is aim.*

RECONNECTING CONTROLLABLES TO TRAINING SPECIFICS
Your Bullseye has been one of your strongest tools all program. This week it honestly reflected where your head was, and there's nothing wrong with that. But heading into a new training block, reconnecting your center ring to something specific in pushing or in the weight room will give the next several weeks a clear direction. You've proven you can apply the internal-standard framework anywhere. Now the question is where you choose to point it when the next block starts.
