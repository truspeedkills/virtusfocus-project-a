GROWTH AREAS
------------------------------------------------------------

EMOTIONAL CHANNELING AND DIRECTION
You have said it yourself: each time you have been hurt, you have leveled up by pouring that energy into your sport. That pattern has produced real results — some of your best training intensity has come from emotional fuel. This week, a personal situation generated a lot of energy. The question going forward is whether that energy finds a specific target or stays diffuse. When it is aimed at a technical element in the icehouse or a specific lift in the weight room, it produces your best sessions. When it is general intensity without a focal point, it burns hot but does not build anything lasting.

*Energy is energy — the difference between intensity and progress is where you point it.*

BULLSEYE SHIFT AWAY FROM TRAINING SPECIFICS
This is the first week in the program where your controllables did not name a single training element. Previous weeks featured sled pushing visualization, coaching receptivity, channeling energy into exercises, and training effort. This week, your entire Bullseye focused on relationships and emotional boundaries. That is honest about where your head was — and there is nothing wrong with that. But heading into the next training block and the next performance camp at the end of the month, reconnecting your center ring to something specific in the icehouse or the weight room will help anchor the energy you are carrying into technical progress.

*Your controllables have been one of your strongest tools all program — pointing them back at something specific in your training will give the next block a clear direction.*

WEEKEND VARIABILITY
Your Monday-through-Friday execution has been rock solid all seven weeks — that consistency is not in question. The weekend tends to be where life outside of sport shows up more intensely: single parenting on Sundays, social dynamics, personal situations like this one. This week, Saturday and Sunday were both days where things were harder. You still recovered, still processed, still moved forward — but the pattern of weekday strength and weekend variability is worth noticing as you head into a stretch with the next performance camp approaching at the end of the month.

*You have proven you can recover from hard weekends quickly — the next step is noticing what helps that recovery happen fastest so you can reach for it intentionally.*
