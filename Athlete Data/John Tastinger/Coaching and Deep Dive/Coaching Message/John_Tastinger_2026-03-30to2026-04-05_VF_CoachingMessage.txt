John —

Six deals in your first week. You set a target of five, hit six, and helped someone on your team close their first. That right there tells me something about what you're capable of when you're locked in.

Here's what else I noticed. Thursday through Sunday, you were a different operator than Monday through Wednesday. Those last four days had lifts, deals, family time — full days, every piece in place. Those were your Win days. The first three days? Business was still happening — you were still signing deals Tuesday and Wednesday — but you slept late every morning, skipped the gym, and scored yourself Neutral. You knew the difference. That matters.

Now let's talk about what just changed. You quit your job this week. That's a significant move, and you made it decisively. But here's the coaching point: the job was also the last thing giving your day a fixed structure. Without it, there's nobody telling you when to wake up, nobody expecting you anywhere at a set time. The mornings you slept late this week happened with the job still technically in place. Without it, the pull toward late nights and late mornings gets stronger — not weaker.

You said you want to be disciplined, consistent, and decisive. That's the identity you're building. This week showed you can execute at a high level when the routine is active. The question isn't whether you can perform — you proved that with the deals. The question is whether you'll build the structure that makes that performance repeatable, or whether you'll keep riding momentum until it runs out.

Your first week told me a lot. You've got the output. Now build the input system that sustains it.
