Angelo —

Welcome back. Two and a half weeks is a gap, but the way you came back tells me more than the gap itself.

You could have submitted a surface-level recap and moved on. Instead, you gave me the most detailed and honest reflection of your entire program. You named every factor that contributed to the championship experience — the exam, the grad school planning, the broken implements, the solo dynamic, the disrupted lifting, the practice that was "all over the place." You did not make excuses. You identified what happened and why. That level of analysis is exactly the "sharp" quality you described at intake.

And here is what I want you to recognize about the two weeks you missed: you said you do not like to talk while you are emotional, that it takes you a bit to collect your thoughts before making decisions. That is not a character flaw. That is self-regulation. You recognized that your initial response was anger and frustration, and you chose to wait until you could process it clearly. That decision — to pause, to let the emotions settle, to come back when you were ready — is a strength, not a weakness.

Now look at what you did this week. Monday you hit the ground running with outdoor practice. Tuesday was your most enjoyable Tuesday yet. You practiced solo on Saturday and Sunday because no one else was there and you wanted to prepare. You worked with a competitive powerlifter and learned from the experience. Your transition into outdoor season is strong, and it is built on the honest processing you did over the past two weeks.

The championship did not go the way you wanted. That is real. But the way you responded to it — processing deliberately, returning with clarity, applying what you learned — that is the resilience you said you wanted to build.
