Angelo —

Five Win days in a week where almost nothing went according to plan. One team practice in seven days. Schedule changes wiping out Tuesday through Thursday. Illness hitting Friday and taking the CMU meet off the table entirely. And through all of that, Monday through Thursday were four straight Wins, Saturday you lifted despite being sick, and Sunday you were back at it.

This was your first Missed day in 42 program days. That is worth naming — not because the Missed day matters, but because what you did after it does. You did not let Friday's illness turn into a weekend of disengagement. You chose to lift on Saturday when staying home would have been the easier decision. That is a recommitment pattern, and it tells me your engagement baseline holds even when your body does not cooperate.

The part of your recap that stood out most was the reframe. You said the illness might have actually been a blessing because your practice preparation was already compromised going into the meet. That is the championship lesson operating at a new level. After the PSAC championship, you identified what happens when preparation is disrupted and you compete anyway. This week, you recognized that same pattern forming in real time — and instead of being frustrated about missing CMU, you saw the logic in it. That kind of awareness is exactly the sharpness you described wanting at intake.

Here is what needs your attention. Your Bullseye has been at one item per ring for three consecutive weeks now. "Myself and my consistency" is accurate — but it has been your center-ring answer since Week 1. Your strongest competitive weeks were the ones where you named specific controllables: effort, mindfulness, work ethic. Bringing that specificity back would sharpen the tool. Also, your daily entries this week were noticeably shorter than prior weeks. The additional context section showed your usual analytical quality, but the day-by-day reflections were brief. Depth in the daily entries is where the small details surface — and those details are what make the coaching specific to you.

You have Easter break ahead. No competition, no team practice schedule, but time to train. That is either an opportunity or a gap — and you already know which one you want it to be.
