Angelo,

"Greater mental stability and equilibrium." That's what you named as the achievement of a recovery week. Not a throw distance. Not a placement. The quiet internal work that holds everything else together.

And the week backed it up. Seven Win days. Gold Streak for the first time since Week 3. Two self-directed practice sessions on the only days you had access. Mental work on clarity. Rest on Easter Sunday with people you care about. You didn't force the week into something it wasn't supposed to be. You set the purpose as recovery, and then you executed it.

I want to talk about the Bullseye, because something shifted. For three straight weeks, your center ring had one item. This week it opened to six: dedication, tenacity, willpower, time management, study habits. Look at that list and compare it to what you told me at intake about the person you're trying to become. Goal-oriented, resilient, tenacious, sharp. Five of the six items you wrote this week connect directly to that language. When the external pressure eased and you had space to think, the depth came back on its own. The compression wasn't a loss. It was a bandwidth problem, and this week proved it.

Now here's where I want to push you. Mt Union is Friday and Saturday. First competition since Shippensburg, three weeks ago. You're rested, illness is behind you, and your preparation mindset is intact. But the six items in your center ring are trait-level. "My tenacity" is something you carry everywhere. In the cage before a throw, you need something sharper. What does tenacity look like in the ten seconds before your first mark? What does willpower look like when the early throws are off and the day could go sideways? The Bullseye works best when it moves from a quality you believe in to a specific thing you can do in the moment.

You built a preparation standard at Shippensburg that produced your best outdoor opener. Mt Union is where you find out if that standard holds across a three-week gap. The body is ready. The center ring is full. Bring one of those traits into the cage with you and make it specific.
