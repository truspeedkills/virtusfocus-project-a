Your Day 1 performance was near your best. Day 2 dropped noticeably. The difference was not preparation — it was accumulated travel fatigue, event intensity, and isolation compounding across two full days. This is the first time your consistency challenge showed up in a competitive setting rather than training. In training, the pattern is about multi-domain demands pulling energy away from sport. In competition, it looks different — cascading factors eroding the conditions that support your best performance.

*Your preparation held. The useful distinction is that sustained performance across multiple competition days asks something different of you than sustained performance across a training week.*

SOLO COMPETITION DYNAMICS
Being the only thrower at a major meet created real challenges — no one to warm up with, no coaching feedback between rounds, and the social friction of needing equipment from another team. The implement issue directly cost you a Weight Throw attempt. These are structural realities of your competitive situation that will likely recur at future high-level meets.

*The solo dynamic is not going away. Treating it as something you prepare for — the way you prepare your technique or your nutrition — puts it in your center ring where it belongs.*
