Mergim —

You threw up at minute 8 of a 12-minute jog. And you finished it.

That moment tells me everything about what's coming for you. The body said stop. The mind said U20s. And you kept moving. Then you did it again — 2-mile uphill run on Wednesday, another 2-mile run on Friday, zero early stops. First practice in four months. First time lifting lower body. First time in the wrestling room actually wrestling.

Last week I asked you to write down three daily targets. The written plan didn't make it to the page — but the behavioral intent ran. You maintained school, recovery, and started adding wrestling knowledge through watching. The structure was there even if the paper wasn't. This week the commitment shifts to something you already do naturally: when the urge to stop hits during conditioning, use your own words. "U20s — keep moving."

Here's what matters most. You said "if I wanted to win U20s this summer I couldn't just stop." That's not motivation — that's connection. You're linking every hard moment to the reason you're here. That connection is what separates people who push through from people who wish they had. You woke up at 4 AM to cheer on teammates you couldn't compete with. You finished conditioning sessions that made you physically sick. That's not just effort. That's the identity of a national champion showing up before the title does.

You want to believe in yourself and get out of your own head. Week 2 is the first evidence that you can. Not because you felt confident — but because you executed when everything in your body told you not to. That's what execution-based confidence looks like.
