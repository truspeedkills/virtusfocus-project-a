Mergim —

Three things happened this week that matter more than anything in the last ten.

First: you got cleared for competition. Seven months of rehab, drilling, and daily discipline. You earned that clearance with every extra hour, every conditioning session you finished when your body told you to stop.

Second: Friday, your shot worked in live for the first time. "What I'm working on in practice is working." That sentence is proof — not belief, proof — that preparation translates to performance. You felt it happen. That's the moment where execution-based confidence becomes real.

Third: Saturday, the mental block showed up during live. "I even started overthinking when I was going live." And here's what matters about that — you named it. You saw it happening in real time. That self-awareness is growth. You can't beat a pattern you don't recognize.

The extra workout commitment from last week — completed in full. Two sessions with partners and coaches, both with specific technical focus. That's the behavior of someone who doesn't wait to be told what to do.

Sunday you bounced back. Talked to your dad, came to practice with purpose, and had a good day. The derailer is not eliminated — it's being managed. Friday proves you can score in live. Saturday proves the overthinking still activates under pressure. Sunday proves you recover. That's the full cycle.

Lock Haven is approaching. Everything you've built gets tested there. You said you need "1000 mental reps of positive thoughts." Start with one: you scored in live. It works.
