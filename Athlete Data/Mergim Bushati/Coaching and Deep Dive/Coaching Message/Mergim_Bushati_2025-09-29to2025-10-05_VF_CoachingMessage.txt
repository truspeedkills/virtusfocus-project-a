"I didn't let myself off the hook."

That sentence is the difference between someone who trains and someone who builds something. You set specific targets for Tuesday and Thursday — and hit both of them. You ran hills and didn't stop. You passed teammates on conditioning who never had shoulder surgery. You did 5 sets above 200 watts on the skier when your body was screaming at you to quit.

Last week I asked you to set a scoring goal before Saturday practice and a conditioning goal before one weekday practice. The conditioning goal landed — Tuesday's 4x100 sprints with the uphill finish. The scoring goal didn't happen because you were home for the weekend. That's fine — the mechanism works when you deploy it. Carry the scoring piece forward this week.

Here's what stands out. You're four weeks in and you've moved from "even if I can't do anything" to "I didn't let myself off the hook." That's identity transformation happening in real time. The self-talk shifted from accepting limitation to holding a standard. The override mechanism from Week 2 — connecting effort to your goals — is now automatic. You're not thinking about whether to push through anymore. You're just doing it.

Second consecutive Growth week. Zero missed days across four weeks. Your consistency is strengthening. The win rate is climbing. The controllable stability is locking in. You're building exactly the kind of evidence base that makes belief concrete rather than abstract.

The weekend pattern is the one area to watch. Saturday and Sunday are consistently lower engagement. That's not a problem when practice drives the structure. But your goals require discipline that runs seven days, not five.
