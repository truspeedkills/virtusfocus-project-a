Monday you had no motivation to do the extra work. And you did it anyway.

That's the most important moment of the week. Not because it was your hardest day physically — but because you used the same goal self-talk that got you through the 12-minute jog in Week 2 and the uphill runs in Week 3, except this time it wasn't your body that wanted to quit. It was your mind. And the tool still worked. "I kept telling myself my goals." Same mechanism, harder test. That's growth.

The first-attack commitment from last week couldn't be tested — wrestle-offs disrupted the schedule and you weren't competing. That carries forward. When live opportunities return, that commitment is still waiting.

Wrestle-off week dropped the tier to Mixed. Three Win days, four Neutral. The pattern is consistent now: when the team schedule runs, you run with it. When it doesn't, engagement drops. That's not a discipline problem — it's a structure gap. The fix is building your own version of practice for the days when practice doesn't exist.

Here's the good news: eight weeks in, your 4-week win rate is still 75% and you haven't missed a single day in the entire program. The trend dips but it doesn't break. The pattern is Growth-Mixed-Growth-Growth-Mixed. You bounce back every time. Now make the dip shallower by owning the off days.
