Mergim —

"In wrestling there never is a right time — you just have to go."

That's you coaching yourself through your own derailer. In real time. On the mat.

You said you always overthink and wait for the right time to score. Then you deliberately chose to go. You set the execution goal before practice started — "whenever I'm in a position to score, just go" — and you executed it. Scored from positions of comfort. Didn't wait for perfect. That is the most significant behavioral shift in the first three weeks.

The conditioning commitment from last week — completed in full. Zero early stops. Monday mile push-through despite leg and back pain. Wednesday 2-mile uphill without stopping. Friday 2-mile treadmill done. The "U20s — keep moving" pattern is now running consistently. That's not a one-week thing anymore. It's becoming how you operate.

This week is Growth tier for the first time. Five Win days. First real practice since April. Medical clearance to drill with a brace. You went from showing up while injured to scoring at practice in three weeks. That trajectory is real.

You entered this program wanting to become a national champion by learning to believe in himself and get out of his own head. Saturday was the first time you got out of your own head on the mat. You didn't think your way out of the overthinking — you acted your way out. Pre-set the goal, then executed it. That's the mechanism. That's what you carry forward.
