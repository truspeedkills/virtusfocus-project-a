------------------------------------------------------------

That's a different kind of composure than what you brought to Week 7's strikeout or Week 9's coach conversation, and it comes with its own set of questions about what happens next.

*The difference between working because you're frustrated and working because it's who you are is the difference between reaction and identity.*

------------------------------------------------------------
GROWTH AREAS
------------------------------------------------------------

KEEPING THE ACCEPTANCE ACTIVE
The acceptance you've found is functional and emotionally mature. But acceptance has two versions. One sits next to ongoing specific development: "I accept the situation AND I'm getting better at specific things every day." The other settles into comfort: "I accept the situation, so nothing needs to change." This week, the daily goals shifted from behavioral specificity to gratitude framing, the reflection entries got shorter, and one hitting session replaced three. None of those are problems by themselves. The pattern they form is worth paying attention to, because it can drift without you noticing.

RECONNECTING INTENTIONALITY TO THE DAILY STANDARD
Your daily execution is perfect. It has been for ten weeks. But the goals sitting next to that execution have traced a long decline. "Write something I accomplished each day" is a tracking exercise, not a behavioral intention. "Take something each day to be thankful for" is a nice thought, and it is not the same as naming something specific you want to build. Your daily standard deserves a daily purpose that matches its quality. One specific thing you're working on, named before the day starts, is the difference between winning the day out of habit and winning it on purpose.

PRACTICE ENVIRONMENT FRUSTRATION
Monday's frustration was different from the playing time frustration you've been processing. "How can everyone else be chill and do whatever and we still get yelled at even though nothing ever changes." That's about the team culture and coaching dynamics, not about your personal situation. You closed Monday with a Win day, which means it didn't disrupt your execution. But it's worth sorting: is this a new source of friction that needs its own outer-ring placement, or is it the same dissatisfaction wearing a different outfit?

------------------------------------------------------------
IDENTITY THREAD
------------------------------------------------------------

Your baseline is about wanting to stay confident during competition. Ten weeks in, the most striking thing about your program isn't your competition confidence. It's what you're building when competition isn't available to you.

Seventy straight Win days. Self-directed practice that happens because you decided it should. Dugout presence that your teammates feel whether you play or not. A willingness to walk up to a coach and have a hard conversation, and a willingness to sit with confusing feedback without letting it break your stride. And now, this week, a willingness to name that you've reached a different relationship with playing time without pretending it doesn't matter or falling apart because it does.

You said it best in the competition section, even though you said it was hard to explain: you're not giving up, and you're not killing yourself for something that isn't coming. You're somewhere in between, and that somewhere is a place most athletes don't know how to stand without losing their footing. You're standing there and still bringing it every day. That counts for more than you probably realize right now.
