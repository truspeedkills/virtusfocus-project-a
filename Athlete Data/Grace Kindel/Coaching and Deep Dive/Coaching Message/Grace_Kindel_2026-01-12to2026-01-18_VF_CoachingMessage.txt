Grace —

Seven days. First hell week of preseason. Full execution every single day.

The Thursday front toss session is the moment worth naming. Below your
standard, no disruption, you kept working. That's your reset pattern
activating before it even had a real test. You didn't spiral. You didn't
let one below-standard session change your frame for the rest of the week.
You reset and continued.

Your center ring was exactly where it belonged all week: mindset, daily
reset, and body health. You prepared, you executed, and your confidence
followed the effort — not the results.

You closed the week with this: "Not only did I prove it to others but to
myself." That's what preparation-based confidence looks like when it's
working. It starts with you. It ends with you. That's the version we're
building.

You came into this program wanting to stay confident in your ability
during competition. That identity is built in practice — in the quality
of your preparation and in how you respond when a rep doesn't go your
way. Thursday gave you the first live rep at both. You ran it correctly.

Your most important controllable heading into Week 2 is what happens in
the moment right after an error — whether you reset before the next rep
or you let the moment compound. Second hell week is where that gets
tested for real.

silent statement: "my effort, my reset." Before the next play begins.
Every time.

End of each practice, one question: Did I use it every time?
That's the full measurement.

Control what's in your center ring.
