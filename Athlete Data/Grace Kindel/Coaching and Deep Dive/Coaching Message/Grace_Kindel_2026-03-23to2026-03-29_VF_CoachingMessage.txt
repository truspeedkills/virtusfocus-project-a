Grace,

"I spoke up, I moved with a purpose, and I am succeeding." You wrote that this week. I want you to sit with those words, because they aren't aspirational. They're a factual description of what happened.

Wednesday you walked up to your coach and started the conversation about your playing time. You debated it Tuesday. You decided Wednesday. And you did it. That conversation didn't come out of nowhere. It came out of three weeks of processing: the frustration in Week 7, the self-directed practice with Kabine in Week 8, and now the direct engagement with the person making the decisions. You moved from reacting to what you can't control to addressing what you can influence. Ownership, plain and simple.

Sunday's pop-out was your second competitive failure in the program, and your most composed response yet. Week 7's strikeout needed two days and a mid-week reset. This one? Processed the same day: "I was ready, I was positive, was a good day." No frustration carry-over. No confidence disruption. Your reset habit is working so smoothly now that you don't even notice it doing its job.

You also hit on your own three times this week: Monday after practice, Wednesday with a teammate who's been struggling, and Friday on your off day. That's not a one-time initiative anymore. You're building your own development track alongside the team program, and that level of self-direction goes beyond what most athletes do without being asked.

One thing I want you to think about. Your coach mentioned speed, and it left you confused. That's worth sitting with, not to stress over, but to sort out. If speed is something you can work on with specific drills, it's a center-ring controllable you just discovered. If it's not something you can meaningfully change right now, it goes in your outer ring next to final decisions. Naming where it belongs is the move.

Goal-setting improved this week for the first time in eight weeks. "Be consistent and intentional" and "do one thing each day specifically to benefit me" have daily structure in them. Keep that going into PSACs. Specific daily intentions are how you put your preparation to work across eight games.

YOUR ACTION: BEFORE EACH GAME AT PSACS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Before each game at PSACs this week, say one specific thing out loud, to Kabine, to yourself, or written on your hand, that you want to own that day. Not "work hard." One specific center-ring action. After each game, name one thing you controlled well, whether you played or not. If you played: name the action. If you didn't play: name how you showed up.

At the end of PSACs, you should have one pre-game intention and one post-game controlled-action for each game day.

Show them what a chance looks like when you use it.

Coach Arron
