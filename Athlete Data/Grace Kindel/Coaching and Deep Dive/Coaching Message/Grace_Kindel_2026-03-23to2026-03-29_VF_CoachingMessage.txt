Grace —

Sixty-three straight Win days. Nine consecutive Growth weeks. And this week you did something you have never done before in this program — you walked up to your coach and started the conversation.

Last week, the commitment was to name one specific controllable before each doubleheader and say it out loud. No doubleheaders happened this week, but Sunday showed the behavior — you set a specific game-day goal ("stay ready when I'm called, have intentional warm ups") and you executed it. You were ready. You were positive. That commitment carries forward into PSACs where the doubleheader format will test it fully.

Here is what earned recognition this week.

You spoke to your coach on Wednesday. You debated it on Tuesday. You decided on Wednesday. And you did it. The conversation was positive, and it took something to walk up and start it. That is advocacy. Last week you went to the field with Kabine and built your own practice. This week you went directly to the person making the decisions and said what you needed to say. You named it yourself: "I spoke up, I moved with a purpose, and I am succeeding." All three of those things are true.

Sunday you got your AB in the second game and popped out. Your response was the most composed competitive failure response of the program. No processing delay. No frustration carry-over. "I was ready, I was positive, was a good day." That is what nine weeks of building this skill looks like — the hinge habit working so well you do not even notice it working.

You also hit on your own three times this week — Monday after practice, Wednesday with a teammate who is struggling, and Friday on an off day. That is not one initiative anymore. That is a pattern. You are building your own development program alongside the team one, and that is a level of ownership that goes beyond what most athletes ever do on their own.

Here is what needs attention. The coach mentioned something about speed that left you confused. That is worth sitting with — not to worry about it, but to decide what to do with it. Is speed something you can work on specifically? If it is, that is a center-ring controllable you just discovered. If it is not something actionable, it belongs in your outer ring alongside final decisions. Either way, naming where it goes is the move.

Goal-setting improved this week for the first time in eight weeks. "Be consistent and intentional, work on the little things" and "stretch everyday and do one thing each day specifically to benefit me" are the most structured goals you have set since Week 2. That improvement is real. Keep that going into PSACs — specific daily intentions are how you put your preparation to work across eight games.

You came into this program wanting to stay confident during competition. Nine weeks later, you have survived pressure, adversity, failure, exclusion, and confusion — and this week you showed you can advocate for yourself on top of all of it. PSACs will give you every opportunity to prove what you already know about who you are becoming.

YOUR ACTION — BEFORE EACH GAME AT PSACS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Before each game at PSACs this week, say one specific thing out loud — to Kabine, to yourself, or written on your hand — that you want to own that day. Not "work hard." One specific center-ring action. After each game, name one thing you controlled well, whether you played or not. If you played: name the action. If you did not play: name how you showed up.

At the end of PSACs, you should have one pre-game intention and one post-game controlled-action for each game day.

Own what is yours.
— Coach Arron
