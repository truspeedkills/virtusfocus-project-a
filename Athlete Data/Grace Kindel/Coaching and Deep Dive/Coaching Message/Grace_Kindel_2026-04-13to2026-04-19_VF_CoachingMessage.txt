Grace,

You wrote one sentence Wednesday that changed the shape of this week. "Try to focus running on my toes and not the backs of my feet." Specific. Physical. Something you can actually work on. It's the first thing you've named in a while that's about a concrete skill you're trying to build, not an emotional posture you're trying to hold. It's worth more than the word count on the entry suggests.

Tuesday sat next to it in a different way. "Been told I was never going to continue playing this sport and I'm here so boom." That's you reaching back into your own history to find the anchor, Grace. You pulled it from the longer arc, not the current week, not the playing time, not the coach situation. You were told you wouldn't make it, and you made it. That's the story you're standing in this week.

Last week the acceptance was the whole story. This week it sat in the background while you did specific things. Running on your toes. Being loud on Tuesday when the energy was low. Helping with equipment on Friday. Showing up for your dad on Saturday. The acceptance didn't leave. It stopped being the center, and something concrete took its place. That's what a healthy acceptance does. It makes room for you to work.

At Tuesday's and Friday's games you did name what you brought. That was most of what last week's ask was reaching for. What fell off was the writing piece, and Saturday and Sunday became the first days you haven't fully won in this program, both coming down to the challenge prompt that didn't get written. Senior Day, family in town, and a chill Sunday aren't the problem. The skipped writing is the pattern to notice, because you've already told yourself what the fix is: "sit down longer and sit with the mindset goals before the day starts." You named it before I could.
