Grace —

That was a hard week. The NC trip was rough — the playing time, the
coach situation, Friday cracking mentally, wanting to come home. That's
real. It's worth saying before anything else.

What's also real: you won all seven days. Not because it was easy.
Because you kept the routine when nothing else was holding. Planet Fitness
Monday. Room workout. School maintained. "Kept the routine for myself" —
that's your own achievement statement for the week. That's not just
execution. That's self-leadership.

The morning write-down showed up behaviorally even if it didn't make it
to paper — the daily structure held, the outcomes of the commitment were
there. Carry it forward.

Here's the most important signal from this week: "Honestly just doing it
for me at this point." Hear what that is. When playing time is
unpredictable, when the coach dynamic is frustrating, when competition
disappoints — you found an internal reason to keep competing. That is
the most durable form of motivation in this system. It doesn't need
favorable conditions. It doesn't require the coach to get it right.
It belongs to you.

You are building something the environment cannot take away from you.
Francis Marion is the same environment as NC — same travel, same coach
dynamic, same structural conditions. You've seen it now. Your controllable
going into that trip is your pre-game mental process, not what gets
decided for you.

writing. After each game: log one thing you controlled. Not the score,

One commitment before. One log after. Every game. That's it.
