Grace —

Seven days of full execution through snow days, makeup lifts, a heavy academic week,
and a dominant Friday scrimmage. That's not a good week — that's a prepared athlete
doing exactly what prepared athletes do.

Last week, the commitment was to write one sentence defining what a successful
performance looks like before leaving for Florida — anchored to a controllable, not
an outcome. The written sentence didn't make it to the page, but your goal this
week said it plainly: "If I'm given a chance, use it well." That's the standard.
Carry it forward.

Two things stood out this week.

Your Bullseye is the sharpest it's been in five weeks. "I can control how well I
warmup" is a competition anchor — not a general mindset statement, but a specific,
actionable lever you can pull before every game in NC and Florida. That's a
meaningful upgrade from where this program started.

Friday scrimmage: hits on all at-bats. That's the first dominant competitive
performance of the program — and it came right after two back-to-back trips with
minimal playing time. You planned the week, prepared through every day of it, and
performed. The chain worked.

You entered this program wanting to stay confident in your ability during
competition. That identity is built from how you prepare when no one's watching —
not from how many reps you get in the lineup. Your execution over the last five
weeks has moved you from Developing to Consistent. That's not a label — it's what
the record shows: 35 straight Win days, stable routines through adversity, and a
derailer that activated in Week 3 and didn't take you down. Your hinge habit —
execute, reset, continue — has held across five consecutive weeks, and this week
it extended into performance results. The warmup controllable you named is the
exact mechanism that makes "If I'm given a chance, use it well" executable.
That's your philosophy and your process working together.

Four consecutive weeks at full execution with low volatility and low fatigue load
heading into this trip. You've built real margin. Keep building from that position.

YOUR ACTION — NC AND FLORIDA
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Before each game in NC and Florida, complete a deliberate warmup with full focus.
Then say your own philosophy to yourself: "If I'm given a chance, I use it well."

No evaluation of playing time or outcomes during the trip.

After you're back — two questions:
  (1) Did I prepare well?
  (2) Did I use my chance?

Yes or no on both. That is the full measurement.

Here is your tool for NC and Florida. You named it. Now use it.

Lead yourself first.
— Coach Arron
