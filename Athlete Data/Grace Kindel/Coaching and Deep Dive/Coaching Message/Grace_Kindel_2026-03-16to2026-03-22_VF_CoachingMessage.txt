Grace —

Fifty-six straight Win days. Eight consecutive Growth weeks. And this week showed me something new about who you are becoming — not because of what happened in a game, but because of what you did on a Sunday.

Last week, the commitment was to write one sentence after each game day naming something you controlled well. Friday you named two — strong warmups and staying positive and loud when you did not get time. The controllable identification is there. What is still missing is the deliberate written sentence as a separate post-game action. That specific commitment has carried forward for several weeks now, and this week it shifts — not because you failed at writing, but because the format needs to match how you actually operate.

Here is what earned recognition this week.

You went to the field on your day off with Kabine and worked on defense. That sentence matters more than anything else in this recap. For two weeks you have been frustrated about not getting outfield reps at practice. This week you stopped waiting for someone else to solve that and created your own opportunity. That is agency. That is you controlling what you can control in the most literal way possible — going to a field and doing the work yourself. Nobody asked you to do that. Nobody assigned it. You decided.

Friday was a game day and you did not play. Your response was to stay positive and stay loud. That is now a consistent standard — you have proven across multiple game days that your presence does not depend on your playing time. You said you are sure as hell not quitting. That grit is real, and it shows every day.

Here is what needs attention. Goal-setting has been at its lowest point for seven consecutive weeks now. Your execution is maximal. Your intention is on autopilot. "Work hard everyday" does not give you a target to aim at — and with PSACs coming up, having a specific target before each doubleheader is how you put your preparation philosophy to work in the moments that matter most.

You also said something important this week — that you want to try to separate softball from your life, to stop letting it constantly take up space in your mind. That awareness is a strength. Recognizing what gets your attention and choosing where to direct it is the same skill you use in your Bullseye — putting the right things in the right rings. You are already good at that on paper. Now you are starting to apply it across your whole week.

You came into this program wanting to stay confident during competition. Eight weeks later, your confidence has survived pressure, adversity, failure, and exclusion. This week you added something new — you stopped waiting for what you needed and went out and built it yourself. That is the version of you that walks into PSACs ready for whatever comes.
