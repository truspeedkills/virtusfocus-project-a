GROWTH AREAS

WEEKEND EXPANSION TO DOUBLE MISSED
Saturday moved from Neutral to Missed for the first time in the program. "Hangout with friends" replaced the work or low-engagement patterns that had kept Saturday at Neutral in Weeks 2 through 4. Combined with Sunday's "Hangout with family," both weekend days scored Missed — the widest weekend gap of the program. *Social time is not the problem. Hanging out with friends and family is a normal part of a balanced week. The growth opportunity is finding a way to do one intentional thing alongside the social time — gym, coaching study, one personal development action — so that the day is not purely reactive.*

GOAL-SETTING PATTERN
"Get better and work harder" is a marginal improvement from the two prior weeks of "Become better" — the "work harder" at least introduces an action concept. But the five-week trajectory of goal specificity tells a story: three specific measurable targets (Week 1) to one specific target (Week 2) to "Become better" repeated (Weeks 3-4) to "Get better and work harder" (Week 5). The goals have moved from measurable to aspirational. When you had specific goals in Week 1, you could look at the end of the week and evaluate yourself against them. With "Get better and work harder," there is no clear way to know whether you achieved it.

*A goal does not have to be complicated to be useful. "Gym twice" or "one intentional activity on Saturday" would give you something to measure. When you can measure it, you can see your own progress — and that visibility is part of what keeps the habit alive.*

ACHIEVEMENT FRAMING
"Made it to spring break" as your achievement statement is a completion framing — getting to the finish line rather than describing what you built along the way. Compare it to Week 1's "The way I handled things" (process-focused) or even Week 4's "A lot of school work done preparing for spring break" (effort-focused). This week's version positions spring break as the reward and the preceding weeks as something to survive. That framing can signal reduced intentional engagement heading into the break itself.

*How you describe your achievement shapes how you think about the week. "Made it to spring break" says the week happened to you. "Finished every single weekday as a Win" says you happened to the week. Same data, different ownership.*

Two gym sessions. Named in advance. Told to someone. That is where spring break starts.
