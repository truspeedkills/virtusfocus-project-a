GROWTH AREAS

HINGE HABIT REGRESSION
Zero gym sessions this week. The four-week trajectory — 2, 1, 2, 0 — shows the gym has never become a stable part of your week. It bounced back after a down week before, but this is the first time it disappeared entirely. The spring break academic push may have legitimately compressed your schedule, but the gym is the habit you identified at the start of this program as the anchor for your personal discipline. When it drops to zero, it is worth asking whether the schedule genuinely did not allow it or whether it became the first thing to get cut when the week got busy.

*A hinge habit is called that because other things swing on it. When it is present, other good decisions tend to follow. When it is absent, the structure around it loosens. Noticing what happens to the rest of your week when the gym drops out is part of understanding how your habits connect to each other.*

GOAL-SETTING STAGNATION

The goal itself becomes a placeholder rather than a driver.

*A specific goal does not have to be complicated — "gym twice" or "one intentional Sunday activity" counts. What matters is that you can look at the end of the week and know clearly whether you did it or not.*

*The scoring process works best when every day gets an honest assessment, even the ones that feel unremarkable. Skipping it removes the moment where you check in with yourself about how the day went.*
