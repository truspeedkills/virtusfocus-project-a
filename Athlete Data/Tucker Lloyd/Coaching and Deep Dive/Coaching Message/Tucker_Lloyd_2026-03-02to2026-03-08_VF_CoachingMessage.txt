Tucker —

Three Win days again this week — Monday, Tuesday, Thursday. You pushed through a heavy academic load to get yourself ready for spring break, and that effort showed. Your achievement this week was real: a lot of school work completed with the finish line in sight.

A few things I want to name. The gym did not happen this week — zero sessions. That is the first time in four weeks you have gone without a single gym day. I know the schedule was loaded, but the gym is your hinge habit for a reason. When it drops, it tells me something about how the week squeezed you. That is worth noticing, not as a failure, but as a signal.

Sunday — you golfed. That is different from the last two Sundays, which were both "Nothing." Golfed is still a Missed day by your scoring, but I want you to see what changed. You went from complete disengagement to doing something. That shift matters. The Sunday alarm I asked you to set may or may not have been the reason, but either way, you showed up to the day differently than before. The bar is not golf specifically — it is choosing to engage when nobody requires it.

You picked "Durability is what transforms average into excellence over time" as your mindset focus. That is the right word for right now. Durability is not about one great week. It is about holding your habits together when the structure around you changes. And that is exactly what is about to be tested.

Spring break starts. No classes. No homework schedule. The thing that has driven your Win days every single week — academic structure — is gone for a full week. This is the most important week of your program so far, because it is the first time you will see what your habits look like without anyone requiring them.
