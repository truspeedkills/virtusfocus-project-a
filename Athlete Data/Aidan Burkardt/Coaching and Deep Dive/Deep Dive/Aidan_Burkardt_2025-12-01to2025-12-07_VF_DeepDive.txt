GROWTH AREAS
------------------------------------------------------------

WEEKEND SCORING CONSISTENCY
Across all three weeks, your Saturdays and Sundays have been productive — gym sessions, school work, time with friends — but they have not been scored. Your weekly numbers are built on five days instead of seven, which keeps your totals lower than your actual effort warrants. You are putting in the work on weekends. The question is whether you are giving yourself credit for it.
*Full weeks have seven days, and the ones that happen without structure sometimes reveal the most about your habits.*

SLEEP AS A PERSISTENT THREAD
Sleep was the primary growth area in Week 1 — you named it as a challenge and identified late bedtimes as the lever. Week 2, you said you needed to lock in on sleep. This week, sleep did not come up at all. That could mean it has improved, or it could mean it moved to the background during finals. Given that your next stated goal includes always prioritizing sleep, it is still on your radar. The gap is between naming it as a priority and having a routine around it.
*The habits that stay on the to-do list without a concrete structure tend to stay unresolved — the ones that get a specific routine tend to stick.*

REFLECTION DEPTH SUSTAINABILITY
Your reflection quality took a meaningful step forward this week. The identity-level language, the balanced self-assessment, and the emotional sophistication in your Bullseye all contributed. Weeks 1 and 2 were more activity-reporting. This week was genuine self-assessment, connecting your training to who you are becoming. The growth area is whether that deeper engagement stays consistent or was specific to a high-confidence week.
*The best reflections tend to happen when things are going well — the real test is whether the depth holds when a week does not go as planned.*

When you started this program, you said you wanted to independently be proud of who you are.
