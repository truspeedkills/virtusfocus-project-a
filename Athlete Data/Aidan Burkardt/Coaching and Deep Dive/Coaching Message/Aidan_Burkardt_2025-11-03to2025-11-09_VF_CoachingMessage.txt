Aidan —

Four Win days out of five scored, a .96 10m fly, a 300lb floor press, and two meetings with the Pitt coaching staff to set walk-on goals. That's not just a good first week — that's an athlete who didn't wait for someone else to tell him he was ready to start.

Two things stood out.

First, the Pitt meetings. You didn't ask for permission to pursue the walk-on. You initiated the conversation yourself — scheduled the meeting, set the goals, took the step. That is independent confidence in action. Not confidence built from a result or a compliment, but from deciding you're ready and acting on it. That's exactly the identity you described wanting at intake.

Second, your Bullseye. You placed "I can't control where I'm at right now based on the past" in the noise ring — which means you see it. You know the comparison pattern is there, and you know it doesn't belong in the center. That awareness is the first step toward not letting it run your preparation. Keep placing it where it belongs.

The one area to sharpen: sleep. You named it yourself — "sleep has been rough due to school work." Thursday's dip from Win to Neutral came directly from going to bed late. Your training execution is strong. Your gym discipline is real. Sleep is the one controllable that protects everything else. When the recovery isn't there, the execution base you've built has less to run on.

You came into this program wanting to build independent self-esteem — to be proud of who you are without needing validation from anyone else. Scheduling those Pitt meetings was that identity in action. You didn't wait. You moved. That's the standard to build from.
