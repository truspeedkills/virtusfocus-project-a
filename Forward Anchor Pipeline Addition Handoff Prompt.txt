WHAT IS THE FORWARD ANCHOR?
============================================================

A single-line text input on the Weekly Recap screen:
  Prompt: "One thing you can control next week."

The athlete writes ONE short, controllable focus they carry into
the next week. Not a goal list, not a wish — one specific thing
within their control.

Examples:
  - "Respond calmly to mistakes."
  - "Complete my morning challenge every day."
  - "Bring energy to practice."

The Forward Anchor is the psychological bridge between weeks.
The Weekly Recap closes the current week — the Forward Anchor
is the one piece that points forward.

============================================================
HOW IT DIFFERS FROM EXISTING FIELDS
============================================================

Three related but distinct concepts already exist:

MICRO_COMMITMENT (Stage 3 output — coach-assigned)
  Generated by Coach Arron in the ONE Action section of each
  weekly Coaching Message. Coach assigns a specific behavioral
  action. Tracked via prior_commitment_result the following week.
  Has modality rules (Written, Behavioral, Verbal, Oral). The
  athlete does NOT author this — the coach does.

Q3_UPCOMING_GOALS (Motivation Inventory — athlete-authored)
  Free text: "What are your goals for the upcoming week?"
  Open-ended, no controllability constraint. Can contain multiple
  goals, outcome-oriented wishes. Routes to
  upcoming_context.athlete_stated_next_goal.
  No alignment tracking against subsequent behavior.

FORWARD ANCHOR (proposed — athlete-authored)
  Single-line, constrained to ONE controllable focus. Explicitly
  framed around controllability ("one thing you can control").
  Creates a perception-reality comparison: what the athlete says
  they will focus on vs. what they actually do. Week-to-week
  consistency signal. Bridges the weekly close to daily actions.

The Forward Anchor fills a gap none of the existing fields cover:
a constrained, controllable, athlete-authored weekly intention
with behavioral alignment tracking.

============================================================
3 NEW ANALYTICAL SIGNALS
============================================================

SIGNAL 1: CONTROLLABILITY CLASSIFICATION
  Does the athlete think in controllable vs. outcome terms?
  - Controllable: "Bring energy to practice every day"
  - Outcome-Oriented: "Win my next match"
  - Mixed: "Play confident in the big moments"
  Longitudinal pattern reveals growth (outcome → controllable is
  aligned with the Bullseye Method's center-ring philosophy).

SIGNAL 2: WEEK-TO-WEEK FOCUS CONSISTENCY
  Compare current forward_anchor to prior week(s):
  - Consistent: same theme or related controllable focus
  - Shifting: different focus area each week
  - Scattered: no coherent thread across 3+ weeks

SIGNAL 3: FORWARD ANCHOR ALIGNMENT
  Does what the athlete anchored on show up in their actual
  behavior the following week? Tracked via Morning Tune-Up focus
  words, Bullseye entries, journal reflections, WTD patterns.
  Classification: Aligned / Partial / Misaligned / Insufficient Data
  This mirrors self_ratings perception-reality alignment — but at
  the intention level rather than the self-assessment level.
  NOTE: This is a DEFERRED signal — computed the NEXT week
  looking back, same timing as prior_commitment_result.

============================================================
6 DESIGN QUESTIONS TO RESOLVE
============================================================

All 6 must be resolved before implementation begins.

DQ1: MICRO_COMMITMENT INTERACTION MODEL
  How does the coach-assigned micro_commitment interact with the
  athlete-authored Forward Anchor? Three options:

  OPTION A — Independent Tracks
    micro_commitment stays coach-assigned. Forward Anchor is
    acknowledged but separate. Coach may reference the athlete's
    anchor but the micro_commitment is still coach-directed.

  OPTION B — Anchor-Informed Commitment
    The micro_commitment builds ON the athlete's Forward Anchor
    when alignment is strong. If controllable and specific, coach
    uses it as foundation and adds precision. If outcome-oriented
    or vague, coach redirects toward controllable alternative.

  OPTION C — Anchor Replaces Commitment (Premium only)
    For established Premium athletes with strong controllability
    patterns, the Forward Anchor IS the commitment. Base tier and
    early-program athletes still get coach-assigned micro_commitments.

  This is the biggest design decision in this proposal.

DQ2: ALIGNMENT TIMING
  Forward Anchor Alignment is inherently a NEXT-WEEK signal (you
  state your anchor this week, alignment is measured next week).
  This means:
  - Week 1 forward_anchor_alignment = always "No Prior Week"
  - The signal is always one week behind
  - Same timing pattern as prior_commitment_result
  Confirm this is acceptable or explore alternatives.

DQ3: CONTROLLABILITY CLASSIFICATION METHOD
  Pure keyword-based (deterministic) or agent-classified
  (judgment-based)? Controllability is nuanced:
  - "Play with confidence" — is this controllable?
  - "Be a good teammate" — controllable but vague
  - "Win my match" — clearly outcome
  Recommendation from proposal: agent-classified with guided
  examples (similar to PPD tie-breaker). But this adds LLM
  judgment where the pipeline prefers deterministic rules.

DQ4: BASE TIER FORWARD ANCHOR
  Base tier athletes submit the Weekly Recap (which includes the
  Forward Anchor) but do NOT receive a Coaching Message with a
  micro_commitment. The Forward Anchor may be their ONLY forward-
  looking element. Does this change how Stage 6 uses it for Base
  tier daily snippets?

DQ5: Q3_UPCOMING_GOALS REDUNDANCY
  With the Forward Anchor added, q3_upcoming_goals becomes
  partially redundant. Options:
  - Keep both (q3 stays open-ended, Forward Anchor is structured)
  - Retire q3 and let Forward Anchor + competition schedule cover
    the forward-looking space
  - Merge: reframe q3 to broader context only

DQ6: COMPLIANCE CLASSIFICATION
  Is the raw Forward Anchor text athlete-private (like journal
  content — never shown to coaches) or athlete-visible (like WTD
  scores — can inform coach-facing output)?
  Recommendation from proposal: raw text stays private (inform-
  only for Stage 4), but controllability classification and
  alignment signal are compliance-safe in translated form.
