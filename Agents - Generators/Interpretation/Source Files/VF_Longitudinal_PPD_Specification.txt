================================================================
SECTION A: ARCHITECTURE OVERVIEW
================================================================

PURPOSE:
The Longitudinal PPD system compares what the athlete THINKS
their primary mental performance friction areas are (intake
self-report PPD) against what accumulated execution data SHOWS
their actual friction areas are (execution-derived PPD). The
gap between these two produces the PPD Alignment Signal:
Aligned / Gap / Blind Spot.

This is a Stage 2 (Interpretation Engine) enhancement that
feeds Stage 3 (Coaching Output Engine) for improved coaching
direction and tone calibration. It does NOT feed Stage 4
(Coach Insights) or any institutional output.

MIRROR MODEL:
The Longitudinal PPD mirrors the existing self_ratings_alignment
system (Section 12, VF_Interpretation_JSON_Rules.txt):

  self_ratings_alignment:
    Compares: weekly 1-10 self-rating vs. composite score bands
    Output: Aligned / Conflated / Undervalued
    Cadence: weekly
    Routing: Stage 3 tone (Conflated=ground, Undervalued=elevate)
    Walling: inform-only, labels never surfaced

  longitudinal_ppd:
    Compares: intake PPD bucket ranking vs. execution-derived
              bucket ranking
    Output: Aligned / Gap / Blind Spot
    Cadence: weekly (stabilized by rolling window)
    Routing: Stage 3 priority (Tier 2 replacement when
             Established) + tone (Blind Spot=ground, Gap=elevate)
    Walling: inform-only, labels never surfaced

KEY ARCHITECTURAL FEATURES:
- Inform-only walling (classifications stay internal to pipeline)
- Behavioral translation in all output (never name classifications)
- Leverages existing computation (composite scores, coach flags,
  v9.6 fields — no new weighted formulas)
- App-athlete feature only (CF = "insufficient data")
- Future Stage 2 enhancement — not required for app v1 launch

CANONICAL EXAMPLE — TUCKER LLOYD (BLIND SPOT):
  Intake PPD Top 3: Overthinking Loop (5), Discipline Gap (5),
                    Confidence Volatility (3)
  Execution data (5 weeks): 3 consecutive Missed Sundays,
    zero gym sessions in Week 4, weekend engagement expanding
    from 1→2 Missed days, rhythm disrupted, stagnant
    consistency trend
  Intake Structure Gap score: 2 (Q13 amplifier only — athlete
    did NOT select "Lack of routine or structure" on Q15)
  Execution Structure Gap evidence: STRONG
  PPD Alignment Signal: BLIND SPOT

================================================================
SECTION B: EXECUTION-TO-BUCKET MAPPING
================================================================

Each of the 8 PPD buckets is mapped to a set of execution-
derived signals that indicate whether that friction area is
ACTUALLY present in the athlete's behavior, regardless of
intake self-report.

SCORING APPROACH: Hybrid (Option C)
Each bucket is classified into a 5-tier evidence strength
using deterministic rules based on existing composite score
bands, coach flags, and v9.6 fields. No new weighted formulas
are created — the system leverages all existing computation.

EVIDENCE TIERS:

  STRONG — Multiple execution signals converge on this bucket,
           sustained across weeks. High confidence — this is a
           real friction area regardless of self-report.

  MODERATE — Some execution signals present, or strong signals
             but limited weeks of evidence. Meaningful — worth
             monitoring, may inform coaching direction.

  WEAK — Minimal or isolated execution evidence. Low confidence
         — insufficient to redirect coaching.

  NONE — No execution signals fire for this bucket. Not present
         in behavioral data.

  CONTEXT INSUFFICIENT — Required conditions (competition,
                         specific stressors) not present in the
                         measurement window. Cannot evaluate —
                         distinct from None (absence of context,
                         not absence of problem).

----------------------------------------------------------------
BUCKET 1: OVERTHINKING LOOP
Excessive mental rehearsal of negative scenarios; rumination
displacing execution focus.
----------------------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY SIGNALS:
- reactivity_risk_score.band (Elevated = reactive/ruminative
  execution patterns)
- bullseye_behavior.outer_ring_dominant_rate (high = persistently
  fixating on uncontrollables)
- wtd_question_patterns.intraweek_volatility (High = day-to-day
  score swings suggesting mental state instability)

SECONDARY SIGNALS:
- bullseye_behavior.contradiction_detected (Yes = naming
  controllables but focusing on uncontrollables)

EVIDENCE TIER DETERMINATION:

  STRONG:
    reactivity_risk_score.band == "Elevated"
    AND (outer_ring_dominant_rate >= 0.40
         OR intraweek_volatility == "High")
    Sustained 2+ weeks in rolling window.

  MODERATE:
    reactivity_risk_score.band == "Moderate"
    AND (outer_ring_dominant_rate >= 0.40
         OR intraweek_volatility == "High"
         OR contradiction_detected == "Yes")
    OR: reactivity_risk_score.band == "Elevated"
        (single week only, not yet sustained)

  WEAK:
    Any single signal present at threshold but others normal.
    Example: outer_ring_dominant_rate >= 0.40 in isolation
    with Low reactivity and Low volatility.

  NONE:
    reactivity_risk_score.band == "Low"
    AND outer_ring_dominant_rate < 0.40
    AND intraweek_volatility != "High"
    AND contradiction_detected != "Yes"

NOTE: Overthinking is partially content-derived (Stage 2
detects rumination language in journaling). The execution
mapping captures the behavioral signature of overthinking —
volatility, outer-ring fixation, contradiction. Content-
derived signals (e.g., journaling themes) are NOT used here
to maintain the execution-derived boundary.

----------------------------------------------------------------
BUCKET 2: CONFIDENCE VOLATILITY
Confidence tied to outcomes rather than preparation; unstable
self-belief.
----------------------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY SIGNALS:
- self_ratings_alignment.confidence classification (Conflated
  or Undervalued = perception-reality mismatch on confidence)
- consistency_signal (Variable or Declining = week-to-week
  WTD instability)

SECONDARY SIGNALS:
- WTD score variance between competition vs non-competition
  weeks (score drops when stakes rise = outcome-dependent
  confidence)
- follow_through_score.band declining trend over weeks
  (weakening commitment follow-through can reflect eroding
  self-belief)

EVIDENCE TIER DETERMINATION:

  STRONG:
    self_ratings_alignment.confidence == "Conflated"
    AND consistency_signal in ["Variable", "Declining"]
    Sustained 2+ weeks in rolling window.
    OR: self_ratings_alignment.confidence == "Conflated"
        AND competition-week WTD drop observed.

  MODERATE:
    self_ratings_alignment.confidence == "Conflated"
    (single dimension mismatch without additional signals)
    OR: self_ratings_alignment.confidence == "Undervalued"
        AND consistency_signal == "Variable"
    OR: consistency_signal == "Declining" sustained 2+ weeks
        without self_ratings data.

  WEAK:
    self_ratings_alignment.confidence == "Undervalued"
    in isolation (athlete underestimates but execution
    shows capability — this is the OPPOSITE of confidence
    volatility; may indicate low self-belief but not
    volatility per se).
    OR: Isolated single-week consistency dip.

  NONE:
    self_ratings_alignment.confidence == "Aligned"
    AND consistency_signal in ["Stable", "Improving"]

  NOTE ON UNDERVALUED:
  "Undervalued" confidence (athlete rates low, execution
  shows high) is NOT the same as Confidence Volatility.
  Undervalued suggests low self-belief, not unstable belief.
  Confidence Volatility specifically requires outcome-
  dependent swings. Undervalued alone maps to Weak, not
  Moderate or Strong.

----------------------------------------------------------------
BUCKET 3: MISTAKE RECOVERY LAG
Slow/incomplete mental reset after errors; mistakes carry
forward across days.
----------------------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY SIGNALS:
- recovery_score.band (Low = direct measure of post-disruption
  recovery failure)
- recovery_speed_days (4+ = slow return to Win after Missed day)
- recommitment_signal (Low = poor within-week bounce-back)

SECONDARY SIGNALS:
- cross_week_recovery.recovery_days (high or "did not recover"
  = recovery failure extending across week boundary)
- recovery_failure flag (fires when recovery_score.band == Low)

EVIDENCE TIER DETERMINATION:

  STRONG:
    recovery_score.band == "Low"
    OR recovery_failure flag fires
    OR (recovery_speed_days >= 4 sustained across 2+ recovery
        events in rolling window)

  MODERATE:
    recovery_score.band == "Moderate"
    OR recommitment_signal == "Low"
    OR cross_week_recovery.recovery_days >= 3

  WEAK:
    recovery_speed_days == 3 (isolated single instance)
    AND recovery_score.band != "Low"
    AND recommitment_signal != "Low"

  NONE:
    recovery_score.band == "Strong"
    OR recovery_score == "no disruption" (fixed 85 — no
    adverse events occurred, recovery not tested)

  CONTEXT INSUFFICIENT:
    No WTD disruption events in measurement window (zero
    Missed days across all measured weeks). Recovery
    capability cannot be evaluated without adversity.
    NOTE: This is distinct from "no disruption" Recovery
    Score (which returns fixed 85). Context Insufficient
    here means the BUCKET cannot be evaluated, not that
    recovery is good.

NOTE: This is the cleanest mapping in the system — Recovery
Score was designed to measure exactly this behavioral
dimension.

----------------------------------------------------------------
BUCKET 4: FOCUS DRIFT / DISTRACTION
Attention pulled from controllables during performance;
external distractions disrupt engagement quality.
----------------------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY SIGNALS:
- drift_score.band (Early or Active = engagement erosion /
  compression pattern)
- bullseye_behavior.outer_ring_dominant_rate (high over
  sustained weeks = attention directed at uncontrollables)

SECONDARY SIGNALS:
- component_avoidance flag (selective avoidance of one system
  component = attention not fully engaged)
- journaling_behavior.domain_omission_pattern != "none"
  (omitting domains suggests attention narrowing or avoidance)
- drift_early flag or drift_active flag

EVIDENCE TIER DETERMINATION:

  STRONG:
    drift_score.band == "Active"
    OR drift_active flag fires
    OR (drift_score.band == "Early" AND outer_ring_dominant_rate
        >= 0.40 sustained 2+ weeks)

  MODERATE:
    drift_score.band == "Early"
    OR (outer_ring_dominant_rate >= 0.40 sustained 2+ weeks
        without drift_score elevation)
    OR (component_avoidance flag fires AND domain_omission_pattern
        != "none")

  WEAK:
    drift_early flag fires in isolation (single week, no
    supporting signals).
    OR: domain_omission_pattern != "none" in isolation.

  NONE:
    drift_score.band == "None"
    AND outer_ring_dominant_rate < 0.40
    AND no component_avoidance flag
    AND domain_omission_pattern == "none"

NOTE: Drift Score measures engagement erosion broadly — not
just focus/distraction specifically. Bullseye outer-ring
dominance and domain omission add focus-specific evidence
that distinguishes this bucket from Discipline Gap (which
also involves drift-adjacent patterns).

----------------------------------------------------------------
BUCKET 5: PRESSURE REACTIVITY
Behavioral/mental performance changes under high-stakes
conditions; altered execution patterns when pressure exists.
----------------------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY SIGNALS:
- reactivity_risk_score.band (Elevated = volatile execution
  patterns indicating event-reactive behavior)
- WTD score drop in competition weeks vs non-competition
  weeks (direct evidence of performance degradation)
- emotional_intensity (High, especially recurring = language-
  based intensity spikes)

SECONDARY SIGNALS:
- baseline_alignment == "Partially Aligned" during competition
  weeks (derailer activation under competitive pressure)
- reactivity_elevated flag

SEASON PHASE CONTEXT GATE:
This bucket requires competitive or high-stakes context to
manifest. The current_season_phase field (collected weekly
via check-in) gates evaluation:

  In-Season / Post-Season:
    FULLY EVALUABLE. Competition context exists. All signals
    meaningful.

  Pre-Season:
    PARTIALLY EVALUABLE. Some pressure contexts exist (tryouts,
    scrimmages, evaluations) but less frequent. Evidence
    weighted at reduced confidence — Strong evidence in
    Pre-Season maps to Moderate tier. Moderate maps to Weak.

  Off-Season / Year-Round (no competition in window):
    CONTEXT INSUFFICIENT. Cannot evaluate. Not the same as
    None — the conditions to trigger pressure reactivity
    have not been present.

  Mixed season phases in rolling window:
    Use the most evaluable phase present. If 2 weeks are
    In-Season and 2 weeks Off-Season, evaluate using the
    In-Season weeks only.

EVIDENCE TIER DETERMINATION (when evaluable):

  STRONG:
    reactivity_risk_score.band == "Elevated"
    AND (competition-week WTD drop observed
         OR emotional_intensity == "High" in competition week)
    Sustained across 2+ competition-context weeks.

  MODERATE:
    reactivity_risk_score.band == "Elevated" (without
    competition-specific WTD drop — general volatility
    present but not yet linked to pressure context)
    OR: Competition-week WTD drop observed but
        reactivity_risk_score.band == "Moderate"
    OR: emotional_intensity == "High" in competition week
        without reactivity elevation

  WEAK:
    Isolated single-week reactivity elevation without
    competition context confirmation.
    OR: emotional_intensity == "Moderate" in competition
        week with no other signals.

  NONE:
    reactivity_risk_score.band == "Low"
    AND no competition-week WTD drops
    AND emotional_intensity not elevated in competition weeks
    (Requires sufficient competition-context weeks to
    evaluate — see Context Gate above)

----------------------------------------------------------------
BUCKET 6: DISCIPLINE GAP
Inconsistent motivation/effort; habits fluctuate based on
mood, outcomes, or circumstances.
----------------------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY SIGNALS:
- ownership_index.band (Low = not self-initiating engagement,
  dependent on external prompts)
- follow_through_score.band (Weak = accepting commitments but
  not executing = effort inconsistency)

SECONDARY SIGNALS:
- morning_disengagement flag (selective morning skip while
  completing evening = effort selectivity, not structural
  absence)
- surface_engagement flag (high completion but degraded
  quality = going through motions)

EVIDENCE TIER DETERMINATION:

  STRONG:
    ownership_index.band == "Low"
    AND (follow_through_score.band == "Weak"
         OR morning_disengagement flag fires)
    OR: ownership_low flag AND follow_through_weak flag
        fire simultaneously
    OR: surface_engagement flag fires sustained 2+ weeks

  MODERATE:
    ownership_index.band == "Low" (without follow-through
    confirmation)
    OR: follow_through_score.band == "Weak" (without
        ownership confirmation)
    OR: morning_disengagement flag fires (isolated)

  WEAK:
    ownership_index.band == "Moderate" AND
    follow_through_score.band == "Moderate"
    AND one supporting flag fires (surface_engagement
    or morning_disengagement, single week)

  NONE:
    ownership_index.band == "High"
    AND follow_through_score.band in ["Strong", "Moderate"]
    AND no discipline-related flags

DISTINGUISHING FROM STRUCTURE GAP (BUCKET 7):
Discipline Gap = motivation/effort inconsistency. The athlete
CAN structure but doesn't maintain effort consistently.
Structure Gap = routine/rhythm absence. The athlete doesn't
HAVE the routine.

Key differentiator: If performance is strong when external
structure exists (weekdays, scheduled activities) but collapses
when structure is removed (weekends, breaks), the primary
signal is Structure Gap, not Discipline Gap. Discipline Gap
presents as inconsistent effort WITHIN structured time
(skipping morning tune-ups despite having time, doing the
minimum, surface engagement).

Tucker Lloyd example: His weekday execution is strong (5/5 Win
in Week 5) — this rules out Discipline Gap as primary.
His weekend execution collapses (2/2 Missed) — this points to
Structure Gap. Tucker has motivation; he lacks self-directed
routine. Discipline Gap = Moderate (follow-through on micro-
commitments has been partial), Structure Gap = Strong.

----------------------------------------------------------------
BUCKET 7: STRUCTURE GAP
Absence/inconsistency of pre-performance routines, daily
rhythms, or training structure.
----------------------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY SIGNALS:
- rhythm_score.band (Disrupted = timing and sequence
  inconsistency in daily routines)
- rhythm_disrupted flag (formal flag for structural breakdown)

SECONDARY SIGNALS:
- evening_review.sequence_integrity (Broken = routine
  component ordering not maintained)
- Weekday vs weekend WTD divergence (structural dependency —
  performance collapses when external scaffolding removed)

EVIDENCE TIER DETERMINATION:

  STRONG:
    rhythm_score.band == "Disrupted"
    OR rhythm_disrupted flag fires
    OR (rhythm_score.band == "Variable" AND weekday-weekend
        WTD win rate divergence >= 3 Win-day difference
        AND sustained 2+ weeks in rolling window)

  MODERATE:
    rhythm_score.band == "Variable"
    OR sequence_integrity == "Broken"
    OR (weekday-weekend divergence present but < 3 Win-day
        difference or not yet sustained 2+ weeks)

  WEAK:
    sequence_integrity == "Partial"
    AND rhythm_score.band == "Stable"
    AND no weekday-weekend divergence
    (Minor routine imperfections without structural impact)

  NONE:
    rhythm_score.band == "Stable"
    AND sequence_integrity == "Intact"
    AND no weekday-weekend divergence

WEEKDAY-WEEKEND DIVERGENCE CALCULATION:
For each week in the rolling window:
  weekday_win_rate = (Mon-Fri Win days) / (Mon-Fri scored days)
  weekend_win_rate = (Sat-Sun Win days) / (Sat-Sun scored days)
  divergence = weekday_win_rate - weekend_win_rate
If divergence >= 0.40 (approximately 3+ Win-day difference
in a 7-day week) sustained across 2+ weeks → Strong evidence
of structural dependency.

Tucker Lloyd validation: Weeks 2-5 show weekday 4-5 Wins,
weekend 0-1 Wins. Divergence is extreme and sustained.
Structure Gap = Strong.

----------------------------------------------------------------
BUCKET 8: ECOSYSTEM FRICTION
External pressure from coaches, parents, or environment
destabilizing mental performance.
----------------------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY SIGNALS:
- stress_load (Elevated, especially without competition
  context — suggests environmental rather than competitive
  pressure)
- noise_fixation_present (Yes = fixation on external noise
  intruding on controllable focus)

SECONDARY SIGNALS (CONTENT-DERIVED SUPPLEMENTS):
- Bullseye outer-ring content referencing coaches, parents,
  or environmental stressors (Stage 2 detects in text analysis)
- Journaling themes indicating ecosystem conflict (Stage 2
  already captures)

EVIDENCE TIER DETERMINATION:

  STRONG:
    stress_load == "Elevated" sustained 2+ weeks
    AND (noise_fixation_present == "Yes"
         OR Bullseye outer-ring coach/parent references
            sustained 2+ weeks)
    AND no scheduled_competition in those weeks (ruling out
        competition-induced stress)

  MODERATE:
    stress_load == "Elevated" (single week, non-competition)
    OR noise_fixation_present == "Yes" sustained 2+ weeks
    OR Bullseye outer-ring coach/parent references present
       but stress_load == "Moderate"

  WEAK:
    stress_load == "Moderate" with isolated ecosystem-
    referencing outer-ring content (single week).
    OR: noise_fixation_present == "Yes" (single week only)

  NONE:
    stress_load in ["Low", "Moderate"] consistently
    AND noise_fixation_present != "Yes"
    AND no ecosystem-referencing outer-ring patterns

SIGNAL QUALITY NOTE:
Ecosystem Friction has the LOWEST execution signal quality of
all 8 buckets. It is primarily content-derived, not execution-
derived. The behavioral signature is weak — an athlete
experiencing ecosystem friction might show the same execution
patterns as one with any other stressor. The content-derived
supplements (stress_load, noise_fixation, outer-ring themes)
provide the most meaningful increase in coaching quality for
this bucket, even though they are not pure execution signals.

SECTION C: COMPUTATION CADENCE AND MINIMUM DATA THRESHOLD
================================================================

COMPUTATION CADENCE: Weekly with Stabilized Alignment Signal

The Longitudinal PPD is computed every week as part of the
Stage 2 Interpretation Engine processing. However, the signal
is inherently stable because it is based on a ROLLING WINDOW
of accumulated evidence across all available weeks, not a
single week's data.

ROLLING WINDOW LOGIC:
For each bucket, the evidence tier is determined by the
PREDOMINANT classification across all weeks in the window:

  Step 1: Compute per-week evidence tier for each bucket
          using that week's execution data and the rules
          in Section B.

  Step 2: Across all weeks in the rolling window, determine
          the predominant tier:
          - If a bucket is Strong in >= 50% of evaluable weeks
            → Strong overall
          - If a bucket is Moderate-or-above in >= 50% of
            evaluable weeks → Moderate overall
          - If a bucket is Weak-or-above in >= 50% of
            evaluable weeks → Weak overall
          - Otherwise → None

  Step 3: "Context Insufficient" weeks are excluded from the
          percentage calculation (they reduce the denominator,
          not the count). If ALL weeks are Context Insufficient
          for a bucket → Context Insufficient overall.

  Step 4: Derive execution-derived Top 3 by ranking buckets
          by evidence tier (Strong > Moderate > Weak). Ties
          broken by bucket priority order (1→8, matching
          intake PPD Layer 4 tie-breaker).

MINIMUM DATA THRESHOLD: Progressive (Two Confidence Tiers)

  INSUFFICIENT DATA (< 3 weeks accumulated):
    All longitudinal_ppd fields = "insufficient data"
    Status = "insufficient data"
    No computation performed. No alignment signal.

  EMERGING (3-4 weeks accumulated):
    Status = "emerging"
    Execution-derived bucket evidence tiers computed.
    Alignment Signal computed but labeled "Emerging."
    Stage 3 routing: Tier 2 remains static intake PPD.
    Stage 3 tone: Alignment Signal MAY subtly inform tone
    but does NOT redirect coaching emphasis.
    Purpose: Early detection, coaching heads-up, not
    confident enough to redirect.

  ESTABLISHED (5+ weeks accumulated):
    Status = "established"
    Full computation with high confidence.
    Stage 3 routing: Tier 2 replaced with execution-derived
    PPD Top 3.
    Stage 3 tone: Full Alignment Signal tone modification
    active (Blind Spot=ground, Gap=elevate, Aligned=confirm).
    Purpose: Sufficient evidence to confidently redirect
    coaching emphasis when warranted.

RATIONALE FOR 3 AND 5 WEEK THRESHOLDS:
- 3 weeks: Minimum for pattern detection. Tucker's Sunday
  Missed was a pattern by Week 3 (3 consecutive), not just
  a one-off by Week 2. Two weeks is one data point and one
  comparison — insufficient to distinguish signal from noise.
- 5 weeks: Sufficient accumulated evidence for a rolling
  window to be meaningful. Tucker at 5 weeks has an
  unmistakable Structure Gap pattern. Grace at 5 weeks has
  clear Aligned confirmation including adversity test (Week 3)
  and recovery (Weeks 4-5). Enough varied contexts (good
  weeks, bad weeks, possibly competition) for reliable signal.

================================================================
SECTION D: PPD ALIGNMENT SIGNAL CLASSIFICATION
================================================================

The PPD Alignment Signal is determined by comparing the intake
PPD Top 3 (from ppd_top_3 in baseline_intake_profile) against
the execution-derived Top 3 (computed in Section C).

THREE CLASSIFICATION STATES:

  ALIGNED:
    Intake and execution agree on primary friction areas.
    Athlete's self-awareness is accurate. Continue current
    coaching emphasis.

  GAP:
    Intake identified some problems, but execution reveals
    different problems as primary. Some overlap exists —
    the athlete has partial awareness but underestimated
    certain areas.
    Coaching: begin shifting emphasis toward what execution
    shows, while acknowledging what the athlete identified.
    Gradual redirection.

  BLIND SPOT:
    Execution reveals a significant friction area the athlete
    did NOT self-identify at intake (low or zero intake score).
    Coaching: introduce the unseen pattern gently. Grounding
    language — "here's what's showing up in your weeks."
    Not accusatory. Same approach as Conflated in self_ratings
    (ground without shaming).

CLASSIFICATION LOGIC:

  STEP 1: DERIVE EXECUTION TOP 3
    Rank all 8 buckets by evidence tier:
      Strong > Moderate > Weak > None
    Skip "None" and "Context Insufficient" buckets.
    Within same tier, use bucket priority order (1→8).
    Take top 3 (or fewer if less than 3 buckets have
    Weak-or-above evidence).
    If fewer than 3 buckets have any evidence → signal
    may be unreliable. Flag but still compute.

  STEP 2: COMPARE AGAINST INTAKE TOP 3
    Count OVERLAP: buckets appearing in BOTH intake Top 3
    and execution Top 3.
    Identify NEW ENTRIES: buckets in execution Top 3 but
    NOT in intake Top 3.
    Identify DROPPED: buckets in intake Top 3 but NOT in
    execution Top 3.

  STEP 3: CLASSIFY EACH NEW ENTRY
    For each new entry, compare intake score (from
    ppd_all_scores) against execution evidence tier:

    intake_score <= 2 AND execution = Strong → BLIND SPOT
      (Athlete had essentially no awareness. Intake score
       of 0 = no Q15 selection AND no amplifier fired.
       Score of 1-2 = amplifier fired but athlete did not
       self-identify via Q15.)

    intake_score <= 2 AND execution = Moderate → GAP
      (Some evidence emerging but athlete was unaware.
       Moderate confidence — could still be Gap rather
       than confirmed Blind Spot.)

    intake_score 3-4 AND execution = Strong or Moderate → GAP
      (Athlete had SOME awareness — the bucket had a
       non-trivial intake score — but underestimated its
       importance. The friction area was ranked 4th-5th
       at intake but is now top 3 in execution.)

    intake_score >= 5 → unlikely to be a new entry
      (A score of 5+ means the bucket was already
       significant at intake. If it appears in execution
       Top 3, it likely also appeared in intake Top 3.
       Edge case: if it appears as new despite high score,
       classify as GAP — the problems shifted in ranking
       rather than being unseen.)

  STEP 4: DETERMINE OVERALL ALIGNMENT SIGNAL

    3/3 overlap (same Top 3 in both):
      → ALIGNED

    2/3 overlap:
      Check the 1 new entry classification from Step 3:
        If Blind Spot → overall BLIND SPOT
        If Gap → overall GAP

    1/3 overlap:
      → GAP (significant divergence but some self-awareness)
      UNLESS a new entry is classified as Blind Spot →
      overall BLIND SPOT

    0/3 overlap:
      → BLIND SPOT (complete misalignment between self-
         perception and execution reality)

    OVERRIDE RULE:
    Any single bucket classified as Blind Spot in Step 3
    AND execution evidence = Strong → overall signal =
    BLIND SPOT, regardless of overlap count. One strong
    blind spot is enough to flag, even with 2/3 overlap.
    Rationale: A coaching-significant unseen friction area
    should not be masked by partial overlap elsewhere.

  STEP 5: HANDLE EDGE CASES

    Fewer than 3 buckets have evidence:
      If only 2 buckets have Weak-or-above evidence,
      compare those 2 against intake Top 3.
      Apply same overlap logic with adjusted denominator.
      Overall signal reliability is reduced — note in
      alignment_signal output.

    All buckets are None or Context Insufficient:
      longitudinal_ppd.status remains "insufficient data"
      regardless of weeks_accumulated. The athlete has
      execution data but no discernible friction patterns.
      This is rare but possible for high-functioning
      athletes in early Growth weeks.

    Execution Top 3 matches intake Top 3 but in different
    order:
      → ALIGNED. Ranking order differences within the
         same Top 3 are not significant enough to
         constitute a Gap. Stage 3 already has the
         per-bucket evidence tiers and can see shifts
         in relative emphasis.

================================================================
SECTION E: DOWNSTREAM ROUTING RULES
================================================================

STAGE 3: COACHING OUTPUT ENGINE

  PRIORITY ROUTING (modifies Tier 2):

    Status = "established" (5+ weeks):
      Tier 2 = execution-derived PPD Top 3 REPLACES
      static intake PPD.
      Coaching implications from execution-derived buckets
      inform the coaching direction when weekly signals
      (Tier 1) are not driving a specific emphasis.

    Status = "emerging" (3-4 weeks):
      Tier 2 = static intake PPD (UNCHANGED).
      Execution-derived data is too preliminary to redirect
      coaching priority.

    Status = "insufficient data" (< 3 weeks):
      Tier 2 = static intake PPD (UNCHANGED).

  TONE MODIFICATION (applies across all tiers):

    alignment_signal.overall = "Blind Spot":
      GROUNDING TONE. Introduce the unseen pattern
      behaviorally without naming the bucket or the
      comparison mechanism.
      Approach: "Something that's showing up in your
      weeks is..." or "The routine piece is more central
      than it might feel."
      Same coaching approach as Conflated in self_ratings —
      ground without shaming.

    alignment_signal.overall = "Gap":
      ELEVATION TONE. The athlete had some awareness but
      underestimated the area. Elevate the underestimated
      friction area.
      Approach: "That [behavioral pattern] you mentioned —
      it's showing up more than you might think."
      Same coaching approach as acknowledging partial
      awareness while directing attention.

    alignment_signal.overall = "Aligned":
      CONFIRMATION TONE. No adjustment needed. Continue
      current coaching direction.
      Coaching can reinforce the athlete's accurate self-
      understanding: their own read on their challenges
      matches what their weeks are showing.

    Status = "emerging":
      Tone modification available but SUBTLE only. No
      explicit grounding or elevation. At most, a slight
      lean in the direction the emerging signal suggests.
      Example: if Structure Gap is emerging as Strong,
      micro-commitment might lean toward routine/structure
      topics even though Tier 2 priority hasn't changed.

  UPDATED PRIORITY SYSTEM:

    Tier 1 (Always):    Weekly signals (coach flags, composite
                        score concern bands, weekly core_pattern)
    Tier 1.5:           Forward Anchor
    Tier 2 (Fallback):  PPD coaching implications
                        - Static intake PPD (default)
                        - Execution-derived PPD (when
                          status = "established")
    Tier 3 (Default):   Default coaching direction per
                        core_pattern
    + Tone Modification: PPD Alignment Signal
                        (Blind Spot / Gap / Aligned)
                        applies across all active tiers

  MICRO-COMMITMENT INTERACTION:
  When alignment_signal = "Blind Spot" and the blind spot
  bucket relates to a behavioral pattern the micro-commitment
  can address, the micro-commitment SHOULD target the blind
  spot area (subject to the existing micro-commitment
  modality adaptation rule in Section 10 of the Coaching
  Output Instructions). This is the primary mechanism for
  introducing the blind spot pattern to the athlete through
  action rather than explanation.

  Example (Tucker): Blind Spot = Structure Gap. Micro-
  commitment targets weekend routine: "Name two specific
  days you'll get to the gym during spring break. Tell
  someone." This addresses the blind spot without naming it.

STAGE 4: COACH INSIGHTS ENGINE

  ZERO PPD CONSUMPTION MAINTAINED.
  The longitudinal_ppd block is NOT consumed by Stage 4.
  PPD bucket names, alignment signal labels, evidence tiers,
  and all longitudinal PPD terminology remain prohibited.
  Stage 4 continues to surface the same behavioral patterns
  through composite scores and coach flags — different
  pathway, same coaching value. No compliance filter changes
  needed for Stage 4.

STAGE 5: EDITORIAL AUDIT

  Criterion 13 (Pipeline Data Leakage Compliance) expanded
  with Category F subcategories F6-F8 (see Section H).
  Stage 5 audits Stage 3 output for longitudinal PPD
  terminology leakage using the same scan process as
  existing Categories A-F.

      surfaced in any output.

    buckets have Weak-or-above evidence.

    - dropped_from_intake: array of bucket names in
      intake Top 3 but not execution Top 3

    Boolean. When true, all other fields are minimal
    (status = "insufficient data", weeks_accumulated = 0).

SECTION G: EDGE CASES
================================================================

EDGE CASE 1: FIRST WEEK (APP ATHLETE)
  weeks_accumulated = 1. Status = "insufficient data".
  No computation. Output = minimal block with
  weeks_accumulated = 1, core_foundation_fallback = false.

EDGE CASE 2: CORE FOUNDATION ATHLETE
  core_foundation_fallback = true. All fields minimal.
  Zero degradation to existing CF processing. CF athletes
  have no execution data — longitudinal PPD is an App-
  athlete feature only.

EDGE CASE 3: INJURY WEEK / UNUSUAL WEEK
  Injury, illness, or other disruption may temporarily
  shift execution patterns. A single injury week should
  not flip the alignment signal.
  Resolution: The rolling window naturally smooths single-
  week anomalies. Mergim's Week 14 (hamstring injury) would
  temporarily push some bucket tiers toward elevated evidence,
  but the predominant-tier calculation across 14 weeks would
  absorb it. No special handling needed beyond the rolling
  window design.

EDGE CASE 4: INSUFFICIENT DATA DESPITE SUFFICIENT WEEKS
  An athlete has 5+ weeks but all bucket evidence tiers are
  None or Context Insufficient (e.g., perfect execution,
  no adversity, off-season, minimal friction signals).
  Resolution: status = "insufficient data" despite
  weeks_accumulated >= 5. The status field reflects signal
  quality, not just time elapsed. alignment_signal is not
  populated. Stage 3 uses static intake PPD at Tier 2.

EDGE CASE 5: ALL 8 BUCKETS AT ZERO IN INTAKE
  If the athlete's ppd_all_scores has 0 for multiple
  buckets (possible if minimal Q15 selections and few
  amplifiers fire), any execution-derived evidence for
  those buckets would be classified as Blind Spot (intake
  score 0, execution evidence present).
  Resolution: Normal classification logic applies. However,
  intake scores of 0 on all non-selected buckets is the
  expected default — most athletes will have 5 buckets at
  0 (8 total minus 1-3 Q15 selections). The system should
  focus on the contrast between SELECTED buckets (3+
  intake score) and NON-SELECTED buckets (0-2 intake score)
  when determining Blind Spot vs Gap.

EDGE CASE 6: TIED EXECUTION EVIDENCE TIERS
  Multiple buckets have the same evidence tier (e.g.,
  3 buckets all at Moderate).
  Resolution: Break ties using bucket priority order (1→8),
  matching the intake PPD Layer 4 tie-breaker. Internal/
  cognitive buckets (1-5) prioritized over structural/
  external (6-8).

EDGE CASE 7: EXECUTION TOP 3 HAS FEWER THAN 3 BUCKETS
  Possible when only 1-2 buckets have Weak-or-above evidence.
  Resolution: Compare available execution entries against
  intake Top 3. Overlap calculated against available count,
  not always 3. Signal reliability is noted but classification
  still proceeds. Example: if only 1 bucket has evidence and
  it's in intake Top 3 → Aligned (limited data). If 1 bucket
  has evidence and it's NOT in intake Top 3 → Blind Spot or
  Gap depending on intake score.

EDGE CASE 8: SEASON PHASE CHANGES MID-WINDOW
  Athlete transitions from Off-Season to In-Season during
  the rolling window. Pressure Reactivity bucket was Context
  Insufficient in early weeks but becomes evaluable in later
  weeks.
  Resolution: Per Bucket 5 Season Phase Context Gate — use
  the most evaluable phase present. Evaluate Pressure
  Reactivity using only the In-Season weeks. Other buckets
  use all weeks in the window.

EDGE CASE 9: PRIOR WEEK JSON NOT AVAILABLE (GAP IN DATA)
  Program gaps (e.g., Tucker's potential spring break gap,
  Aidan's 10-week gap) reduce the rolling window's effective
  data points.
  Resolution: weeks_accumulated counts only weeks WITH data.
  A 10-week gap does not add 10 to weeks_accumulated. The
  rolling window uses all available JSONs in chronological
  order. If an athlete has 3 weeks of data spread across 8
  calendar weeks, status = "emerging" (3 weeks accumulated,
  not 8).

EDGE CASE 10: ALIGNMENT SIGNAL CHANGES OVER TIME
  An athlete starts as Aligned but execution shifts over
  months — a new friction area emerges that wasn't present
  at intake.
  Resolution: Normal behavior. The alignment signal updates
  weekly based on the rolling window. If the rolling window
  shows a new bucket becoming Moderate-then-Strong over
  weeks, the signal will shift from Aligned to Gap (or
  Blind Spot if intake score was low). This is the system
  working as intended — it detects emerging problems.

EDGE CASE 11: ATHLETE RETAKES INTAKE FORM
  If the app allows intake form re-administration (e.g.,
  at a new season or program re-enrollment), the baseline
  PPD resets.
  Resolution: Out of scope for this specification. If re-
  administration is supported, the new intake PPD becomes
  the comparison baseline, weeks_accumulated resets to 0,
  and the longitudinal PPD recalculates from the new
  baseline. Design decision deferred to app architecture.

EDGE CASE 12: COMPOUND_DISENGAGEMENT FLAG
  When compound_disengagement fires (3+ of 5 scores in worst
  band), multiple buckets may simultaneously show Strong
  evidence. This is expected and correct — a broadly
  disengaging athlete may genuinely have multiple active
  friction areas.
  Resolution: No special handling. All buckets classified
  independently. If 4+ buckets show Strong evidence, the
  execution Top 3 captures the most prominent (using bucket
  priority for ties). The Alignment Signal reflects the
  comparison against intake regardless of how many buckets
  are active.

SECTION K: DESIGN DECISIONS LOG
================================================================

All 9 design questions locked during collaborative session
on 2026-03-18.

DQ1: EXECUTION-TO-BUCKET MAPPING
  Decision: 8-bucket mapping using existing composite scores,
  coach flags, and v9.6 fields. No new weighted formulas.
  Season phase context gate for Pressure Reactivity (Bucket 5).
  Ecosystem Friction (Bucket 8) supplemented with content-
  derived indicators (stress_load, noise_fixation, outer-ring
  themes). See Section B for complete mapping.
  Rationale: Leverages existing computation. Maximizes coaching
  quality without over-engineering. Each bucket has at least
  one primary execution signal.

DQ2: COMPUTATION CADENCE
  Decision: Option (d) — Weekly computation with stabilized
  Alignment Signal. Rolling window across all available weeks
  produces naturally stable signal that doesn't flip-flop.
  Rationale: Matches self_ratings_alignment weekly cadence.
  Rolling window smooths single-week noise while providing
  fresh signal every week.

DQ3: MINIMUM DATA THRESHOLD
  Decision: Option (d) — Progressive threshold with two
  confidence tiers. Emerging at 3 weeks (inform-only, no
  routing change). Established at 5 weeks (full Alignment
  Signal, Tier 2 replacement enabled).
  Rationale: 3 weeks = minimum for pattern detection (Tucker's
  Sunday Missed was a pattern by Week 3). 5 weeks = sufficient
  for confident coaching redirection. Progressive approach
  prevents "6 weeks of misaligned coaching" problem while
  avoiding premature signal overreliance.

DQ4: SCORING APPROACH
  Decision: Option C — Hybrid with 5-tier evidence strength
  (Strong / Moderate / Weak / None / Context Insufficient).
  Deterministic classification rules per bucket using existing
  composite score bands, coach flags, and v9.6 fields.
  Rationale: Produces the best coaching output. Numeric 0-100
  scores (Option A) imply false precision. Binary present/
  absent (Option B) loses ranking capability needed for Blind
  Spot detection. 5-tier evidence strength enables ranking,
  Blind Spot detection, and appropriate coaching confidence
  without manufacturing false precision. No new formulas
  needed.

DQ5: ALIGNMENT SIGNAL CLASSIFICATION
  Decision: Top 3 comparison (not just #1 vs #1). Blind Spot =
  intake score ≤2 AND execution evidence Strong. Gap = intake
  score ≤2 AND execution Moderate, OR intake score 3-4 AND
  execution Strong/Moderate. No sub-states within Aligned.
  One Strong Blind Spot bucket overrides to overall Blind Spot
  even with 2/3 overlap. 4-step classification logic.
  Rationale: Top 3 comparison captures partial overlap (Tucker
  shares Discipline Gap with intake). Blind Spot vs Gap
  distinction based on intake score threshold enables
  appropriate coaching tone (introduce vs elevate). Override
  rule ensures a single significant blind spot is never masked.

DQ6: DOWNSTREAM ROUTING — PRIORITY POSITION
  Decision: Option (d) — Combination. Execution-derived PPD
  replaces static intake at Tier 2 (when Established) + PPD
  Alignment Signal tone modification across all tiers (Blind
  Spot = grounding, Gap = elevation, Aligned = confirmation).
  Forward Anchor unaffected at Tier 1.5.
  Rationale: Addresses both the priority question (WHAT to
  coach) and the tone question (HOW to frame it). Emerging
  tier = subtle tone only, no Tier 2 change. Established =
  full routing + tone.

DQ7: STAGE 4 ROUTING
  Decision: Option (a) — Maintain zero PPD consumption in
  Stage 4. Alignment Signal stays internal to Stage 2/3.
  Rationale: Stage 4 already surfaces the same behavioral
  patterns through composite scores and flags — different
  pathway, same coaching value. Surfacing "Self-Awareness:
  Divergent" in institutional reports creates curiosity gaps
  coaching staff cannot fill without accessing prohibited
  PPD data.

DQ8: EDITORIAL AUDIT EXPANSION
  Decision: Expand Category F with 3 new subcategories (F6:
  PPD Alignment Signal classifications, F7: Longitudinal PPD
  terminology, F8: Intake-to-execution comparison framing).
  Not a new Category H.
  Rationale: Thematically consistent with existing Category F
  (intake profile terminology). Same audit process — F6-F8
  are additional prohibited terms within Criterion 13, not
  new criteria.

DQ9: JSON SCHEMA PLACEMENT
  Decision: Option (d) — New top-level block `longitudinal_ppd`.
  Rationale: Semantically clear — distinct from intake baseline
  (baseline_intake_profile) and weekly execution signals
  (execution_behavior_signals). Avoids nesting derived data
  inside its own input source or inside a read-only pass-
  through block.
