Compliance determination: GREEN (low risk)
Given these constraints:
•	✅ Coaches see only aggregated insights & trends
•	✅ No access to athlete-written content
•	✅ No messaging or communication
•	✅ No task assignment or monitoring
•	✅ No usage visibility (time spent, completion pressure)
•	✅ Athlete input is private by default
👉 This does not create a countable athletically related activity and does not meaningfully interfere with voluntary status.
Bottom line:
This functions as a passive observational wellness / readiness signal, not a coaching activity.
From a compliance lens, it is closer to:
•	Wellness dashboards
•	Sleep / recovery summaries
•	Athlete services reports
—not practice, instruction, or required participation.

Why this design holds up under D1 rules
Here’s how compliance officers tend to think, and how your design answers each concern:
A) “Is this required?”
No.
Nothing is assigned, enforced, or followed up on by coaches.
Voluntary remains voluntary because there is no coach involvement in the activity itself.
________________________________________
B) “Does coach access turn it into CARA?”
No.
Because:
•	Coaches are not present
•	Coaches do not direct behavior
•	Coaches do not monitor compliance or time
•	Coaches do not interact with athletes via the system
They are viewing outcomes, not participation.
________________________________________
C) “Does this add extra time obligations?”
No.
Even if an athlete uses the system daily, there is:
•	No coach awareness of frequency
•	No expectation tied to team consequences
•	No reporting back to coaching staff
This is critical. The lack of usage visibility is one of your strongest compliance protections.
________________________________________
D) “Could this influence playing time decisions?”
This is where schools will be sensitive — and your design helps.
Because coaches only see:
•	Trend-level indicators
•	No raw data
•	No personal disclosures
…it becomes a contextual awareness tool, not an evaluation mechanism.
This should be explicitly stated in onboarding language:
“Insights are informational only and may not be used as a basis for participation, selection, or discipline.”
That sentence alone makes compliance departments breathe easier.

Compliance-safe metrics you can derive and show
Below is a safe metric taxonomy designed specifically to avoid CARA issues, FERPA overreach, and “coach-directed” interpretation.
🟢 Tier 1: Safest (Recommended defaults)
These are aggregate, trend-based, non-actionable metrics.
1) Mental Readiness Index (Trend)
•	7-day rolling average
•	Shown as ↑ ↓ → (not raw scores)
•	No timestamps
•	No daily values
Why safe:
It shows state, not behavior.
________________________________________
2) Consistency Signal (Non-usage-based)
•	Derived from internal pattern stability
•	Example labels:
o	Stable
o	Variable
o	Improving
Important:
Do not label this as “engagement” or “completion.”
Call it internal consistency, not participation.
________________________________________
3) Stress Load Indicator (Band-based)
•	Low / Moderate / Elevated
•	Based on athlete self-report patterns
•	No source attribution (no “because of exams”)
Why safe:
Similar to wellness questionnaires already used in many programs.
________________________________________
4) Confidence Momentum
•	Directional trend only
•	No absolute numbers
•	No comparison between athletes unless anonymized team averages
________________________________________
🟡 Tier 2: Safe with guardrails
These are fine if framed correctly and never used for enforcement.
5) Team-Level Averages (Anonymized)
•	“Team Readiness This Week”
•	Percentages only
•	Minimum group size thresholds (e.g., no breakdowns under 5 athletes)
________________________________________
6) Volatility Index
•	Measures emotional swings, not time spent
•	Useful for sports psych staff
•	Coaches see only high-level flags
Frame as:
“Stability indicator” — not “emotional control.”
________________________________________
🔴 Metrics to AVOID entirely
These raise compliance or cultural red flags:
•	❌ Time spent in app
•	❌ Streaks or completion counts
•	❌ Last login
•	❌ Daily compliance scores
•	❌ Rankings between athletes
•	❌ “At-risk” labels tied to individuals
•	❌ Any metric implying effort or discipline
Even if technically legal, these invite misuse.

Language matters more than people think
What you call things is as important as what they are.
Use language like:
•	“Indicators”
•	“Signals”
•	“Trends”
•	“Context”
•	“Informational only”
Avoid language like:
•	Accountability
•	Compliance
•	Adherence
•	Tracking
•	Monitoring
