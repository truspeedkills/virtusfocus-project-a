VF Tracking & Reporting Metrics Reference

Win the Day Weekly Interpretation:
28–35 (Growth Week) – Strong routine consistency and self-leadership.
14–27 (Mixed Week) – Inconsistent routine execution.
0–13 (Reset Week) – Significant inconsistency; recommitment required.

Daily Category Meaning:
4–5 Points – Win the Day (strong execution).
2–3 Points – Neutral Day (partial follow-through).
0–1 Points – Missed the Mark (routine breakdown).

Coach-Safe Reporting Indicators:
Engagement consistency (Win/Neutral/Missed distribution).
Recommitment signal (response after missed day).
Routine stability (range of category distribution).
Controllable focus quality (Bullseye usage).

Approved Metric Taxonomy (v1.1):

1. Mental Readiness Indicator (Trend Only)
   - Directional (↑ ↓ →)
   - No raw daily values

2. Consistency Signal
   - Stable / Variable / Improving

3. Stress Load Band
   - Low / Moderate / Elevated
   - No source attribution

4. Confidence Momentum (Directional)
   - ↑ Building / → Stable / ↓ Variable

5. Focus Distribution (Bullseye Trend)
   - Center-Dominant / Mixed Focus / Outer-Ring Drift Detected

6. Recommitment Strength
   - Strong / Moderate / Low

7. Growth Phase Status
   - Emerging / Developing / Consistent / Leadership

8. Composite Readiness Signal
   - ↑ Positive (Green) / → Steady (Yellow) / ↓ Attention (Orange)
   - Synthesizes weekly tier, consistency, recommitment, focus,
     and stress load into single directional indicator

9. Pattern Stability Indicator (Longitudinal)
   - Predictable / Transitional / Fluctuating
   - Requires 4+ weeks of data
   - Measures cross-week classification predictability

10. Response Recovery Indicator (Longitudinal)
    - Rapid / Moderate / Extended / No Recovery Data
    - Requires 3+ weeks with at least one non-Growth week
    - Measures return-to-Growth speed after Mixed/Reset

All metrics must be trend-based, non-punitive, and informational.

Compliance Reminder:
All reporting must remain performance-based and non-clinical.
Do not include psychological diagnoses or mental health language.
