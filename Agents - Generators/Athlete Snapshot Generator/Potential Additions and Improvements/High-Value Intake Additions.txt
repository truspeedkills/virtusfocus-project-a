High-Value Intake Questions to Deepen the Athlete Snapshot
1. Ownership & Accountability

Purpose: Identify how self-led the athlete currently is.

VirtusFocus is built around athlete ownership and identity-driven habits 

virtusfocus_offers

, so these questions reveal whether the athlete already operates that way.

Suggested Questions

1.1 Who currently drives your development the most?

Me

My coach

My parents

A mix of all three

Why it matters

Identifies athlete-led vs externally driven growth.

Determines how much ownership coaching is needed.

How it informs Tune-Ups

Low ownership → Daily prompts focus on self-leadership.

High ownership → Emphasize optimization and refinement.

1.2 How often do you review your own performance after practices or games?

Scale:

Never

Sometimes

Usually

Every time

Why it matters
Shows whether the athlete naturally reflects on performance.

Coaching Use

Low reflection → Weekly check-ins focus on awareness and review habits.

1.3 When something goes wrong in competition, what do you usually do first?

Options:

Blame circumstances

Get frustrated

Try to adjust immediately

Reset and refocus

Why it matters
Reveals response vs reaction patterns.

Coaching Use
Directly informs resilience training and Bullseye reset strategies.

2. Pressure & Composure

Purpose: Identify overthinking triggers and reset speed.

Suggested Questions

2.1 What situation makes you overthink the most?

Examples:

Big competitions

After a mistake

When a coach is watching

When I’m trying to prove myself

Why it matters
Pinpoints specific pressure triggers.

Coaching Use
Daily prompts can simulate those moments.

2.2 After a mistake, how long does it usually take you to mentally reset?

Scale:

Immediately

Within the next play/rep

Several minutes

It sticks with me the rest of the game

Why it matters
Quantifies resilience speed.

Coaching Use

Slow reset → Mistake recovery routines become priority.

2.3 What thought shows up most often when you feel pressure?

Examples:

“Don’t mess this up.”

“I need to prove myself.”

“What if I fail?”

Why it matters
Captures the athlete’s pressure narrative.

Coaching Use
Allows precise mental reframing prompts.

Suggested Questions

3.1 Complete this sentence:

3.2 After a bad performance, how do you usually describe yourself?

I get frustrated but move forward

I analyze and adjust

Why it matters

Coaching Use
Guides identity reinforcement messaging.

3.3 Do you see yourself as a leader on your team?

Scale:

Sometimes

Yes

One of the main leaders

Why it matters
Identifies leadership development stage.

Coaching Use
Higher leadership → leadership prompts and culture building.
