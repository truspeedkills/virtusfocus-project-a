Goal: Maximum insight with minimal friction
Target: 10–15 questions total
Time to complete: 4–6 minutes

This aligns with VirtusFocus principles:

Simple → Repeatable → Reliable

SECTION 2 — Performance Identity

Purpose: Understand how the athlete sees themselves.

VirtusFocus is identity-driven, so this section reveals their current internal narrative.

Questions

Purpose: Identify the primary problem VirtusFocus must solve.

Now you have the trigger pattern.

SECTION 4 — Pressure Response

Purpose: Measure composure and resilience.

VirtusFocus teaches Response > Reaction.

Options:

1) VirtusFocus Primary Problem Detector (PPD)

Purpose: Automatically identify an athlete’s Top 3 “Primary Problems” VirtusFocus should solve first.

Inputs (from redesigned intake)

Performance Friction (Select up to 3)
Options:

Low confidence

Focus/distractions

Pressure in big moments

Motivation/discipline

Lack of routine/structure

Coach/parent pressure (ecosystem)

Trigger Context (select 1–2)

Big moments / high stakes

After mistakes

When being evaluated (coach/scouts)

When fatigued / busy life weeks

When expectations are high

Reset Speed after mistake

Immediate

Next rep/play

Few minutes

Sticks most of game

Pressure Thought (short text)
(Used for classification, not “scored” heavily.)

Routine Presence

None

Somewhat

Consistent

Problem Buckets (what PPD outputs)

PPD outputs a ranked list of these 8 Primary Problems:

Overthinking Loop

Confidence Volatility (confidence tied to outcomes)

Mistake Recovery Lag

Focus Drift / Distraction

Pressure Reactivity (stakes change behavior)

Discipline Gap (motivation-based, inconsistent habits)

Structure Gap (no routine; unclear prep)

Ecosystem Friction (parent/coach stress affecting mindset)

Scoring Logic (simple + explainable)
A) Start with “Selection Weight” (high signal)

For each option the athlete selects in Performance Friction:

Selected = +3 to the matching bucket
(If athlete selects 3 items, those 3 buckets each start at +3.)

B) Add “Amplifiers” (small boosts that sharpen ranking)

Reset Speed Amplifier (Mistake Recovery Lag bucket)

Immediate = +0

Next rep/play = +1

Few minutes = +2

Sticks most of game = +3

Routine Amplifier (Structure Gap bucket)

Consistent = +0

Somewhat = +1

None = +3

Trigger Amplifier (context increases specificity)

After mistakes → +2 to Mistake Recovery Lag, +1 to Confidence Volatility

Big moments → +2 to Pressure Reactivity, +1 to Overthinking Loop

Being evaluated → +2 to Overthinking Loop, +1 to Confidence Volatility

Fatigued/busy weeks → +2 to Discipline Gap, +1 to Focus Drift

Expectations high → +2 to Pressure Reactivity, +1 to Confidence Volatility

Ecosystem Amplifier
If athlete indicates home conversations are advice-heavy/pressure:

+3 to Ecosystem Friction

C) Tie-breaker (when scores tie)

Use the Pressure Thought text to tag the loop:

“Don’t mess up / what if I fail / I have to…” → Overthinking Loop +1, Pressure Reactivity +1

“I need to prove…” → Confidence Volatility +1

“I can’t let them down…” → Pressure Reactivity +1, Ecosystem Friction +1

PPD Output Format (what the system returns)

Top 3 Primary Problems with:

Rank

Score

Trigger context

1-line coaching implication (for Tune-Ups / Weekly)

Example output:

1) Mistake Recovery Lag (9) — Trigger: after mistakes — Tune-Ups: reset reps + “next-play” language; Weekly: track reset speed.
2) Overthinking Loop (7) — Trigger: big moments — Tune-Ups: 1-controllable Bullseye target; Weekly: reduce thought clutter.
3) Structure Gap (6) — Trigger: busy weeks — Tune-Ups: 3-step pre-performance routine; Weekly: routine adherence.

Why this works for VirtusFocus

Uses only self-report but produces a real diagnosis.

Stays simple → repeatable → reliable (no complex psych labeling).

Drives immediate personalization of:

Daily prompts

Weekly reflection focus

Snapshot “Known Derailers” + “Initial Coaching Emphasis”

2) Athlete Baseline Index (ABI)

Purpose: A fast baseline score across four pillars so you can:

anchor the Athlete Snapshot baseline

track change over weeks/months

personalize coaching emphasis immediately

ABI Dimensions (4)

Each scored 0–10, total out of 40.

Ownership

Composure (pressure + reset)

Focus

Structure

ABI Inputs (minimal additions to intake)

Add these quick scales (1–5 each):

Ownership

“Who drives your development?”
Me (5) / Mix (3) / Coach-parent led (1)

“How often do you review your performance?”
Every time (5) → Never (1)

Composure

Reset Speed after mistake
Immediate (5) → Sticks all game (1)

“How much do big moments change your decision-making?”
Not at all (5) → A lot (1)

Focus

“During performance, how often do you lose your focus to thoughts/externals?”
Rarely (5) → Constantly (1)

“Do distractions (phone/social/life stress) affect training consistency?”
Rarely (5) → Often (1)

Structure

“Do you have a pre-performance routine?”
Consistent (5) → None (1)

“How consistent is your weekly training + recovery rhythm?”
Consistent (5) → Inconsistent (1)

Each dimension = average of its two items (1–5), then multiply by 2 to convert to 0–10.

ABI Scoring

For each dimension:

Dimension Score (0–10) = round( (Q1 + Q2) / 2 * 2 )

Total ABI = sum of 4 dimensions (0–40)

ABI Bands (optional, useful for Growth Phase support)

0–16: Needs foundation (high variance, low structure)

17–28: Developing (some strengths, inconsistent execution)

29–36: Consistent (reliable habits, stable mindset)

37–40: Leadership (self-led + composed + consistent)

(These bands should support, not replace, the Snapshot Growth Phase classification.)

ABI Output Format (Snapshot-ready)

Return:

Ownership: X/10

Composure: X/10

Focus: X/10

Structure: X/10

Total: X/40

Primary Emphasis: lowest 1–2 dimensions

Coaching Strategy: 1 line per emphasis

Example:

Ownership 6 | Composure 4 | Focus 7 | Structure 3 | Total 20/40
Primary Emphasis: Structure + Composure
Coaching Strategy:

Structure: build a 3-step routine + daily “Win the Day” minimums

Composure: reset language + Bullseye single target under pressure

How ABI improves Daily Tune-Ups + Weekly Check-Ins

Daily Tune-Ups can be dimension-targeted:

Low composure → reset prompt + controllable target

Low structure → micro-routine + streak reinforcement

Weekly Check-Ins can track movement:

“Reset speed improved from 2 → 4”

“Routine consistency improved from 1 → 3”

Snapshot becomes measurable, not just descriptive.
