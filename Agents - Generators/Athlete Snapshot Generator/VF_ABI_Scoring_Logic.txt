VIRTUSFOCUS — ATHLETE BASELINE INDEX (ABI) SCORING LOGIC
(Approved 2026-03-10 — Pipeline Improvement Task 3 Complete)

PURPOSE
These rules define the deterministic scoring logic for the
Athlete Baseline Index (ABI). The ABI produces a quantitative
developmental baseline across 4 pillars from the App intake
form, giving the pipeline an immediate structured measure of
where the athlete stands at entry.

The ABI is computed ONCE at intake from 8 self-assessment
questions (Q7-Q14, Section 2 of the App intake form). It
captures the athlete's SELF-ASSESSED capability across 4
dimensions. It is complementary to the PPD (Primary Problem
Detector), which captures self-reported FRICTION. An athlete
can report high friction in an area (PPD) while self-assessing
moderate capability there (ABI), or vice versa. The two systems
together produce a richer intake profile than either alone.

Core Foundation athletes: ABI is an App-athlete feature only.
Core Foundation athletes do not complete the App intake form
and have no ABI output. The Snapshot Builder uses narrative-
only Growth Phase classification for Core Foundation athletes
(unchanged).

============================================================
PILLAR DEFINITIONS
============================================================

4 pillars. Each measures a distinct developmental dimension.

1. OWNERSHIP
   Degree to which the athlete self-directs their development
   and engages in reflective performance habits. Measures
   internal locus of control over growth.

2. COMPOSURE
   Ability to maintain mental stability under pressure and
   recover from mistakes during performance. Measures
   emotional regulation in competitive contexts.

3. FOCUS
   Ability to sustain attention during performance and resist
   external distractions that disrupt training quality.
   Measures attentional control and distraction resistance.

4. STRUCTURE
   Consistency of pre-performance routines and weekly training
   rhythms. Measures the presence and reliability of systematic
   mental preparation frameworks.

============================================================
INPUTS (8 QUESTIONS — Q7-Q14)
============================================================

All inputs are 1-5 scales from Section 2 of the App intake
form (VF_App_Intake_Form_Specification.txt). All scales run
1 (low/negative) to 5 (high/positive).

--- OWNERSHIP ---

Q7: "How much of your development do you currently drive
     yourself?"
     Anchors: 1 = Others drive most of it /
              5 = I drive almost all of it
     Field: abi_ownership_q1

Q8: "How often do you review your own performance after
     practices or games?"
     Anchors: 1 = Never / 5 = Every time
     Field: abi_ownership_q2

--- COMPOSURE ---

Q9: "After a mistake, how quickly do you mentally reset?"
     Anchors: 1 = It sticks with me for a while /
              5 = I reset almost immediately
     Field: abi_composure_q1
     Note: Also serves as PPD Reset Speed Amplifier
           (Mistake Recovery Lag bucket). See
           VF_PPD_Scoring_Logic.txt Layer 2A.

Q10: "How well do you keep your composure during big moments?"
     Anchors: 1 = I struggle a lot /
              5 = I stay very composed
     Field: abi_composure_q2

--- FOCUS ---

Q11: "During performance, how well do you maintain your focus?"
     Anchors: 1 = I lose focus very often /
              5 = I rarely lose focus
     Field: abi_focus_q1

Q12: "How much do outside distractions (phone, social media,
      life stress) affect your training?"
     Anchors: 1 = They affect me a lot /
              5 = They rarely affect me
     Field: abi_focus_q2

--- STRUCTURE ---

Q13: "How consistent is your pre-performance mental routine?"
     Anchors: 1 = I don't have one /
              5 = Very consistent
     Field: abi_structure_q1
     Note: Also serves as PPD Routine Amplifier (Structure
           Gap bucket). See VF_PPD_Scoring_Logic.txt Layer 2B.

Q14: "How consistent is your weekly training and recovery
      rhythm?"
     Anchors: 1 = Very inconsistent /
              5 = Very consistent
     Field: abi_structure_q2

============================================================
FORMULA
============================================================

Step 1: Compute each pillar score.

  pillar_score = round((Q_a + Q_b) / 2 * 2)

  Where Q_a and Q_b are the two 1-5 scale inputs for that
  pillar.

Step 2: Sum all 4 pillar scores for total ABI.

  total_abi = ownership + composure + focus + structure

RANGE:
  Per-pillar: 2-10 (integer, no rounding ambiguity)
  Total: 8-40

The formula averages the two questions, then multiplies by 2
to scale to a 2-10 range. Every possible input combination
produces a clean integer:

  (1+1)/2*2 = 2    (1+2)/2*2 = 3    (2+2)/2*2 = 4
  (2+3)/2*2 = 5    (3+3)/2*2 = 6    (3+4)/2*2 = 7
  (4+4)/2*2 = 8    (4+5)/2*2 = 9    (5+5)/2*2 = 10

Note: The range is 2-10, not 0-10. The formula cannot produce
values below 2 because the minimum input value is 1 (not 0).
This is by design — the 1-5 scale captures meaningful
differentiation across the full athlete population, and the
2-10 output range accurately reflects the possible input space.

============================================================
TOTAL ABI BAND THRESHOLDS
============================================================

  8-16:   Needs Foundation
          High variance, low structure, limited self-direction.
          Most pillars in Low band.

  17-28:  Developing
          Some strengths, inconsistent execution. Most intake
          athletes land here. Wide range is intentional — this
          is the developmental band where coaching has the
          highest marginal impact.

  29-36:  Consistent
          Reliable habits, stable mindset. Most pillars in
          Moderate-High range. Strong self-assessment across
          dimensions.

  37-40:  Leadership
          Self-led, composed, focused, structured. Near-max
          across all pillars. Very rare at intake.

============================================================
INDIVIDUAL PILLAR BAND THRESHOLDS
============================================================

Each pillar has its own band label in addition to the total
ABI band.

  2-4:   Low
         Both questions at 1-2, or one at 1 and one at 3.
         Significant developmental gap in this dimension.

  5-7:   Moderate
         Average self-assessment. Developing capability with
         room for growth. Most common band at intake.

  8-10:  High
         Both questions at 4-5. Strong self-assessed capability
         in this dimension.

Individual pillar bands support:
- Primary Emphasis determination (band context alongside score)
- Dashboard visualization (color-coded per pillar)
- Downstream coaching personalization (Stage 3 tone calibration)
- Longitudinal tracking (pillar band movement over time)

============================================================
PRIMARY EMPHASIS DETERMINATION RULES
============================================================

Primary Emphasis identifies the 1-2 pillars where coaching
should focus first. It is a deterministic output derived from
pillar scores.

RULES:

1. Rank all 4 pillars by score in ascending order.

2. The lowest-scoring pillar is ALWAYS Primary Emphasis #1.

3. If the 2nd-lowest pillar is within 2 points of the lowest,
   it becomes Primary Emphasis #2.

4. MAXIMUM 2 emphases. Never 3 or 4, regardless of score
   distribution.

5. When 3 or more pillars tie for lowest, take the first 2 by
   PILLAR PRIORITY ORDER:

   Priority 1: Ownership
   Priority 2: Composure
   Priority 3: Focus
   Priority 4: Structure

   Rationale: Internal capabilities (ownership, composure) are
   higher-leverage coaching targets than structural dimensions
   (focus, structure) when all else is equal. This matches the
   PPD bucket priority logic (internal/cognitive before
   structural/external).

6. If all 4 pillars are exactly equal:
   primary_emphasis = ["Balanced"]

   "Balanced" indicates no specific pillar needs emphasis —
   the athlete's self-assessment is uniformly distributed. The
   total ABI band still tells the coaching story (all at 3 =
   total 12 = Needs Foundation across the board; all at 6 =
   total 24 = Developing uniformly). The Snapshot Builder
   interprets "Balanced" accordingly.

EXAMPLES:

  Scores: Own=6, Com=5, Foc=8, Str=7
  Lowest: Composure (5). 2nd: Ownership (6), gap=1. <=2.
  Result: ["Composure", "Ownership"]

  Scores: Own=5, Com=6, Foc=6, Str=4
  Lowest: Structure (4). 2nd: Ownership (5), gap=1. <=2.
  Result: ["Structure", "Ownership"]

  Scores: Own=7, Com=4, Foc=6, Str=7
  Lowest: Composure (4). 2nd: Focus (6), gap=2. <=2.
  Result: ["Composure", "Focus"]

  Scores: Own=8, Com=4, Foc=8, Str=9
  Lowest: Composure (4). 2nd: Own/Foc tied at 8, gap=4. >2.
  Result: ["Composure"]

  Scores: Own=6, Com=6, Foc=6, Str=6
  All equal.
  Result: ["Balanced"]

  Scores: Own=4, Com=4, Foc=4, Str=8
  Three-way tie at 4. Pillar priority: Own > Com > Foc.
  Result: ["Ownership", "Composure"]

============================================================
ABI-GROWTH PHASE RELATIONSHIP
============================================================

RULE: Soft Input.

The ABI band INFORMS the Snapshot Builder's Growth Phase
classification but does NOT constrain it.

The Snapshot Builder should reference the ABI band when
justifying its Growth Phase classification, but is not bound
by it. The agent can classify an athlete as Consistent even
with a Developing ABI band if the narrative evidence warrants
it (and vice versa).

RATIONALE:
- Growth Phase is fundamentally behavioral (demonstrated
  execution patterns over time), not self-assessment-based.
- ABI is a one-time self-report snapshot. Athletes regularly
  over-rate or under-rate themselves (e.g., Tucker Lloyd's
  8/10 mental game self-rating despite clear structural gaps).
- The existing pipeline uses deterministic behavioral
  thresholds for Growth Phase progression (VF_Interpretation_
  JSON_Rules.txt Section 4C). Weekly data overrides intake
  classification within the first 2+ weeks.
- Consistency with pipeline philosophy: intake captures
  perception, execution data captures reality.

EXAMPLE USAGE:

  ABI band = Needs Foundation, but athlete has Approach-framed
  pressure thought, high commitment ("All-in"), and strong
  narrative self-awareness.
  -> Snapshot Builder may classify as Developing, noting the
     ABI band in the justification: "Despite a Needs Foundation
     ABI score, the athlete's commitment level and self-
     awareness position them at the Developing threshold."

  ABI band = Consistent, but narrative shows significant
  identity friction, avoidance-framed pressure thought, and
  limited self-direction.
  -> Snapshot Builder may classify as Developing, noting the
     ABI band divergence: "The athlete's Consistent ABI score
     reflects self-assessed capability that is not yet matched
     by demonstrated behavioral patterns."

============================================================
OUTPUT FORMAT
============================================================

The ABI produces 2 output objects stored in the athlete
snapshot:

------------------------------------------------------------
abi_scores (primary output)
------------------------------------------------------------

  {
    "ownership": 6,
    "composure": 5,
    "focus": 8,
    "structure": 7,
    "total": 26,
    "band": "Developing",
    "primary_emphasis": ["Composure", "Ownership"],
    "pillar_bands": {
      "ownership": "Moderate",
      "composure": "Moderate",
      "focus": "High",
      "structure": "Moderate"
    }
  }

Field definitions:
- ownership / composure / focus / structure: Integer 2-10.
  Individual pillar scores.
- total: Integer 8-40. Sum of 4 pillar scores.
- band: String. Total ABI band classification.
  Valid values: "Needs Foundation" | "Developing" |
                "Consistent" | "Leadership"
- primary_emphasis: Array of 1-2 strings (pillar names),
  or ["Balanced"] if all 4 pillars are equal.
  Valid pillar values: "Ownership" | "Composure" | "Focus" |
                       "Structure"
- pillar_bands: Object with 4 keys. Individual pillar band
  classifications.
  Valid values per pillar: "Low" | "Moderate" | "High"

------------------------------------------------------------
abi_raw_inputs (audit trail)
------------------------------------------------------------

  {
    "ownership_q1": 3,
    "ownership_q2": 3,
    "composure_q1": 3,
    "composure_q2": 2,
    "focus_q1": 4,
    "focus_q2": 4,
    "structure_q1": 3,
    "structure_q2": 4
  }

All values are integers 1-5, stored exactly as the athlete
answered them on the intake form.

Purpose:
- Re-administration comparison (longitudinal ABI tracking —
  compare intake answers to future re-assessment)
- Audit trail (verify pillar score computation)
- PPD cross-reference (composure_q1 = Q9 Reset Speed
  Amplifier; structure_q1 = Q13 Routine Amplifier)

------------------------------------------------------------
Coaching strategy lines
------------------------------------------------------------

Coaching strategy lines are NOT part of the ABI output. The
ABI produces clean data (scores, bands, emphasis). The
Snapshot Builder (Task 5) synthesizes coaching emphasis from
PPD + ABI + narrative data — that is where strategy lines
belong.

============================================================
EDGE CASES
============================================================

1. ALL PILLARS EQUAL:
   If all 4 pillar scores are identical, primary_emphasis =
   ["Balanced"]. The total ABI band still communicates the
   developmental level. Example: all 4 at 3 = total 12 =
   Needs Foundation; all 4 at 6 = total 24 = Developing.

2. THREE-WAY TIE FOR LOWEST:
   When 3 pillars tie for lowest, take the first 2 by pillar
   priority order (Ownership > Composure > Focus > Structure).
   Example: Own=4, Com=4, Foc=4, Str=8 -> emphasis =
   ["Ownership", "Composure"].

3. BOUNDARY-EXACT 2ND EMPHASIS:
   When the 2nd-lowest pillar is exactly 2 points above the
   lowest, it IS included. The threshold is "within 2 points"
   inclusive. Example: lowest=4, 2nd=6 -> included.

4. MAXIMUM EMPHASIS CAP:
   Even if 3+ pillars are within 2 points of the lowest, only
   2 emphases are generated. The 2 lowest-scoring pillars are
   selected; ties broken by pillar priority order.

5. ALL QUESTIONS MINIMUM (1,1,1,1,1,1,1,1):
   Every pillar = round((1+1)/2*2) = 2. Total = 8.
   Band = Needs Foundation. Emphasis = ["Balanced"].
   Pillar bands = all Low.

6. ALL QUESTIONS MAXIMUM (5,5,5,5,5,5,5,5):
   Every pillar = round((5+5)/2*2) = 10. Total = 40.
   Band = Leadership. Emphasis = ["Balanced"].
   Pillar bands = all High.

7. EXTREME ASYMMETRY:
   Athlete answers 5,5 on one pillar and 1,1 on another.
   Example: Own=10, Com=2, Foc=10, Str=2. Total = 24 =
   Developing. But the profile is highly polarized. Primary
   Emphasis = ["Composure", "Structure"] (both at 2, first 2
   by pillar priority: Composure > Structure). The Snapshot
   Builder should note the asymmetry narratively.

8. PPD-ABI CONTRADICTION:
   When PPD and ABI produce seemingly contradictory results
   (e.g., PPD shows no Overthinking but ABI Composure is Low),
   both are stored as-is. The contradiction itself is
   informative — it reveals a gap between perceived friction
   (what gets in the way) and self-assessed capability (how
   strong is this skill). The Snapshot Builder may note the
   divergence narratively. The future PPD Alignment Signal will
   surface these gaps systematically.

9. FORM VALIDATION:
   All 8 questions (Q7-Q14) are required fields. The app must
   enforce 1-5 integer responses for all. If the form
   submission somehow arrives with missing or out-of-range
   values, the ABI computation flags an error and the Snapshot
   Builder must prompt for re-entry. ABI cannot be computed
   with incomplete inputs.

------------------------------------------------------------

reactive. Primary derailer = loss of confidence following
errors. Hinge habit = immediate reset and refocus after
mistakes. 6/10 mental game self-rating.

  Q7  = 3 (moderate self-drive — committed but development
         partly coach-directed)
  Q8  = 3 (notices outcomes but no systematic self-review)
  Q9  = 3 (locked from PPD — moderate reset)
  Q10 = 2 (struggles in big moments — primary derailer fires
         under pressure, Week 3 High EI)
  Q11 = 4 (focus not a primary issue — 35/35 Win days)
  Q12 = 4 (external distractions not identified as an issue)
  Q13 = 3 (locked from PPD — somewhat consistent routine)
  Q14 = 4 (35/35 Win days, very consistent weekly engagement)

  Total: 6 + 5 + 8 + 7 = 26 -> Developing

  Lowest: Composure (5)

  primary_emphasis = ["Composure", "Ownership"]

  reinforces the composure signal. Ownership as 2nd ABI
  emphasis adds information PPD does not capture — Grace is
  not yet fully self-directing her development. Focus is High

============================================================
DOWNSTREAM CONSUMPTION
============================================================

The ABI output is consumed by:

1. SNAPSHOT BUILDER (Stage 1):
   - abi_scores informs Growth Phase classification (soft
     input — informs but does not constrain)
   - primary_emphasis identifies initial coaching focus areas
   - pillar_bands provide structured context for coaching
     priority narratives
   - abi_raw_inputs stored for longitudinal comparison

2. INTERPRETATION ENGINE (Stage 2):
   - abi_scores stored as baseline reference fields in the
     athlete's first weekly JSON
   - Enables future longitudinal ABI comparison when re-
     assessment is implemented
   - Pillar bands inform baseline context for coaching output
     personalization

3. COACHING OUTPUT ENGINE (Stage 3):
   - primary_emphasis informs coaching language selection and
     thematic emphasis
   - pillar_bands inform tone calibration (Low band pillar =
     more foundational coaching language; High band pillar =
     reinforcement language)

4. FUTURE — LONGITUDINAL ABI:
   - abi_raw_inputs enables re-administration comparison
     (same 8 questions re-administered periodically)
   - Pillar score movement over time becomes a developmental
     tracking metric
   - Combined with PPD Alignment Signal for comprehensive
     perception-vs-reality analysis
