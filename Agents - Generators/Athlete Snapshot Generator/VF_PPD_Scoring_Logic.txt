VIRTUSFOCUS — PRIMARY PROBLEM DETECTOR (PPD) SCORING LOGIC
(Approved 2026-03-10 — Pipeline Improvement Task 2 Complete)

PURPOSE
These rules define the deterministic scoring logic for the
Primary Problem Detector (PPD). The PPD identifies an athlete's
Top 3 self-reported performance friction areas from 8 predefined
buckets using a 4-layer scoring system: selection weight,
amplifiers, tie-breaker classification, and final ranking.

All scoring layers are fully deterministic except the tie-breaker
(Layer 3), which requires agent classification of free-text input.
Tie-breaker classification follows explicit categorical rules with
dual classification allowed.

The PPD is computed ONCE at intake from the App intake form. It
captures what the athlete THINKS their primary problems are.
A future execution-derived Longitudinal PPD (computed periodically
from accumulated pipeline data) will capture what the DATA SHOWS
their actual problems are. The gap between these two produces the
PPD Alignment Signal (Problem Awareness Aligned / Problem Awareness
Gap / Blind Spot). The output format below is designed to support
this future comparison.

Core Foundation athletes: PPD is an App-athlete feature only.
Core Foundation athletes do not complete the App intake form and
have no PPD output. The Snapshot Builder uses narrative-only
derailer identification for Core Foundation athletes (unchanged).

============================================================
PPD BUCKET DEFINITIONS
============================================================

8 buckets, fixed order (this order is also the tie-breaking
priority — lower number wins ties):

1. Overthinking Loop
   Excessive mental rehearsal of negative scenarios before or
   during performance. Rumination that displaces execution focus.

2. Confidence Volatility
   Confidence tied to outcomes rather than preparation. Rises
   after success, drops after failure. Unstable self-belief.

3. Mistake Recovery Lag
   Slow or incomplete mental reset after errors. Mistakes carry
   forward and compound across subsequent performance moments.

4. Focus Drift / Distraction
   Attention pulled away from controllables during performance
   or training. External distractions (phone, social media,
   life stress) disrupt engagement quality.

5. Pressure Reactivity
   Behavioral or mental performance changes under high-stakes
   conditions. Big moments, evaluation, or external expectations
   alter execution patterns.

6. Discipline Gap
   Inconsistent motivation or effort. Habits fluctuate based on
   mood, outcomes, or life circumstances. Commitment without
   consistent follow-through.

7. Structure Gap
   Absence or inconsistency of pre-performance routines, daily
   rhythms, or training structure. No systematic mental
   preparation framework.

8. Ecosystem Friction
   External pressure from coaches, parents, or environment that
   destabilizes the athlete's mental performance. Obligation-
   driven stress from relationships within the performance
   ecosystem.

============================================================
INPUTS (6 QUESTIONS)
============================================================

1. Q15 — performance_friction_selections (multi-select, 1-3)
   Role: Selection Weight (primary PPD input)
   8 options, one per bucket. Each selection = +3 to matching bucket.

2. Q16 — trigger_context_selections (multi-select, up to 2)
   Role: Trigger Amplifier
   5 options. Each adds +2 to a primary bucket and +1 to a
   secondary bucket.

3. Q9 — abi_composure_q1 (1-5 scale)
   Role: Reset Speed Amplifier (Mistake Recovery Lag bucket only)
   "After a mistake, how quickly do you mentally reset?"
   1 = sticks with me, 5 = reset almost immediately

4. Q13 — abi_structure_q1 (1-5 scale)
   Role: Routine Amplifier (Structure Gap bucket only)
   "How consistent is your pre-performance mental routine?"
   1 = no routine, 5 = very consistent

5. Q18 — pressure_thought_text (free text)
   Role: Tie-breaker classification
   "Before a big moment, what thought shows up most often?"

6. Q23 — home_conversation_pattern (single-select, 4 options)
   Role: Ecosystem Amplifier (Ecosystem Friction bucket only)
   "After games or competitions, conversations at home are usually:"

============================================================
LAYER 1: SELECTION WEIGHT (Q15)
============================================================

Each friction selection adds +3 to the corresponding bucket.
Minimum 1 selection required. Maximum 3 selections allowed.
The app UI must enforce the 1-3 constraint.

Mapping:
  "Overthinking"                         -> +3 Overthinking Loop
  "Confidence going up and down"         -> +3 Confidence Volatility
  "Hard time letting go of mistakes"     -> +3 Mistake Recovery Lag
  "Losing focus or getting distracted"   -> +3 Focus Drift / Distraction
  "Pressure in big moments"             -> +3 Pressure Reactivity
  "Staying motivated or disciplined"     -> +3 Discipline Gap
  "Lack of routine or structure"         -> +3 Structure Gap
  "Pressure from coaches or parents"     -> +3 Ecosystem Friction

There is no "Other" option. The 8 options map 1-to-1 with the
8 PPD buckets. This constraint ensures deterministic scoring.

============================================================
LAYER 2: AMPLIFIERS (Q9, Q13, Q16, Q23)
============================================================

Amplifiers add small weights that sharpen the ranking beyond
the selection weight. Each amplifier targets specific bucket(s).

------------------------------------------------------------
2A. RESET SPEED AMPLIFIER (Q9 -> Mistake Recovery Lag)
------------------------------------------------------------

Q9 measures reset speed on a 1-5 scale. Low values = slow reset
= higher amplification of the Mistake Recovery Lag bucket.
Scale is inverted for amplifier mapping.

  Q9 = 5 (reset almost immediately)  -> +0
  Q9 = 4 (reset quickly)             -> +1
  Q9 = 3 (moderate reset)            -> +1
  Q9 = 2 (takes a while)             -> +2
  Q9 = 1 (sticks with me)            -> +3

Design note: Q9=3 and Q9=4 both produce +1. The behavioral
difference between "moderate" and "quick" is smaller than the
difference between "sticks with me" and "takes a while." The
largest penalty applies at the extreme low end.

This amplifier fires regardless of whether the athlete selected
"Hard time letting go of mistakes" in Q15. If they did NOT
select it (bucket base = 0), the amplifier still adds its value.
This means an athlete who doesn't self-identify mistake recovery
as a friction area but rates reset speed as 1 still receives
+3 to the Mistake Recovery Lag bucket from the amplifier alone.

------------------------------------------------------------
2B. ROUTINE AMPLIFIER (Q13 -> Structure Gap)
------------------------------------------------------------

Q13 measures routine consistency on a 1-5 scale. Low values =
no routine = higher amplification of the Structure Gap bucket.
Scale is inverted for amplifier mapping.

  Q13 = 5 (very consistent)  -> +0
  Q13 = 4 (consistent)       -> +0
  Q13 = 3 (somewhat)         -> +1
  Q13 = 2 (minimal)          -> +2
  Q13 = 1 (no routine)       -> +3

Design note: Q13=4 and Q13=5 both produce +0. An athlete who
rates their routine as "consistent" or "very consistent" has a
functional routine — no Structure Gap amplification is warranted.
The meaningful behavioral break is between "somewhat" (3) and
"consistent" (4).

Same independence rule as 2A: fires regardless of Q15 selection.

------------------------------------------------------------
2C. TRIGGER AMPLIFIER (Q16 -> multiple buckets)
------------------------------------------------------------

Q16 allows up to 2 trigger context selections. Each trigger
adds +2 to a primary bucket and +1 to a secondary bucket.
If the athlete selects 0 triggers, no amplification occurs.

  "Big competitions or high-stakes moments"
      -> +2 Pressure Reactivity, +1 Overthinking Loop

  "After making a mistake"
      -> +2 Mistake Recovery Lag, +1 Confidence Volatility

  "When being evaluated (coach, scouts, recruiters)"
      -> +2 Overthinking Loop, +1 Confidence Volatility

  "During busy or stressful life weeks"
      -> +2 Discipline Gap, +1 Focus Drift / Distraction

  "When expectations from others are high"
      -> +2 Pressure Reactivity, +1 Confidence Volatility

When 2 triggers are selected, all bucket adjustments from both
triggers are summed. A single bucket can receive amplification
from multiple triggers if both route to it. For example, if the
athlete selects both "Big competitions" and "Expectations high,"
Pressure Reactivity receives +2 + +2 = +4 from triggers alone.

Trigger context is also stored in the PPD output to provide
coaching specificity (see Output Format section).

------------------------------------------------------------
2D. ECOSYSTEM AMPLIFIER (Q23 -> Ecosystem Friction)
------------------------------------------------------------

Q23 is a single-select with 4 options describing the home
conversation pattern after competitions.

  "Mostly supportive"                   -> +0
  "A lot of analysis or advice"         -> +2
  "Pressure to perform"                 -> +3
  "We don't discuss performance much"   -> +1

Design rationale:
- "Mostly supportive" = no friction signal.
- "Analysis or advice" = real friction source (well-meaning parents
  who over-analyze create performance pressure) but less acute than
  direct pressure. +2 captures this intermediate signal.
- "Pressure to perform" = clear ecosystem friction. Full +3.
- "Don't discuss" = disengagement signal, not friction signal. The
  athlete isn't experiencing pressure from home but lacks the
  support channel. +1 gives a small signal without equating
  disengagement to active pressure.

Same independence rule: fires regardless of Q15 selection.

============================================================
LAYER 3: TIE-BREAKER (Q18)
============================================================

Q18 is free text: "Before a big moment, what thought shows up
most often?" The Snapshot Builder agent classifies the text into
one of 4 categories. Each category adds small (+1) adjustments
to specific buckets.

CATEGORIES:

  Avoidance
    Pattern: Negative-outcome-focused, fear-driven, loss-aversion
    Examples: "Don't mess up," "What if I fail," "I hope I don't
    choke," "What if it goes wrong"
    Adjustments: +1 Overthinking Loop, +1 Pressure Reactivity

  Proving
    Pattern: External-validation-seeking, outcome-dependent worth
    Examples: "I need to prove myself," "Show them what I can do,"
    "I have to be the best out there"
    Adjustments: +1 Confidence Volatility

  Obligation
    Pattern: Others-focused pressure, responsibility burden
    Examples: "I can't let them down," "Everyone is counting on
    me," "I have to do it for my family/team"
    Adjustments: +1 Pressure Reactivity, +1 Ecosystem Friction

  Approach
    Pattern: Process-focused, controllable-oriented, positive
    Examples: "Let's go," "Trust the process," "One play at a
    time," "Execute the game plan"
    Adjustments: No bucket adjustment (positive indicator)

CLASSIFICATION RULES:

1. The Snapshot Builder agent reads the full pressure thought
   text and classifies the dominant framing.

2. DUAL CLASSIFICATION is allowed when the text clearly contains
   two distinct framings. Example: "Don't mess up or let anyone
   down" contains Avoidance ("Don't mess up") AND Obligation
   ("let anyone down"). Both classifications apply, and ALL
   bucket adjustments from both categories are summed.

3. Maximum 2 classifications per pressure thought. If the text
   contains elements of 3+ categories, classify the 2 most
   dominant framings only.

4. The classification is stored in the PPD output as
   ppd_tie_breaker_classification for audit trail purposes.
   Format: single category name, or "Category1+Category2" for
   dual classifications (e.g., "Avoidance+Obligation").

5. If the pressure thought text is empty, illegible, or cannot
   be classified, no tie-breaker adjustment is applied. The
   classification is stored as "Unclassified."

============================================================
LAYER 4: FINAL RANKING
============================================================

After all 4 layers are applied:

1. Sum all bucket scores (selection weight + amplifiers +
   tie-breaker adjustments).

2. Sort all 8 buckets by score in descending order.

3. Take the Top 3 buckets as the PPD result.

4. TIE-BREAKING: When two or more buckets have the same score,
   the bucket with the lower position number wins. This is a
   deterministic priority order:

   Priority 1: Overthinking Loop
   Priority 2: Confidence Volatility
   Priority 3: Mistake Recovery Lag
   Priority 4: Focus Drift / Distraction
   Priority 5: Pressure Reactivity
   Priority 6: Discipline Gap
   Priority 7: Structure Gap
   Priority 8: Ecosystem Friction

   Rationale: Internal/cognitive friction buckets (1-5) are
   prioritized over structural/external buckets (6-8) because
   when all else is equal, internal friction is typically the
   higher-leverage coaching target.

5. The #1 ranked bucket is stored as ppd_primary_problem.

============================================================
THEORETICAL MAXIMUM PER BUCKET
============================================================

Each bucket has a different maximum possible score based on
how many scoring pathways reach it:

  Pressure Reactivity:     9  (selection + 2 trigger primaries + dual tie-breaker)
  Mistake Recovery Lag:    8  (selection + Q9 max amplifier + trigger primary)
  Overthinking Loop:       7  (selection + trigger primary + trigger secondary + tie-breaker)
  Ecosystem Friction:      7  (selection + Q23 max amplifier + tie-breaker)
  Confidence Volatility:   6  (selection + 2 trigger secondaries + tie-breaker)
  Structure Gap:           6  (selection + Q13 max amplifier)
  Discipline Gap:          5  (selection + trigger primary)
  Focus Drift / Distraction: 4  (selection + trigger secondary)

This asymmetry is structural and intentional. Buckets that
represent convergence points (like Pressure Reactivity, which
can be fed by multiple trigger types and tie-breaker categories)
have more input pathways. This reflects the reality that some
friction areas are influenced by more situational factors than
others.

============================================================
EDGE CASES
============================================================

1. MINIMUM SELECTION (Q15):
   The app must enforce a minimum of 1 selection and maximum of
   3 selections for Q15. If the form submission somehow arrives
   with 0 selections, the PPD computation flags an error and the
   Snapshot Builder must prompt for re-entry. PPD cannot be
   computed with 0 friction selections.

2. MULTI-WAY TIE FOR TOP 3:
   When 4+ buckets tie for positions within the Top 3 (e.g.,
   four buckets all score 5), the deterministic bucket priority
   order (1-8) resolves all ties. No ties are left unresolved.

3. FRICTION-AMPLIFIER CONTRADICTION:
   When an athlete's Q15 selection and scale answer contradict
   (e.g., selects "Lack of routine or structure" in Q15 but
   rates Q13=5, very consistent routine), the PPD scores as-is.
   The +3 selection weight fires, the +0 amplifier fires. Total
   for Structure Gap = 3. The Snapshot Builder may note the
   contradiction narratively in the athlete snapshot. The future
   PPD Alignment Signal will detect these self-perception gaps
   longitudinally.

4. ALL BUCKETS AT ZERO (impossible with constraints):
   With the minimum 1 selection enforced, at least one bucket
   will always have a minimum score of +3. Additionally, Q9 and
   Q13 amplifiers fire independently of Q15 selections, so
   Mistake Recovery Lag and Structure Gap can have non-zero
   scores even without Q15 selection.

5. MAXIMUM SCORING SCENARIO:
   Athlete selects 3 frictions including Pressure Reactivity,
   selects both "Big competitions" and "Expectations high" as
   triggers, pressure thought classified as Avoidance+Obligation:
   Pressure Reactivity = 3 + 2 + 2 + 1 + 1 = 9 (theoretical max)

6. ZERO TRIGGER SELECTIONS (Q16):
   Q16 allows "up to 2" selections. If the athlete selects 0
   triggers, Layer 2C produces no adjustments. This is valid —
   some athletes may not identify situational triggers. The PPD
   result will be driven by selection weight + scale amplifiers +
   tie-breaker only.

7. APPROACH-FRAMED PRESSURE THOUGHT:
   If Q18 is classified as Approach, no tie-breaker adjustments
   are made. The tie-breaker layer is skipped. Any existing ties
   are resolved by bucket priority order. This is intentional —
   an approach-framed athlete's ranking should be determined
   entirely by their friction selections, triggers, and scale
   answers, not nudged by their pressure thought.

============================================================
OUTPUT FORMAT
============================================================

The PPD produces 4 output fields stored in the athlete snapshot:

------------------------------------------------------------
ppd_top_3 (array of 3 objects)
------------------------------------------------------------

  [
    {
      "rank": 1,
      "bucket": "Overthinking Loop",
      "score": 7,
      "trigger_context": "Big competitions or high-stakes moments",
      "coaching_implication": "Controllable-focus cues + single Bullseye target under pressure"
    },
    {
      "rank": 2,
      "bucket": "Pressure Reactivity",
      "score": 7,
      "trigger_context": ["Big competitions or high-stakes moments", "When being evaluated (coach, scouts, recruiters)"],
      "coaching_implication": "Controllable-focus under pressure + approach-framed self-talk"
    },
    {
      "rank": 3,
      "bucket": "Confidence Volatility",
      "score": 4,
      "trigger_context": "When being evaluated (coach, scouts, recruiters)",
      "coaching_implication": "Preparation-based confidence anchoring + evidence collection"
    }
  ]

Field definitions:
- rank: Integer 1-3. Position in the Top 3 ranking.
- bucket: String. The PPD bucket name.
- score: Integer. The total score after all 4 layers.
- trigger_context: String, array of strings, or null.
    - null: No trigger from Q16 added points to this bucket.
    - String: One trigger added points to this bucket.
    - Array: Two triggers both added points to this bucket.
    Includes ALL triggers that contributed to this bucket (both
    primary +2 and secondary +1 contributions qualify).
- coaching_implication: String. Static per-bucket coaching line
    (see table below). This is a starting-point recommendation;
    the Snapshot Builder synthesizes the full coaching emphasis
    from PPD + ABI + narrative data.

STATIC COACHING IMPLICATION LINES:

  Overthinking Loop:
    "Controllable-focus cues + single Bullseye target under pressure"

  Confidence Volatility:
    "Preparation-based confidence anchoring + evidence collection"

  Mistake Recovery Lag:
    "Reset reps + next-play language"

  Focus Drift / Distraction:
    "Focus cue routines + distraction boundaries"

  Pressure Reactivity:
    "Controllable-focus under pressure + approach-framed self-talk"

  Discipline Gap:
    "Win the Day minimums + habit stacking"

  Structure Gap:
    "Build pre-performance routine + daily rhythm anchors"

  Ecosystem Friction:
    "Communication boundaries + support system clarification"

------------------------------------------------------------
ppd_all_scores (object, 8 key-value pairs)
------------------------------------------------------------

  {
    "overthinking_loop": 7,
    "confidence_volatility": 4,
    "mistake_recovery_lag": 2,
    "focus_drift": 0,
    "pressure_reactivity": 7,
    "discipline_gap": 0,
    "structure_gap": 1,
    "ecosystem_friction": 3
  }

All 8 bucket scores are stored, including zeros. This supports:
- Future longitudinal PPD comparison (execution-derived vs intake)
- PPD Alignment Signal computation (requires all 8 buckets to
  detect Blind Spots — buckets scoring low at intake but high
  in execution data)
- Historical reference for coaching trajectory analysis

------------------------------------------------------------
ppd_primary_problem (string)
------------------------------------------------------------

The bucket name of the rank-1 entry in ppd_top_3. Shorthand
for downstream pipeline consumption.

  "Overthinking Loop"

This field feeds the baseline_primary_derailer derivation in
the Snapshot Builder. The Snapshot Builder uses ppd_primary_problem
as a structured input alongside the narrative derailer analysis
to produce the final baseline_primary_derailer text.

------------------------------------------------------------
ppd_tie_breaker_classification (string)
------------------------------------------------------------

The agent's classification of Q18 pressure thought text. Stored
for audit trail and future analysis.

Valid values:
  "Avoidance"
  "Proving"
  "Obligation"
  "Approach"
  "Avoidance+Proving"
  "Avoidance+Obligation"
  "Proving+Obligation"
  "Unclassified"

Note: "Avoidance+Approach" and other Approach combinations are
not valid — Approach framing is definitionally incompatible with
Avoidance/Proving/Obligation framings. If the text contains both
approach and non-approach elements, classify the non-approach
framing(s) only.

============================================================
DOWNSTREAM CONSUMPTION
============================================================

The PPD output is consumed by:

1. SNAPSHOT BUILDER (Stage 1):
   - ppd_primary_problem informs baseline_primary_derailer
   - ppd_top_3 coaching implications inform Initial Coaching
     Emphasis section
   - ppd_tie_breaker_classification enriches the Stress & Trigger
     Profile section

2. INTERPRETATION ENGINE (Stage 2):
   - ppd_top_3 stored as baseline reference fields
   - Enables future PPD Alignment Signal computation when
     execution-derived PPD is implemented

3. COACHING OUTPUT ENGINE (Stage 3):
   - ppd_top_3 informs coaching emphasis and language selection
   - coaching_implication lines provide starting-point coaching
     themes for personalization

4. FUTURE — PPD ALIGNMENT SIGNAL:
   - ppd_all_scores enables bucket-by-bucket comparison against
     execution-derived PPD scores
   - Signal classifications: Problem Awareness Aligned (intake
     and execution agree), Problem Awareness Gap (intake identifies
     problems execution doesn't confirm), Blind Spot (execution
     reveals problems intake didn't identify)
