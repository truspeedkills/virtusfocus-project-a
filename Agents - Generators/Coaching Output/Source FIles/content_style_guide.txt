This style guide ensures all content (social posts, emails, scripts, and marketing) is consistent, sharp, and aligned with the VirtusFocus brand identity.

1. Core Content Identity

VirtusFocus content is:

Clear:no fluff, no filler

Direct:strong framing, strong takeaways

Grounded:real-world examples and insights

Actionable:gives direction, not theory

Composed:calm, focused, authoritative

Performance-oriented:tied to identity, discipline, consistency

The content should always make athletes, parents, or coaches think:
“This gives me clarity and direction.”

2. Core Messaging Themes

All content should naturally reinforce:

Ownership

Confidence built through structure

Discipline as freedom

Focus on controllables

Consistency over intensity

Identity as the root of long-term change

Simplicity over complexity

These themes should show up frequently, subtly, and naturally:they are the backbone of VirtusFocus messaging.

3. Tone & Voice Rules

Write in a way that mirrors the experience of a grounded coach with sharp mental clarity.

Should Sound Like:

Calm intensity

Confident, not loud

Wise, not preachy

Supportive, not coddling

Strategic, not academic

Language Style:

Short sentences

Strong verbs

Clean structure

No unnecessary adjectives

Use simple, powerful metaphors

Ask tight, reflective questions

Avoid:

Hyperbole

Corny motivational lines

Long paragraphs

Overly intellectual explanations

Harsh criticism or guilt-driven tone

4. Formatting for High-Performance Content
Short-Form Social Media (Reels, TikToks, Shorts)

Start with a punchy hook in the first sentence

Use short lines (1–2 sentences max)

Break up ideas visually

End with a crisp takeaway or call to action

Example Format:

Here’s why confidence disappears under pressure:

You're thinking about everything except what you can control.

Simplify your focus → your performance follows.

Instagram + Facebook Captions

1–2 sentence hook

3–7 short lines with strong insight

Clear emotional or tactical takeaway

CTA: save, share, follow, or reflect

Caption Example:

Most athletes don’t lack confidence. They lack clarity.

When your mind is full of noise, you can't trust your training.

Reduce the variables.

Control the controllables.

Confidence is a byproduct of clarity.

Carousel Posts

Every slide should deliver a single, sharp idea.

Structure Template:

Slide 1 → Big, bold claim

Slide 2 → Why this matters

Slide 3 → The pattern or mistake

Slide 4 → The real fix

Slide 5 → Action step or thought-provoking closer

Emails (Short, Value-Driven Emails)

Clear subject line

Strong first line (no preamble)

3–5 short paragraphs

Single takeaway

Tie back to identity, discipline, or clarity

End with a grounded call to action

Email Rhythm Example:

Most athletes lose confidence for one simple reason:

They don’t know where their focus should be.

When pressure shows up, the mind expands into everything.

The fix is narrowing the focus into one controllable.

Simple → repeatable → reliable.

This is how you build confidence that lasts.

Emails (Newsletter Style)

Include 3–4 sections:

Insight or mindset breakdown

A simple mental framework

A practical application

A coaching question or reflection

Tone should be calm, thoughtful, and strategically supportive.

5. Content Structures & Templates
A. The Clarity Breakdown

Use when explaining a mindset issue.

Here’s the problem

Here’s why it happens

Here’s the real cause

Here’s the simple fix

Here’s your action

B. The Identity Shift

Use when connecting behavior to identity.

Old identity pattern

Why it’s limiting

New identity to adopt

How to reinforce it

What this changes immediately

C. The “Control the Controllables” Framework

Use in stress, pressure, or performance posts.

The situation

What the athlete focuses on

What they should focus on

How to shift attention

What improves instantly

D. The “Simple System” Post

Use to emphasize structure, habits, or routines.

Overcomplicated behavior

The cost

The simplified version

How to apply

How momentum grows

E. “What Most Athletes Get Wrong” Format

Great for hooks + viral posts.

Call out the mistake

Explain why it happens

Simplify the truth

Provide the right pattern

Give a small action step

6. High-Converting Hook Types

These work especially well for athletes, parents, and coaches.

For Athletes:

“Most athletes struggle with this…”

“If you overthink during games, read this.”

“The quickest way to build confidence is…”

“You don't need motivation. You need this.”

For Parents:

“If you want your athlete to be confident without pressure…”

“Here’s the mindset support your athlete actually needs.”

For Coaches:

“If your team struggles with consistency…”

“Your athletes need this system to handle pressure.”

7. Emotional Tone Targets

Every piece of content should create one or more of these feelings:

Clarity:“I know what to do next.”

Groundedness:“This makes sense.”

Capability:“I can do this.”

Responsibility:“This is on me.”

Calm Confidence:“I can handle pressure.”

No hype.
No guilt.
Just grounded strength.

10. Final Rule of VirtusFocus Content

Every piece of content must answer at least one:

Does this give clarity?
Does this reduce noise?
Does this reinforce identity?
Does this create direction or momentum?

If the answer is yes, it’s on-brand.

11. Register and Punctuation

CONTRACTION DEFAULT: VirtusFocus coaching content uses contractions naturally. "Don’t," "isn’t," "you’re," "that’s," "here’s," "it’s" are standard speech. Formal non-contraction prose is reserved for specific emphasis moments only. This applies across all competitive levels.

EM DASH PROHIBITION: Em dashes (—) are not used in any athlete-facing or parent-facing VirtusFocus output. Use commas, periods, colons, or sentence breaks instead. This applies to Coaching Messages, Deep Dives, Parent Coaching Messages, Parent Insights, and Daily Coaching Snippets.
