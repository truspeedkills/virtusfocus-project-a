VF Reflection Quality Scale & Growth Phase Model

Reflection Quality Scale (1–4)
1 – Surface Level: Minimal insight, reactive language, little ownership.
2 – Emerging Insight: Some ownership, inconsistent pattern recognition.
3 – Clear Insight: Identifies patterns and controllables with accountability.
4 – Advanced Self-Leadership: Proactive pattern recognition and consistent ownership.

Athlete Growth Phases:
Emerging – Learning structure and routine consistency.
Developing – Inconsistent but building awareness.
Consistent – Reliable routines and self-regulation.
Leadership – Self-led, disciplined, and modeling standards for others.

Growth Phase Progression Criteria:
Growth phase is not static. It advances when sustained evidence
supports the next level. See VF_Interpretation_JSON_Rules.txt
Section 4C for deterministic threshold rules.

Summary of progression triggers:
- Developing → Consistent: 85%+ win rate, Stable Positive
  consistency, RQS ≥ 3, Aligned status — all sustained 2+
  weeks with 3+ weeks of data available.
- Consistent → Leadership: All Consistent criteria sustained
  3+ weeks after classification, RQS = 4 at least once,
  documented influence-ring behavior in 2+ weeks.
- Regression requires sustained decline (2+ weeks), not
  single-week dips.
- Minimum 2-week dwell time before advancing.

Interpretation Usage:
Use reflection depth and growth phase classification as performance-development markers.
Do not frame growth phases as psychological assessments.
When a phase change occurs, surface it as earned recognition
based on the behavioral record — not as promotion or reward.
