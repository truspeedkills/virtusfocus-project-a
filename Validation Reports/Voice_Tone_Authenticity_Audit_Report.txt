============================================================
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
============================================================

OVERALL AUTHENTICITY ASSESSMENT: ADEQUATE — with specific
patterns that require attention before scale deployment.

Individual paragraphs frequently pass the human-coach
test. The coaching content is strong, behaviorally
grounded, and athlete-specific. The problems emerge at
the STRUCTURAL level — identical sentence constructions,
formulaic transitions, predictable document architecture,
and cross-athlete template sameness that would be
detectable to anyone reading 2-3 consecutive weeks or
comparing messages across athletes.

TOP 3 STRENGTHS:

1. ATHLETE-SPECIFIC BEHAVIORAL GROUNDING. The output
   consistently references specific days, events, and
   the athlete's own words. The coaching observations
   are earned by evidence, not generic. This is the
   pipeline's strongest quality.

2. DOMAIN AND COMPETITIVE LEVEL CALIBRATION. Sport-
   specific language is precise (Aidan's sprint metrics,
   Jasmine's icehouse vocabulary, Angelo's throws
   terminology, John's business language). The voice
   genuinely adapts across domains.

3. HIGH EI WEEK HANDLING. The most emotionally authentic
   writing in the corpus occurs during High EI or
   disrupted weeks (Mergim Wk18 competition, Grace Wk7
   strikeout, Jasmine Wk7 relationship stress). The
   constraint of empathy-first produces more natural,
   less formulaic output.

TOP 3 CONCERNS:

1. "THAT IS [NOUN]." STANDALONE DECLARATIVE OVERUSE.
   This single sentence construction appears in EVERY
   athlete's output, in EVERY file audited (~80+ total
   instances across the corpus). It is the #1 AI-
   detectable pattern. (See Category B1, Section 2.)

2. TRENDLINE ARC RECITATION. Deep Dive trendline sections
   retell the athlete's entire program history from Week
   1 every week, growing longer each time. By Week 9
   (Grace) or Week 19 (Mergim), this is a multi-paragraph
   enumeration of hyphenated labels. No human coach
   retells the full arc weekly. (See Category E2,
   Section 2.)

3. CROSS-ATHLETE STRUCTURAL CLONING. Deep Dives for all
   7 athletes follow an identical internal template:
   same section order, same Strength Signal format
   (heading + paragraph + italicized capper at 100%
   consistency), same opening patterns. A reader
   comparing any two athletes' DDs would see the same
   document architecture. (See Category D1, Section 3.)

PRIORITY RECOMMENDATION: Address the "That is [noun]."
construction and the trendline recitation pattern before
any new athlete output is generated. These two issues
account for the majority of the AI-detectability risk.

2. CATEGORY-BY-CATEGORY FINDINGS

------------------------------------------------------------
CATEGORY A: SENTENCE STRUCTURE PATTERNS
------------------------------------------------------------

A1. PARALLEL CONSTRUCTION OVERUSE
Severity: MODERATE
Frequency: Present in all 7 athletes' output

The pipeline produces "You [verb]. You [verb]. You [verb]."
inventory sequences as its default device for listing
behavioral evidence. Examples:

Grace Wk8 CM: "That is agency. That is you controlling
what you can control."

Angelo Wk7 DD: "You did not drift. You used the two
practice days you had available. You did mental work on
clarity and stability. You rested on Sunday."

The "You did X. You did Y. You did Z." inventory pattern
is the most consistent structural AI tell across all
athletes. A human coach would cluster several actions into
a single sentence or break the rhythm with a fragment or
different subject.

Recommendation: Add a structural variation rule requiring
that behavioral evidence lists use at least 2 different
grammatical structures within any 4-sentence sequence.
Cluster actions into compound sentences, lead with a
detail, or use a fragment to break the rhythm.

------------------------------------------------------------

A2. FORMULAIC TRANSITIONS
Severity: MODERATE
Frequency: 30+ instances across the corpus

Five transition patterns appear with predictable regularity
across all athletes:

(1) "Here is what I want you to see" / "Here is what
    earned recognition" / "Here is what needs attention"
    — Used in Grace Wks 7-9 CMs (identical in all 3),
    Mergim (5 of 6 sampled weeks), Angelo, Tucker, Aidan.

(2) "The question is [whether]..." — 9 instances in
    Angelo's 8 files, 4 in Tucker's 8 files, 4 in
    Mergim's 12 files, 2+ in Jasmine, 2+ in John. This
    is the pipeline's default device for framing a
    developmental challenge.

(3) "I also want to name..." / "I want to acknowledge..."
    — 3 of 4 Jasmine CMs use this as a topic bridge.

(4) "Now the test..." / "This is the biggest test..."
    — Tucker Wks 4-5 use near-identical phrasing.

(5) "Here is what [number] weeks have built..." — Mergim,
    Grace, Angelo all use this in their DD trendlines.

Recommendation: Build a rotation bank of 6-8 transition
alternatives for each function (pivoting to recognition,
pivoting to growth, framing a challenge, bridging to a
new topic). Enforce variety checking against the prior 2-3
weeks of output for the same athlete. "The question is"
should be capped at 0 per CM and 1 per DD maximum.

------------------------------------------------------------

A3. TOPIC-SENTENCE-THEN-EVIDENCE PATTERN
Severity: MINOR
Frequency: Structural default across all files

Nearly every paragraph opens with a thesis claim, then
provides supporting evidence, then delivers an
interpretive statement. This is standard essay structure,
not coaching voice. Most visible in DD Strength Signals
where every sub-section follows: bold label, behavioral
claim, evidence, italicized observation.

A real coach sometimes leads with the detail and lets the
meaning emerge. The pipeline almost never does this.

Recommendation: Specify that 1-2 paragraphs per CM and
1-2 sections per DD should lead with the behavioral detail
before arriving at the coaching point.

------------------------------------------------------------

A4. CADENCE MONOTONY
Severity: MINOR (CMs), MODERATE (DDs)
Frequency: DDs more affected than CMs

CMs generally have reasonable sentence-length variation.
DDs tend toward medium-length sentences (15-25 words)
across entire sections without the short punchy breaks
that mark human writing. The Mindset Summary and Weekly
Trendline sections are the most monotonous.

Recommendation: Add a cadence variation note to the DD
Framework specifying that each section should contain at
least one sentence under 8 words and at least one over
30 words.

------------------------------------------------------------

A5. EXCESSIVE ENUMERATION
Severity: MINOR
Frequency: Occasional

The CMs largely avoid numbered lists. The DDs use bold
sub-headers appropriately. The main enumeration concern
is the trendline sections (covered in E2) and Angelo's
Identity Thread four-trait mechanical mapping (covered in
Category D2 below).

CATEGORY B: VOCABULARY AND PHRASING PATTERNS
------------------------------------------------------------

B1. OVERUSED COACHING PHRASES
Severity: MAJOR
This is the highest-priority finding in the audit.

PHRASE FREQUENCY TABLE (see also Section 7):

"That is [noun]." STANDALONE DECLARATIVE
Total instances found: 80+ across ~50 files audited
(average 1.5+ per file, with some files containing 4-5)
Found in: Every athlete. Every file type. Every week.
Severity: MAJOR

Examples:
- Grace Wk7 CM: "That is your hinge habit" / "That is
  not something most people do" / "That is a choice"
  (3 in one CM)
- Mergim Wk17 CM: "That is not a goal anymore. That is
  a conclusion you earned."
- Mergim Wk18 CM: "That is the derailer doing exactly
  what derailers do" / "That recovery speed is the
  growth" / "That is not a phrase -- that is a man
  processing a hard day" (3 in one CM)
- Jasmine Wk6 CM: "That is an active choice" / "That is
  the frustration-to-communication pattern getting
  stronger" (2 in one CM)
- John Wk1 DD: "That is an identity-level change"

A real coach uses "That is [noun]" occasionally as an
emphatic punctuation device. Using it 3-5 times per
document, and using it in every single document across
7 athletes, creates an unmistakable AI fingerprint.

"NOT X -- Y" CORRECTION PATTERN
Total instances: 40+ across the corpus
Found in: Every athlete, nearly every file
Severity: MODERATE

Examples:
- Grace Wk8 CM: "not because of what happened in a game,
  but because of what you did on a Sunday"
- Angelo Wk6 CM: "not because the Missed day matters,
  but because what you did after it does"
- Mergim Wk17 CM: "That was not confidence built on
  belief. That was confidence built on evidence."
- John DD Wk1: "it is not about working harder, it is
  about becoming someone who does the mundane things
  reliably"

This negation-then-reframe is the pipeline's primary
rhetorical move. It appears at least once per file across
all athletes. The device itself is effective coaching
communication, but its universal frequency makes it a
detectable template.

"THE QUESTION IS [WHETHER]..."
Total instances: 25+ across the corpus
Found in: All 7 athletes
Severity: MODERATE

Angelo alone has 9 instances across 8 files. Tucker has
4 across 8 files. The phrase appears in both CMs and DDs,
sometimes in both documents for the same week (John Wk1
CM and DD pose essentially the same question).

"HERE IS WHAT [I WANT YOU TO SEE]..."
Total instances: 15+ across the corpus
Severity: MODERATE

Used as the standard pivot from evidence to interpretation
across all athletes. Grace Wks 7-9 use the identical
phrase "Here is what earned recognition this week."

"THE VERSION OF YOU THAT..."
Total instances: 10+ across Tucker and Aidan files
Severity: MAJOR (for those athletes)

Tucker Wks 4-6 DDs: "the weekday Tucker" vs "the weekend
Tucker." Aidan Wk4 DD: "Not the version that needs a
7.2... The version that can look at a 7.5..." — three
"version" references in one paragraph. No human coach
uses "the version of you" to describe an athlete.

Recommendation: Cap "That is [noun]." to maximum 2 per
CM and 2 per DD. Cap "Not X -- Y" to maximum 1 per CM
and 2 per DD. Cap "The question is" to 0 per CM and 1
per DD. Retire "the version of you" entirely -- replace
with "you" or specific contextual framing. Add all of
these to the Coaching Output Instructions as tracked
frequency limits.

B2. REGISTER SHIFTS
Severity: MODERATE (DDs), MINOR (CMs)

The most notable register shifts occur in Deep Dives
where the voice oscillates between direct coaching and
analytical observation:

Grace Wk8 DD: "Emotional intensity returned to Low after
Week 7's Moderate elevation" — direct pipeline field
description. A coach would never say "emotional intensity
returned to Low."

Grace Wk9 DD: "One data point does not confirm a trend
reversal" — statistical analysis language.

Tucker Wk5 DD: "Your Win days by week: 4, 4, 3, 3, 5.
Your Missed days: 0, 1, 1, 1, 2." — raw number sequences
presented as prose. No human writes this way.

Jasmine's CMs use an absolute no-contraction register
(0% contractions across all CMs) which crosses from
elevated to stiff. Even for a Professional-level athlete,
a coach would use occasional contractions.

Recommendation: Ban pipeline classification language from
DDs (no "Emotional intensity returned to Low," no "Stable
Positive," no "Growth tier"). Require that data be
presented in coaching voice ("You have won almost every
day for the last month" not "Rolling win rate: 96.4%").
Allow 15-20% contraction usage even for Professional
athletes.

------------------------------------------------------------

B3. PIPELINE VOCABULARY LEAKAGE
Severity: MODERATE

No hard compliance violations (no composite score names,
flag IDs, etc.). However, conceptual fingerprints of
pipeline classifications appear throughout the DDs:

- "Consistent phase maintained" — Grace Wks 7-9 DDs
- "Developing phase maintained" — Angelo Wks 5-7 DDs
- "Emotional intensity Low" — verbatim in multiple DDs
- "Growth tier" — Jasmine Wk1 DD, Angelo Wk1 DD
- "RQS 2" / "RQS at 2" — Angelo, Jasmine DDs
- "Approach-framed execution" — Mergim Wks 2, 17, 18
  (used directly in athlete-facing CMs)

"Approach-framed" is the most notable leakage — it is
pipeline-internal terminology surfaced directly to the
athlete 3 times. A college wrestler would not understand
"approach-framed execution."

Recommendation: Add "approach-framed" to the prohibited
vocabulary list with replacement: "going toward something
instead of away from it" or "competing to score, not to
not lose." Require that growth phase and emotional
intensity be described behaviorally, never by
classification name.

Generally well-controlled. The writing follows the brand

Angelo's output uses "deliberately" / "intentional" /

This is thematically appropriate (preparation discipline

B5. HEDGING LANGUAGE

B6. EM DASH OVERUSE
Severity: MODERATE (DDs)

Em dash counts per file:
- CMs average: 3-5 (acceptable)
- DDs average: 5-8 (elevated)
- Peak: Grace Wk9 DD (12), Angelo Wk7 DD (11), Tucker
  Wk6 DD (8), Mergim Wk19 DD (7), Jasmine Wk6 DD (7)

DDs trend upward in em dash usage over time. The em dash
is frequently used for the "Not X -- Y" pattern, which
compounds with the B1 finding. A human coach would use
more periods and fewer parenthetical insertions.

Recommendation: Cap em dashes at 6 per DD. When the
"Not X -- Y" construction is used, count each instance
against the cap.

C1. OPENING HOOK FORMULA
Severity: MAJOR (Grace), MODERATE (others)

Grace Kindel Wks 7-9 CMs use an identical opening
template:
- Wk7: "Forty-nine straight Win days. Seven consecutive
  Growth weeks. And this week..."
- Wk8: "Fifty-six straight Win days. Eight consecutive
  Growth weeks. And this week..."
- Wk9: "Sixty-three straight Win days. Nine consecutive
  Growth weeks. And this week..."

Six of six recent Grace files (CMs + DDs) open with the
same "[count]. [count]. And this week..." formula. This
is the second most detectable AI pattern after "That is
[noun]."

Tucker opens 3 of 4 CMs with Win/Missed day count.

Angelo and Aidan show better variation. John's CM opening
is the strongest in the corpus ("Six deals in your first
week.").

Recommendation: Specify 5+ opening approaches (stat-led,
quote-led, moment-led, question-led, challenge-led,
context-led) and require rotation. No athlete should see
the same opening formula 3 weeks in a row.

C2. PARAGRAPH FLOW PREDICTABILITY
Severity: MODERATE

CMs follow a highly predictable internal structure:
1. Opening hook (stats or behavioral headline)
2. Prior commitment bridge
3. Recognition block ("Here is what earned recognition")
4. Growth/attention block ("Here is what needs attention")
5. Identity anchor callback
6. YOUR ACTION section
7. Closing + signature
8. NEXT WEEK FOCUS QUESTION

This order is essentially identical every week across all
athletes. A real coach would sometimes lead with the
challenge, skip recognition when the week demands it, or
vary the proportion of praise to push.

Recommendation: Add structural variation guidance allowing
CMs to occasionally front-load the challenge, lead with
a question, or merge recognition and growth into a single
narrative rather than separate blocks.

------------------------------------------------------------

C3. DEEP DIVE SECTION SAMENESS
Severity: MODERATE

Every DD across all 7 athletes follows the exact same
internal pattern:
- Mindset Summary: opens with "[N] weeks into this
  program..." or "This was the week that..."
- Trendline: opens with longitudinal data enumeration
- Strength Signals: 3-4 items, each with identical format
  (ALL CAPS heading + evidence paragraph + italicized
  observation)
- Growth Areas: 2-3 items, same format as above
- Identity Thread: opens with baseline reference

The fixed count of Strength Signals (always 3-4) and
Growth Areas (always 2-3) is detectable at scale. A human
analyst would sometimes have 5 strengths and 1 growth
area, or 2 strengths and 4 growth areas.

Recommendation: Allow the Strength Signal count to vary
from 2-5 and Growth Area count to vary from 1-4 based on
the week's character. Permit occasional section reordering
for disrupted weeks.

------------------------------------------------------------

C4. YOUR ACTION SECTION VARIATION
Severity: MINOR — This is a strength.

YOUR ACTION sections show good variation in modality
(written, oral, behavioral, social, combined), specificity
(practice-based, competition-based, daily routine),
and subtitle (contextual per week). Aidan's actions are
the best in the corpus for variety and specificity.

------------------------------------------------------------

C5. CLOSING PHRASE VARIETY
Severity: MINOR

Most athletes receive 3-4 different closing phrases across
their program. Some cross-athlete duplication exists
("Control what's in your center ring" and "Lead yourself
first" each used for 3+ athletes). Jasmine's closings are
the most distinctive and varied.

Recommendation: Develop 2-3 athlete-specific closing
phrases per athlete that connect to their baseline profile,
in addition to the standard rotation.

E1. ITALICIZED OBSERVATION QUALITY
Severity: MODERATE
Frequency: 100% — every Strength Signal and Growth Area
item across all athletes ends with an italicized
observation, with zero exceptions.

The italicized lines follow a consistent template:
abstract the specific evidence into a universal coaching
principle.

Strong examples:
- Grace Wk7 DD: "This is the first time your reset habit
  was tested by a real competitive negative. The fact
  that it held is the strongest evidence in seven weeks
  that this piece of your mental game is no longer
  developing -- it is working."

Formulaic examples:
- Grace Wk8 DD: "The athletes who create their own
  development opportunities are the ones who eventually
  outgrow the situations that frustrated them."
- Grace Wk9 DD: "The athletes who build their own
  development programs are the ones who eventually
  outgrow the limitations of the opportunities they
  are given."
  (Nearly identical to Week 8. Same structure, similar
  wording, same principle in consecutive weeks.)

The "The athletes who [X] are the ones who [Y]" general-
principle opener should not appear in consecutive weeks.
The 100% consistency of italicized cappers — never
varying, never omitted — is a structural AI signature.

Recommendation: Reduce italicized observation frequency
to 70-80%. Permit 1-2 items per DD to end without an
italicized capper (let the evidence speak). When used,
vary the form: sometimes a question, sometimes a callback
to athlete language, sometimes a concrete observation
rather than a universal maxim.

E2. TRENDLINE SECTION NATURALNESS
Severity: MAJOR

The Weekly Trendline is the least human-sounding section
across all Deep Dives. Two specific problems:

PROBLEM 1: FULL ARC RECITATION
The trendline retells the athlete's entire program history
from Week 1 every single week, appending the new week's
data to the end. By Week 9 (Grace), this produces a
9-item enumeration of hyphenated labels:

Grace Wk9 DD: "outcome-reactive at baseline, preparation-
driven through the preseason, structure-sustained under
adversity in Week 3, action-proven in Week 4, identity-
embedded in Week 5, competition-validated in Week 6,
failure-resilient in Week 7, agency-driven in Week 8,
and now advocacy-active in Week 9."

This reads like a data visualization legend. No coach
writes nine hyphenated compound adjectives in a single
sentence.

By Week 19 (Mergim), the athlete has read the same origin
story three consecutive times with one new paragraph
appended.

Jasmine's DD Wk6 and Wk7 trendlines are nearly identical
for their first 5-6 paragraphs. The first sentence of
Wk6 ("Six weeks in, here is what your journey looks
like.") and Wk7 ("Seven weeks in, here is what your
journey looks like.") differ by one word.

PROBLEM 2: DATA-ANALYSIS VOICE
Tucker Wk5 DD: "Your Win days by week: 4, 4, 3, 3, 5.
Your Missed days: 0, 1, 1, 1, 2." — Raw number sequences.

Angelo Wk6 DD: "Your rolling win rate across the last
four weeks is 85.7 percent." — Calculated to one decimal
place. No coach reports rolling win rates as percentages
in prose.

Grace Wk9 DD: "One data point does not confirm a trend
reversal." — Statistical analysis language.

Recommendation: After Week 4, the trendline should never
enumerate every prior week. Reference 2-3 key inflection
points relevant to the current week. Replace hyphenated
arc labels with behavioral descriptions. Replace exact
percentages with qualitative descriptors ("you have won
almost every day" not "96.4 percent").

3. CROSS-ATHLETE VOICE DIFFERENTIATION ASSESSMENT
============================================================

QUESTION: Does each athlete receive a distinct coaching
voice?

WHERE INDIVIDUATION SUCCEEDS:

1. SPORT-SPECIFIC LANGUAGE. Each athlete's output uses
   domain-appropriate terminology. Aidan gets sprint
   metrics (".96 10m fly," "30-60 split at 2.98").
   Jasmine gets bobsled vocabulary ("icehouse," "push
   champs," "sled pushing"). John gets business language
   ("deals," "proposals," "operator"). Angelo gets
   throws terminology. This is excellent.

2. ACCOUNTABILITY CALIBRATION. Tucker (Mixed tier, 6
   weeks) receives more direct accountability language
   ("you chose not to," "the bar is not golf"). Grace
   (Growth tier, 9 weeks) receives more recognition-
   forward coaching. Jasmine (Professional) receives
   elevated framing. This maps correctly to competitive
   level and weekly tier.

3. EMOTIONAL INTENSITY ADAPTATION. High EI weeks
   (Mergim Wk14 injury, Mergim Wk18 competition, Grace
   Wk7 strikeout, Jasmine Wk7 relationship stress)
   produce warmer, slower-paced, more empathetic output
   that is well-differentiated from Low EI weeks.

WHERE INDIVIDUATION FAILS:

1. DD STRUCTURAL CLONING. All 7 athletes receive DDs with
   identical internal architecture: same section order,
   same Strength Signal format (heading + paragraph +
   italicized capper), same Growth Area format, same
   Trendline enumeration pattern. Jasmine (Olympic
   bobsled) and John (entrepreneur, Week 1) receive
   structurally indistinguishable Deep Dives. The
   template overwhelms the voice.

2. SHARED PHRASE INVENTORY. The "That is [noun]."
   construction, the "Not X -- Y" reframe, the "The
   question is" framing, and the "Here is what" pivot
   appear in every athlete's output at similar frequency.
   A reader comparing any two athletes' CMs would
   encounter the same rhetorical devices.

3. CLOSING PHRASE OVERLAP. "Control what's in your center
   ring" and "Lead yourself first" each appear for 3+
   athletes. A reader comparing Tucker and Aidan's CMs
   would see the same closing phrase.

4. IDENTITY THREAD MECHANICAL MAPPING. Angelo's DDs
   enumerate all 4 baseline traits (goal-oriented,
   resilient, tenacious, sharp) every single week in the
   Identity Thread using the same colon-delineated
   format. Tucker's DDs repeat the "weekday version vs.
   weekend version" binary across Wks 4-6. These have
   become crutches rather than evolving observations.

4. COACHING RELATIONSHIP MATURITY ASSESSMENT
============================================================

QUESTION: Does the voice mature over time within each
athlete?

GRACE KINDEL (9 weeks):
- Content maturation: YES. Coaching observations become
  progressively more specific and longitudinally grounded.
  By Week 9, the coach references specific prior weeks,
  connects patterns across 2-month spans, and the micro-
  commitments are highly situation-tailored.
- Voice maturation: MIXED. Week 1 CM uses contractions
  and shorter sentences — sounds more natural. By Week 9,
  the voice has shifted to formal non-contraction English
  ("did not," "That is," "it is") which sounds stiffer.
  The specification-driven structure (separator bars,
  identical transitions) crystallized between Week 1 and
  Week 7, adding consistency but reducing naturalness.

MERGIM BUSHATI (19 weeks):
- Content maturation: YES. Week 18 CM ("Match 2 is the
  real data") is surgically specific compared to Week 1
  CM (generically encouraging). The athlete's own
  language is integrated more naturally in later weeks.
  YOUR ACTION sections evolve from generic to highly
  situation-specific.
- Voice maturation: PARTIAL. The "That is [noun]." device
  persists from Week 1 to Week 19 at the same rate. The
  "Here's what" transition persists across the full span.
  A coach 19 weeks into a relationship should have
  developed shorthand and familiarity — more direct
  challenges, fewer framing devices.

TUCKER LLOYD (6 weeks):
- Voice starts more natural (Week 1 has better rhythm and
  conversational register) and gets slightly more
  formulaic over time. The "weekday vs. weekend" framing
  becomes a repeated talking point without variation.

AIDAN BURKARDT (5 weeks):
- Week 5 CM is the single most human-sounding CM in the
  entire corpus ("You asked me a direct question this
  week, so I'm going to answer it directly."). The voice
  is responsive to what the athlete brought, not what the
  template expects.

JOHN TASTINGER (1 week):
- The CM adapts well to Week 1. The DD is remarkably
  detailed and confident for a first interaction — a real
  coach would be more tentative in Week 1, with more
  "what I am seeing so far" language and fewer confident
  pattern declarations.

OVERALL: Content quality improves over time across all
athletes. Voice authenticity does not consistently
improve — and in some cases, the specification-driven
structure makes later weeks more formulaic than early
weeks.

5. WEEK-TYPE VOICE VARIATION ASSESSMENT
============================================================

GOLD STREAK vs. DISRUPTED WEEKS:
The coaching voice DOES differentiate between week types,
primarily in content focus and emotional calibration.
However, the DD structural template remains identical
regardless of week character — same section count, same
Strength Signal format, same internal architecture. A
disrupted week should feel structurally different (shorter
strengths, longer recovery narrative, potentially
reordered sections). Instead, disruption is reflected in
content but not in form.

HIGH EI vs. LOW EI WEEKS:
High EI weeks produce the corpus's most authentic writing.
The constraint of empathy-first forces more natural,
evidence-grounded prose. Mergim Wk18 (first competition),
Grace Wk7 (first competitive failure), and Jasmine Wk7
(relationship stress) are among the strongest files
because the emotional specificity gives the writing a
natural anchor.

COMPETITION vs. RECOVERY WEEKS:
Competition weeks (Angelo Wk5, Aidan Wk4, Mergim Wk18)
are well-differentiated — they lead with competitive
moments and the coaching observations are appropriately
more evaluative. Recovery weeks (Angelo Wk7, Tucker Wk6)
correctly shift tone to recovery and recommitment.

FINDING: Week-type content differentiation is adequate.
Week-type structural differentiation is absent. The DD
template does not flex based on the week's character.

6. SPECIFICATION IMPROVEMENT RECOMMENDATIONS
============================================================

PRIORITY: HIGH

H1. CAP "THAT IS [NOUN]." CONSTRUCTION
Where: Coaching Output Instructions (frequency limit)
What: Maximum 2 standalone "That is [noun]." sentences per
CM, maximum 2 per DD. Force variation: embed judgments
mid-sentence, use different constructions, or let evidence
speak without a declarative stamp.
Why: #1 AI-detectable pattern. Appears in every file.

H2. ELIMINATE TRENDLINE ARC RECITATION
Where: Deep Dive Framework (Trendline section guidance)
What: After Week 4, the Trendline should reference 2-3
key inflection points relevant to the current week. Never
enumerate every prior week. Never use hyphenated compound-
adjective arc labels. Replace percentage citations with
qualitative descriptors.
Why: #2 AI-detectable pattern. Grows worse with each week.

H3. ADD PHRASE FREQUENCY LIMITS
Where: Coaching Output Instructions (new section)
What: Track and cap these constructions:
- "The question is [whether]...": 0/CM, 1/DD max
- "Not X -- Y" correction: 1/CM, 2/DD max
- "Here is what [I want you to see]": 0 (retire entirely,
  replace with varied transitions)
- "The version of you that": 0 (retire entirely)
- "I also want to name/acknowledge": 0/CM (retire)
- Em dashes: 4/CM, 6/DD max
Why: These phrases appear at template-detectable frequency
across all athletes.

H4. RESTORE CONTRACTIONS AS DEFAULT
Where: Coaching Output Instructions (voice section)
What: Contractions are the default register for Coach
Arron. Formal no-contraction prose should be reserved
only for specific emphasis moments (1-2 per CM max).
Even Professional-level athletes should receive 70-80%
contraction usage.
Why: The shift from Week 1 contractions to later-week
formal prose makes the voice less human over time.

PRIORITY: MEDIUM

M1. ADD DD STRUCTURAL VARIATION
Where: Deep Dive Framework (section guidance)
What: Allow Strength Signal count to vary 2-5, Growth
Area count to vary 1-4. Permit section reordering for
disrupted weeks. Allow occasional omission of the
italicized observation (target 70-80%, not 100%).
Why: Fixed counts and 100% capper consistency are
detectable at scale.

M2. ADD CM-DD DEDUPLICATION RULE
Where: Coaching Output Instructions (new rule)
What: No phrase longer than 8 words should appear
identically in both the CM and DD for the same week.
Why: Jasmine Wk7 has "That is not a small thing. That
is a skill." verbatim in both CM and DD. John Wk1 has
the same "The question is whether..." construction in
both documents.

M3. BREAK CM OPENING FORMULA
Where: Coaching Message Framework (opening guidance)
What: Specify 5+ opening approaches (stat-led, quote-led,
moment-led, question-led, challenge-led). No athlete
should see the same opening formula in consecutive weeks.
Why: Grace Wks 7-9 use identical "[count]. [count]. And
this week..." template.

M4. ADD TRANSITION ROTATION BANK
Where: Coaching Output Instructions (new section)
What: Provide 6-8 alternative transitions for each
function (pivoting to recognition, pivoting to growth,
framing challenges, bridging topics). Enforce variety
against prior 2-3 weeks.
Why: "Here is what earned recognition" appears identically
in consecutive Grace CMs.

M5. BAN PIPELINE CLASSIFICATION LANGUAGE FROM DDs
Where: Deep Dive Framework (voice rules)
What: Prohibit: "Emotional intensity returned to Low,"
"Consistent phase maintained," "Developing phase
maintained," "Growth tier," "RQS [number]," "Stable
Positive," "approach-framed." Require behavioral
description instead.
Why: These pipeline-concept descriptions break the
coaching voice register.

PRIORITY: LOW

L1. VARY IDENTITY THREAD APPROACH
Where: Deep Dive Framework (Identity Thread guidance)
What: Do not enumerate all baseline traits every week.
Pick 1-2 most relevant and go deeper. Reference the full
set only every 3-4 weeks.
Why: Angelo's DDs mechanically map 4 intake traits weekly.

L2. DEVELOP ATHLETE-SPECIFIC CLOSING PHRASES
Where: Coaching Message Framework (closing guidance)
What: In addition to standard rotation, develop 2-3
athlete-specific closings connected to each athlete's
baseline profile.
Why: Cross-athlete duplication of closing phrases.

L3. REGENERATE REMAINING PRE-FIX DDs
Where: Operations (not specification)
What: Regenerate all DDs outside the Session 69 scope
(Grace Wk1, Aidan Wks 1-2, Tucker Wk1, Angelo Wk1,
Mergim Wks 1-16) under current DD Framework.
Why: Pipeline metadata and coach-facing labels in old-
format DDs are athlete-visible.

8. EXEMPLAR PASSAGES
============================================================

The 5 best passages — most authentically human-sounding:

1. AIDAN BURKARDT, WEEK 5 CM
   File: Aidan_Burkardt_2026-02-16to2026-02-22_VF_
   CoachingMessage.txt

   "You asked me a direct question this week, so I am
   going to answer it directly. You noticed that you
   are mentally rehearsing your start and race feelings
   throughout the day -- checking to make sure they are
   still there. And you recognized that the constant
   rehearsal is draining you before practice. That
   observation alone is the kind of self-awareness most
   athletes never develop. Here is the answer:
   visualization works when it has a window. It stops
   working when it runs all day."

   WHY IT WORKS: The coach is responding to a specific
   question from the athlete. The observation validates
   before the answer arrives. The answer itself is
   concrete and memorable ("a window"). This sounds like
   a real coaching conversation — responsive, earned,
   specific.

2. MERGIM BUSHATI, WEEK 18 CM
   File: Mergim_Bushati_2026-01-05to2026-01-11_VF_
   CoachingMessage.txt

   "Match 1 -- you blanked out. 'Completely forgot
   everything. I wrestled like nothing like how I do in
   the wrestling room.' That happened. I am not going
   to pretend it did not... But here is what happened
   next. Match 2 -- you just wrestled... Listen to me
   carefully: match 2 is the real data."

   WHY IT WORKS: Honest acknowledgment ("I am not going
   to pretend it did not"), directional shift ("But here
   is what happened next"), and authoritative reframe
   ("Listen to me carefully"). The sentence variety is
   excellent. The athlete's own words anchor the entire
   passage.

3. GRACE KINDEL, WEEK 7 CM
   File: Grace_Kindel_2026-03-09to2026-03-15_VF_
   CoachingMessage.txt

   "Monday you stepped in as a pinch-hitter, you struck
   out, and the first thing you wrote was 'that sucks.'
   By Wednesday, you wrote 'I just need to reset and
   I will be fine.' That two-day sequence is the most
   important thing that has happened in this program."

   WHY IT WORKS: Uses the athlete's exact words from two
   different days. The pacing mirrors how a real coach
   would narrate this moment. The claim ("the most
   important thing") feels earned and personal.

4. JOHN TASTINGER, WEEK 1 CM
   File: John_Tastinger_2026-03-30to2026-04-05_VF_
   CoachingMessage.txt

   "Six deals in your first week. You set a target of
   five, hit six, and helped someone on your team close
   their first. That right there tells me something
   about what you are capable of when you are locked in."

   WHY IT WORKS: Direct, specific, earned. "That right
   there tells me" is natural coaching speech. The
   numbers are concrete. The opening is not generic
   welcome language — it is about what John actually did.

5. JASMINE JONES, WEEK 6 CM
   File: Jasmine_Jones_2025-06-23to2025-06-29_VF_
   CoachingMessage.txt

   "When your teammates pushed for a standard test in
   the icehouse on Wednesday, you declined. That was
   not avoidance. That was strategy."

   WHY IT WORKS: Sharp, direct, earned recognition. The
   "That was not avoidance. That was strategy." reframe
   is exactly what a coach would say. Short sentences,
   clear judgment, no filler.

   "Your Win days by week: 4, 4, 3, 3, 5. Your Missed
   days: 0, 1, 1, 1, 2."

   WHY IT FAILS: Raw number sequences presented as
   sentences. This is a data table forced into prose.
   No human coach writes this way.

3. ANGELO ALLEN, WEEK 7 DD, IDENTITY THREAD
   File: Angelo_Allen_2026-03-30to2026-04-05_VF_
   DeepDive.txt

   "Seven weeks in, the pattern is clear. Peak
   performance weeks happen when preparation is
   deliberate. Recovery weeks work when rest is
   deliberate. Disruption weeks hold when engagement
   is deliberate. The common thread is not volume or
   external structure -- it is intention."

   WHY IT FAILS: Triple parallel "X weeks [verb] when
   Y is deliberate" construction. Textbook AI-generated
   prose. No human coach writes three consecutive
   sentences with identical syntactic architecture to
   arrive at a fourth sentence that names the abstract
   principle.

4. JASMINE JONES, WK6 DD / WK7 DD, TRENDLINE OPENING

   Wk7: "Seven weeks in, here is what your journey looks
   like. You started by owning your training..."

   WHY IT FAILS: The first sentence differs by one word
   ("Six" vs "Seven"). The subsequent week-by-week
   recitation is nearly verbatim. The athlete reading
   both would see a copy-paste with one paragraph

5. AIDAN BURKARDT, WEEK 4 DD, IDENTITY THREAD
