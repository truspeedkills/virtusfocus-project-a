**\*\*How the Bullseye Method Is Used in the Mindset Operating System**

**1. Purpose of the Bullseye Method in the System**

The Bullseye Method is a **core pillar** of the VirtusFocus Mindset
Operating System. It teaches athletes to consistently separate:

1.  **What they can control** (Center Ring)

2.  **What they can influence** (Middle Ring)

3.  **What they cannot control** (Outer Ring)

This classification directly supports VirtusFocus goals:\
• athlete-led growth\
• emotional regulation\
• stress management under pressure\
• ownership of daily behaviors\
• identity-based habit development

This principle appears in **daily workflows, weekly reviews, AI
coaching logic, analytics, and interface design**.


**2. Where the Bullseye Appears in the App (System Summary)**

**Daily Evening Check-In (Mandatory, every evening)**

Bullseye Method is the **third and final step** of the Evening Review
workflow:

1.  **Win The Day Scoring** (5 questions → 0--5 points)
   

2.  **Daily Journaling** (Work/School, Practice/Competition, Home Life)

3.  **Bullseye Method Classification**

    -   "What I Can Control"

    -   "What I Could Influence"

    -   "What I Couldn't Control"


**Real-Time (Optional/Self Serve)**

Athletes may use the Bullseye Method **in the moment** during stress,
conflict, competition, or anxiety.\
The app should allow:\
• quick-access Bullseye input\
• optional "in-the-moment reframing" log\
• logging these entries so they can be added to separately from Daily Recap


**3. Daily Recap → Bullseye Method Workflow Detail**

**3.1 Trigger**

Occurs after the athlete submits:

1.  Win The Day Score

2.  Daily Journaling fields

Then the UI transitions into the Bullseye screen.

**3.2 Required Input Fields**

Each category is a **free-text field** with guidance examples.

**A. CAN CONTROL (Center Ring)**\
Examples: attitude, effort, breathing, posture, reactions.\
App provides examples pulled from Bullseye Method content.


**B. CAN INFLUENCE (Middle Ring)**\
Examples: communication, leadership, team culture, outcomes indirectly.

**C. CANNOT CONTROL (Outer Ring)**\
Examples: others' decisions, weather, referees, the past, external
opinions.

**3.3 Validation**

• Minimum input: At least **one item** must be listed in each field.\
• Input type: Free text (string).\
• Error message if empty:\
"List at least one thing from today that fits this category."


**5.Purpose**

Allows athletes to self-regulate during:\
• competitions\
• stressful moments at school\
• interpersonal conflicts\
• emotional spikes


**App Behavior**

-   Quick-access button ("Use Bullseye Now")

-   Minimal input UI:

    -   "What just happened?"

    -   "Can Control"

    -   "Can Influence"

    -   "Can't Control"

-   Option to "tag" the moment (competition, school, home, social, etc.)

**6. Why the Bullseye Must Happen Daily (Behavioral Rationale)**

From the Coaching Playbook and Bullseye Method documentation:\
• Bullseye reframing builds **self-leadership** and **emotional
regulation**\
• Helps athletes stop leaking energy into uncontrollables\
• Increases resilience and shortens recovery from adversity\
• Creates consistency between intention (morning) and execution (day)\
• Helps AI detect patterns and tailor coaching language

Because this is a foundational component of athlete development, the
Bullseye Method is **non-optional** in the Evening Review

**Frontend**

-   The Bullseye screen must be simple and low friction.

-   Auto-save behavior recommended to prevent data loss.

-   Real-time quick-entry version should prioritize speed (1--2 taps +
    text).
