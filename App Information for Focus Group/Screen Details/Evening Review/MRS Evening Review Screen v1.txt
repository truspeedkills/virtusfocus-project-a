# Evening Review Screen v1 - Mermaid-Ready Specification

## Purpose

Help the athlete **close the day with ownership, not overthinking**.

This is the **most behavior-shaping screen in the app**.

The goal is not insight for insight's sake - it is: - honest self-assessment - emotional regulation - clean closure

By the end, the athlete should feel: > "I looked at today clearly. I'm complete. Tomorrow resets."

This document translates **Evening Review Screen (v1)** into a **progressive, state-aware specification** built explicitly for **Mermaid diagram generation**.

## Global Screen Rules

**Structural Rules** - One directional flow - Progressive reveal (not all sections visible at once) - No branching exits - One completion endpoint

**Behavioral Rules** - Reflection without rumination - Choice over explanation - Data capture without emotional charge

**Mermaid Translation Note** This screen should be modeled as a **linear progression with gated sections**, not parallel modules.

# SCREEN FLOW - PROGRESSIVE SECTIONS

## SECTION 1 - Win the Day Review (Core Action)

### Purpose

Create a fast, honest snapshot of how the athlete led themselves today.

This is the **primary action** of the Evening Review.

### Layout

- Five stacked Yes / No cards
- One question per card
- Full-width tap targets

### Questions (Fixed Order)

- Did I act with intention?
- Did I pursue progress over perfection?
- Did I respond well to adversity?
- Did I accomplish my Mindset Challenge?
- Did I end with gratitude?

### Interaction

- Tap = Yes or No
- Immediate visual confirmation
- Yes = 1 point
- No = 0 points

### Rules

- No explanations
- No modals
- No tooltips

Once all five cards are answered → advance automatically.

### Hard Rules

- ❌ No skipping questions
- ❌ No rewording per day
- ❌ No emotional language tied to score

## SECTION 2 - Bullseye Method (Contextual, Lightweight)

### Purpose

Help the athlete re-center focus **without forcing emotional excavation**.

This section adapts based on earlier usage.

### Entry Logic

**If Bullseye was NOT used earlier today:** Prompt: > "Tap to log what you focused on today."

**If Bullseye WAS used earlier today:** Prompt: > "Want to add anything else?"

### Layout Options

- Three columns or rings:
  - Control
  - Influence
  - Can't Control

### Interaction

- Tap-based input only
- 1-3 taps total max
- Short text per tap (phrase-level)

### Rules

- No essays
- No required balance across rings
- Athlete may skip if emotionally spent

### Hard Rules

- ❌ Do not force Bullseye depth
- ❌ No coaching feedback here
- ❌ No corrective language

## SECTION 3 - Short Journal (Optional Depth)

### Purpose

Allow reflection **without pressure**.

This is optional depth - not required labor.

### Entry Logic

**If no journal entry exists today:** Prompt: > "Anything you want to note about today?"

**If journal entry exists already:** Prompt: > "Anything you want to add?"

### Text Box Rules

- 2-3 visible lines max
- Expands only if athlete types
- No minimum length

### Behavioral Rule

- Encourage depth
- Never demand it

### Hard Rules

- ❌ No required word count
- ❌ No emotional grading
- ❌ No auto-prompts mid-entry

## SECTION 4 - Completion & Closure

### Purpose

End the day cleanly.

No motivation. No judgment. No carryover.

### Score Reveal

- Shown neutrally

Example: > Win the Day: 4 / 5

No color-coding. No interpretation.

### Closing Copy

"Logged. Reset. Complete."

### CTA

- Button: Done

On tap: - Save entry - Return to Home

### Hard Rules

- ❌ No streak language
- ❌ No encouragement clichés
- ❌ No tomorrow planning here

Progression is **linear and gated** - not modular.

## Why This Screen Works

This screen: - Forces honesty without shame - Captures behavior data fast - Allows emotional processing without overload - Teaches reset as a skill - Ends the day psychologically closed

It feels like: > A coach saying, "We looked at it. You're done. Get rest."
