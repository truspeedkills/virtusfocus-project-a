WHY THIS VISUAL NEEDS TO EXIST
================================

The Pitch Script is locked and rehearsed. Arron and Nicole
deliver a 30-min tag-team pitch from 12:15 to 12:45.
Currently nothing on the screen during that block. The room
is staring at the speakers for thirty minutes with no visual
anchor.

A simple click-through HTML visual gives attendees a
structural anchor for each pitch section without competing
with the spoken delivery. One slide per section. Arron or
Nicole clicks once at each hand-off seam.

The visual is a presence cue, not a content dump. The
spoken script carries the substance. The visual carries
the frame.

This is scope-adjacent to the Pricing+Deployment and
Cross-Room Synthesis scripts that were authored in the
preceding working session. Those were written first because
they were higher-risk for the day-of operation (un-scripted
30 minutes vs scripted 30 minutes). The pitch visual is
additive enrichment to an already-scripted block.

OPEN DESIGN DECISIONS (RESOLVE IN SESSION)
============================================

These need user collaboration. Pause for each.


OPEN 1: VISUAL STYLE + COLOR PALETTE

Two anchor options:

  (a) MATCH MOCKUP WALKTHROUGH GALLERY AESTHETIC.
      Dark theme (zinc-950 background, amber accents,
      system font stack). Matches the look attendees see
      during the mockup walkthrough block 30 min later.
      Visual continuity across the pitch and walkthrough
      blocks.

  (b) LIGHT THEME WITH SEGMENT-CODED ACCENTS.
      White or off-white background. Segment-coded color
      accents on Screen 5 matching the packet covers
      (deep blue institutional, forest green D2C, amber
      GTM). Distinct visual identity from the walkthrough
      block.

  RECOMMEND (a). Visual continuity across pitch +
  walkthrough is more important than distinct identity for
  the pitch alone. Attendees see the same dark-theme
  aesthetic for an hour, which reads more like one
  cohesive product than two separate stylings.


OPEN 2: SCREEN 4 (PROOF) LAYOUT

Three athletes to show (A/B/C). Options:

  (a) THREE COLUMNS SIDE BY SIDE.
      All three athletes visible at once. Sport, level,
      one-line description per column. Dense but
      compresses the proof story to a single screen.

  (b) ONE AT A TIME, SUB-CLICKS.
      Screen 4 has three sub-states. Arron clicks through
      A then B then C as he tells each story. Sub-clicks
      sync to the spoken hand-offs in Section 3.

  (c) HYBRID: ALL THREE VISIBLE, HIGHLIGHTED ROTATION.
      Three columns visible. Active column highlighted
      with brighter color or border. Sub-clicks rotate the
      highlight. Attendees can see all three but visual
      focus follows the spoken story.

  RECOMMEND (c). Keeps the proof landscape visible (good
  for slow processors who want to scan ahead) while
  reinforcing the spoken narrative with active highlighting.
  Sub-click rotation is light implementation lift.


OPEN 3: TRANSITION STYLE

Click-through transitions between the 5 main screens:

  (a) INSTANT CUT.
      Click and the next screen appears immediately. Zero
      animation. Lowest risk on day-of (no animation glitch
      can derail the pitch).

  (b) FADE TRANSITION.
      Brief 150-200ms fade between screens. Smoother feel.
      Slight risk if browser performance is degraded.

  (c) SLIDE TRANSITION.
      Screens slide in from the right. More cinematic but
      higher implementation cost and animation risk.

  RECOMMEND (a). Smart-board environment is unpredictable
  (different browsers, different hardware, different
  resolutions). Instant cut is the most reliable choice.


OPEN 4: KEYBOARD / CLICK CONTROLS

How does Arron or Nicole advance the screen during the
pitch:

  (a) SPACEBAR / RIGHT ARROW = NEXT.
      Standard presentation control. Wireless clicker
      compatible. Familiar to anyone who has ever used
      Keynote or PowerPoint.

  (b) CLICK ANYWHERE = NEXT.
      Click on the smart-board surface anywhere advances
      the screen. Touch-friendly. No keyboard required.

  (c) BOTH.
      Spacebar / right arrow OR click anywhere both
      advance.

  RECOMMEND (c). Lowest risk, most flexibility. Arron can
  use a wireless clicker if he has one. Or click the
  smart-board if he is standing near it. Either works.


OPEN 5: FOOTER / HEADER TREATMENT

Persistent visual chrome on every screen:

  (a) MINIMAL CHROME.
      Just the screen content. No header, no footer, no
      slide numbers. Maximum content area.

  (b) CORNER BADGE ONLY.
      Small "VirtusFocus - Focus Group - May 9 2026" badge
      in one corner. No slide numbers, no progress bar.

  (c) FULL CHROME.
      Header with the badge plus slide number ("2 of 5")
      and a thin progress bar. Standard presentation
      conventions.

  RECOMMEND (b). The corner badge is a subtle wayfinding
  cue without competing with the screen content. Slide
  numbers are not useful for attendees and can read as
  performative.
