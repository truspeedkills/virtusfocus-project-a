2. PURPOSE
============================================================

The v2.3/V5 validation process surfaced an emergent template
pattern at the DD opening level: the "This [is/was] the [X]
week" construction appears at 75% frequency across 4 DDs in
the validation corpus (3 of 4: Angelo Wk8, Tucker Wk8,
Angelo Wk9 — Grace Wk11 broke the pattern).

75% crosses the 70% monitoring threshold established in
Appendix A Section 9. Per the protocol, this is the signal
to act — not through a new generation rule or Criterion 14
fail condition, but through Voice Reference teaching.

The work of this session: add 2-3 new exemplars to the Voice
Reference that teach alternative DD opening approaches,
breaking the default "This [is/was] the [X] week" pattern
without replacing it with a new template.

This is narrow, high-stakes work. The Voice Reference is the
primary voice authority for Stage 3. Any exemplar added must
meet the existing quality bar (see VF_Coach_Arron_Voice_
Reference.txt Section 4 Pairs 9-11 as the closest design
precedent). A sloppy exemplar addition degrades the teaching
document more than it helps.

3. CONTEXT — WHY NOW, WHY THIS WAY
============================================================

WHY NOW:
The Voice Quality Comparison Analysis established a
monitoring protocol: flag emergent templates at 70%+
frequency across the v2.3 corpus. Appendix B of that same
analysis confirmed Template 2 crossed that threshold.
Acting now is the protocol working as designed, not a
reactive patch.

WHY VOICE REFERENCE (NOT GENERATION RULES):
The v2.3 architecture explicitly chose teaching over
prohibition. Prior versions (v2.1) added rules to Section 35
and found that prohibition produces workaround behavior
(telegraphing survived v2.1's explicit no-telegraphing rule;
only v2.3's exemplar teaching eliminated it). Adding a new
Criterion 14 fail condition or a new Section 35 rule would
repeat the v2.1 mistake.

Voice Reference additions are the designed intervention path
for emergent patterns. The architecture was built for
exactly this: surface a pattern through validation, add a
teaching exemplar, rely on the model to generalize from the
exemplar rather than comply with a rule.

WHY DD-SPECIFIC:
The existing 18 annotated exemplars (Section 3) and 11
before/after pairs (Section 4) are all CM-oriented. None
demonstrate DD opening approaches. This is a known gap
flagged in the Voice Quality Comparison Analysis Section 8
(Remaining Gaps) and Appendix B Section 4. The absence of
DD-opening
exemplars is the structural reason the model converges on
a single default approach for DD openings.

WHY 2-3 EXEMPLARS (NOT ONE):
The "Best Not Better" principle (locked during prior voice
quality improvement work) applies. One new exemplar teaches
one new approach —
sufficient to break the current template but insufficient
to expand range. The model needs to see multiple alternative
DD opening approaches to develop an instinct for variation
rather than settling on whichever exemplar you add as the
new default.

WHY CAREFUL QUALITY MATTERS:
If a new exemplar has its own template-adjacent structure,
the fix becomes the new problem. The prior Voice Reference
Section 4 WHAT CHANGED second-pass rewrite work spent
significant effort on vocabulary tracking (no pedagogical
verb exceeding 2 uses across 8 blocks), instinct-first
framing, and review-process jargon elimination. Those
constraints apply here too.

4. LOCKED DESIGN DECISIONS
============================================================

1. ADDITION GOES INTO VOICE REFERENCE.
   Not generation rules (Section 35). Not Criterion 14.
   Not a framework update. The Voice Reference is the
   designed intervention path.

2. FORMAT = VOICE REFERENCE SECTION 4 BEFORE/AFTER PAIRS.
   Section 4 is the template-retirement mechanism. Before =
   the pattern being retired. After = the alternative that
   expands the range. This matches the precedent set by
   Pairs 6 (triple-negative), 7 (version of X), 8 ("Here is
   what" announcements). Section 4 is the right home for
   template-break work.
   (Rationale: Category A exemplars in Section 3 teach
   general coaching approaches. Section 4 pairs teach
   specific pattern retirements. This work is the latter.)

3. BEFORE EXAMPLES WILL USE REAL PRODUCTION OUTPUT.
   Pair 6 (triple-negative) used Grace Wk10 DD's actual
   opening as the Before example. Same logic applies here:
   the Before example needs to BE the template being
   retired. Angelo Wk8 DD ("This was the first week in your
   program where things didn't go the way you planned"),
   Tucker Wk8 DD, and Angelo Wk9 DD all provide candidate
   Before examples.

4. AFTER EXAMPLES WILL BE FRESHLY WRITTEN.
   Not adapted from existing production output. Grace Wk11
   DD's "Something shifted this week, Grace, and it shifted
   quietly" can serve as a REFERENCE for one acceptable
   alternative approach, but the AFTER examples in the new
   pairs should be written fresh against real athlete data
   (same approach as the Voice Reference Section 4 Pairs
   9-11 design work).

5. 2-3 PAIRS ADDED. NOT ONE, NOT FIVE.
   Two pairs minimum. Three maximum. More than three and
   you're padding. Fewer than two and the teaching lands
   on a single alternative, risking new template emergence.

6. EACH AFTER EXAMPLE MUST DEMONSTRATE A DISTINCT OPENING
   APPROACH.
   The three candidate approaches to choose among:
   (a) "Something shifted" style — opens with state-change
       recognition before naming specifics
   (b) Athlete-quote opening applied to DD — mirrors what
       CM openings do best, opens with the athlete's own
       language and pivots to interpretation
   (c) Single specific moment opening applied to DD —
       opens with one event and lets meaning emerge
   (d) Direct observation — opens with the coach's
       diagnostic read ("The through-line this week was X")
   (e) Other approaches surfaced during collaborative
       design
   Pick 2-3 approaches that are maximally distinct from
   each other AND from the "This [is/was] the [X] week"
   construction being retired.

7. QUALITY CONSTRAINTS (INHERITED FROM SESSION 101/102):
   - Deliberate vocabulary tracking — no pedagogical verb
     exceeding 2 uses across the new pairs
   - Instinct-first framing in WHAT CHANGED blocks (every
     opening sentence names the coaching reason before
     describing the rhetorical structure)
   - Explicit Voice Principle reference in each pair (which
     principle the After example embodies)
   - Zero em dashes
   - Zero review-process jargon ("load-bearing," "contrast-
     reframe," etc.)
   - Zero retired C3 constructions in After examples (the
     retired pattern list from v2.3 Section 35 C3)

8. USER COLLABORATION EXPECTED.
   Voice Reference Section 4 Pairs 9-11 design precedent:
   candidate approaches surfaced, user shaped decisions on

============================================================

   Prior Voice Reference build work established: user
   explicitly said "saving time at the expense of quality
   is a non starter." Same rule applies. If a draft isn't

   The fix must expand expressive range. If the 2-3 new
   exemplars share structural features (e.g., all open with

   distinct from the others AND from the Before pattern

   Cross-reference v2.3 Section 35 C3 list before
   finalizing. Retired constructions: "Here is what [I
   want you to see]," "The version of you that," "I also
   want to name/acknowledge," "[N] weeks into this program"
   (as DD trendline opener), "The question is [whether],"
   "Not X. Y." / "not X, but Y," "The athletes who [X]
   are the ones who [Y]."

   You are adding new pairs, not revising existing ones.
   Pairs 1-11 are sealed. If you discover an issue with an
   existing pair during this session, flag it and defer to
   a future session.

5. DO NOT ADD RULES TO SECTION 35 OR CRITERION 14.
   This work is exemplar addition. Any instinct to write
   "and add a rule prohibiting X" should be redirected to
   "what exemplar teaches the model to avoid X."

6. EM DASH BAN IS ABSOLUTE.
   Zero em dashes in new content. Verify with grep after
   editing.

7. USE COMMAS AND PERIODS, NOT EM DASHES.
   Specific replacement rules from v2.3 Section 35 C1:
   parenthetical → comma; reframe → period or comma + but;
   pivot → colon; dramatic pause → period.

8. PRESERVE USER COLLABORATION RHYTHM.
   This is not a solo build. Surface decisions, propose
