4. GRADING FRAMEWORK — V2.1 RULE-BY-RULE ASSESSMENT
============================================================

For each of the 4 post-v2.1 files, grade every applicable
C-rule from v2.1 Section 35:

C1. EM DASH BAN
    Grade: PASS / FAIL (count violations)
    Check: Zero em dashes in output.

C2. CONTRACTION DEFAULT REGISTER
    Grade: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK
    Check: Are contractions the default? Are formal non-
    contractions reserved for emphasis? Count formal non-
    contractions per document (max 1-2/CM, 3-4/DD). Are the
    formal ones used for genuine emphasis or just randomly
    scattered?

C3. "THAT IS [NOUN]." RULE
    Grade: PASS / FAIL
    Check: Max 1 per CM, 1 per DD, never both same week.
    Are alternatives used when emphatic naming occurs?

C4. PHRASE FREQUENCY LIMITS
    Grade: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK
    Check: Zero retired phrases ("Here is what," "The version
    of you that," "I also want to name/acknowledge," "[N]
    weeks into this program"). Capped phrases within limits.
    3-week non-repeat window respected.

C5. CM-DD DEDUPLICATION
    Grade: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK
    Check: Are the CM and DD genuinely complementary, or do
    they echo each other? Does the DD explore what the CM
    highlights from a different angle?

C6. TRANSITION VARIETY + NO-TELEGRAPHING
    Grade: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK
    Check: Are transitions varied and content-carrying? No
    announcement-before-the-point patterns. Could any
    transition sentence be deleted without loss?

C7. CM OPENING APPROACH VARIETY
    Grade: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK
    Check: What opening approach does each CM use? Is it
    appropriate for the week? Is it different from the prior
    week's opening?

C8. PIPELINE CLASSIFICATION LANGUAGE
    Grade: PASS / FAIL (list violations)
    Check: Zero pipeline jargon in athlete-facing output.
    No JSON field names, no tier names, no RQS scores.

C9. IDENTITY THREAD VARIATION (DD only)
    Grade: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK
    Check: Does the DD pick 1-2 relevant threads or enumerate
    the full baseline? Does the framing evolve from prior
    weeks?

C10. ATHLETE-SPECIFIC CLOSING (CM only)
     Grade: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK
     Check: Does the closing connect to this specific athlete's
     arc, or is it a generic rotation phrase?

5. ADDITIONAL QUALITY DIMENSIONS
============================================================

Beyond the C-rules, assess these quality dimensions:

VOICE AUTHENTICITY
  Would a teammate believe a human coach wrote this? Which
  paragraphs pass that test and which don't? Be specific.
  Quote the problematic passages.

SENTENCE VARIETY
  Is there rhythmic variation, or does the output fall into
  a predictable pattern? Count sentence lengths across a
  paragraph. Note any passages where every sentence follows
  the same subject-verb-object structure.

SPECIFICITY VS ABSTRACTION
  Does the output ground observations in specific behavioral
  evidence, or drift into abstract coaching philosophy?
  Athlete-specific detail is a known strength of the pipeline.
  Has v2.1 preserved that strength, or have the new rules
  diluted it?

EMOTIONAL CALIBRATION
  Does the tone match the week's emotional intensity?
  Grace Wk9 is Low EI with significant developmental content.
  Tucker Wk7 is Low EI with a "doing more than saying"
  dynamic. Are the tones appropriately distinct?

LENGTH AND DENSITY
  Are the CMs within the 250-450 word target? Are the DDs
  within 850-1200 words? Is every paragraph earning its
  space, or are there passages that pad without adding value?

11. CRITICAL OPERATING RULES
============================================================

- This is a GRADING session, not a regeneration session.
  The primary output is a quality assessment and discussion,
  not new coaching files. Regeneration only happens if the
  user decides specific fixes are needed AND locks decisions.

- The user has noticed issues. Take those seriously. Do not
  grade the output charitably. Be honest about what works,
  what doesn't, and what the rules failed to address.

- Quote specific passages. Vague assessments like "mostly
  good" or "some issues" are not useful. Cite the line,
  explain why it's a problem, and show what it should look
  like instead.

- Compare fairly. The pre-v2.1 output had known problems
  (the audit identified them). The question is whether v2.1
  ACTUALLY FIXED those problems, introduced new ones, or
  left gaps.

- If rules are being followed but the output still sounds
  AI-generated, the rules aren't sufficient. That finding
  is valuable. New rules may be needed.

- "Best not Better" means sometimes the answer is fewer
  rules, not more. If a rule is creating a new template
  pattern, the fix might be removing or loosening the rule.

- Do NOT regenerate any files without explicit user approval.
  Present findings and recommendations first. Lock decisions
  collaboratively. Then execute if the user decides changes
  are needed.
