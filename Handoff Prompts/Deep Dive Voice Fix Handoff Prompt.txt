==============================================

Fix a systemic voice and tone issue across ALL Deep Dive coaching outputs. The Deep Dives currently read as third-person analytical coach-to-coach performance reviews instead of athlete-facing content from Coach Arron. The coaching content and accuracy are correct — the problem is presentation, voice, and tone.

This session has two phases:
1. Revise the Deep Dive Framework specification (the root cause)

==============================================

The Deep Dive is supposed to be the athlete's deeper look into their mental performance journey — written by Coach Arron, addressed TO the athlete, helping them understand what they're building and what to focus on. Instead, the current Deep Dives read like scouting reports or analytical performance reviews that a sport psychologist would write for a coaching staff.

==============================================
WHAT THE DEEP DIVE SHOULD BE
==============================================

The Deep Dive is the athlete's deeper look into their mental performance journey. It is written by Coach Arron, addressed TO the athlete, in second person. It goes deeper than the Coaching Message — where the CM is 250-450 words hitting the highlights, the DD is the full picture: what happened this week, what it means for the athlete's development, where strengths showed up, where growth opportunities exist, and how the current week fits into the larger arc.

KEY DISTINCTIONS:
- Coaching Message = "Here's what stood out and what to do next" (short, action-oriented)
- Deep Dive = "Here's the full picture of your week and what it means for your journey" (comprehensive, insight-oriented)

Both are athlete-facing. Both use Coach Arron's voice. The DD is NOT a coach-to-coach analytical report. It is NOT a performance review. It is a coaching conversation that happens to be more detailed.

The Deep Dive should feel like sitting across from your mental performance coach and hearing: "Let me walk you through what I saw this week and what it means for where you're headed."

VOICE REQUIREMENTS:
- Second person throughout ("you," "your")
- Coach Arron persona (same as CM — calm authority, direct, identity-focused)
- Athlete's first name used naturally (not as a clinical subject reference)
- The athlete should feel like the DD was written FOR them, not ABOUT them
- No pipeline terminology (no "behavioral evidence," no "RQS=4," no "Growth tier")
- Analytical depth is GOOD — the fix is voice and framing, not dumbing down the content

PHASE 1: REVISE THE DEEP DIVE FRAMEWORK
==============================================

Read the current DD Framework first:
- Agents - Generators\Coaching Output\Source FIles\VF_Deep_Dive_Coaching_Analysis_Framework.txt

Then revise it. The revised framework should:

1. ADD a clear voice directive (second person, Coach Arron, athlete-facing — match the CM Framework's clarity)

2. REPLACE the Quality Rule. The new rule should capture "analytically rich coaching content" without pushing toward "performance review." Something like: "Feels like a detailed coaching conversation — specific, evidence-grounded, and personally addressed. Not a report about the athlete. A message to the athlete."

3. REVISE section headers and sub-element framing:
   - "Behavioral Evidence" → integrate naturally into coaching language (the evidence still appears, but woven into the coach's voice, not formatted as a data extract)
   - "Coach Insight (italicized line)" → this framing is backwards. The ENTIRE DD is the coach's insight. If you keep a closing line per strength/growth item, frame it as a coaching observation TO the athlete, not an insight FOR a coach.
   - "Pattern Explanation" → same treatment — explain the pattern TO the athlete in coaching voice

4. KEEP the mandatory 5-section structure. The sections themselves are fine — the issue is how they're written, not what they cover:
   - Section 1: Mindset Summary (what your week looked like from a mental performance standpoint)
   - Section 2: Weekly Trendline (how this week fits into your journey so far)
   - Section 3: Strength Signals (what you did well and why it matters)
   - Section 4: Growth Areas (where the development opportunities are)
   - Section 5: Identity Thread (who you're becoming and the arc you're building)

   NOTE: The current section order in the DD Framework is different from how the DDs are actually being produced. The framework says: Weekly Overview, Strengths & Wins, Opportunities & Growth Areas, Mindset Summary, Coaching Focus Areas. The actual DDs (especially Jasmine's) use: Mindset Summary, Weekly Trendline, Strength Signals, Growth Areas, Identity Thread. The ACTUAL structure is better — align the framework to match. The Coaching Focus Areas / micro-commitment content belongs in the CM, not the DD.

5. KEEP the Integration Requirements (Win the Day, Bullseye, Reflection Quality, Growth Phase references) — these ensure the DD is grounded in the JSON data. Just ensure they're expressed in coaching voice.

6. ADD examples showing the voice difference:
   WRONG: "Jasmine's third week introduces a significant behavioral development: frustration-to-execution channeling as a conscious self-regulation strategy."
   RIGHT: "This was the week you discovered something important about yourself. Those frustrating moments with teammates — instead of letting them derail you, you turned that energy into your best lifting sessions of the program. Wednesday and Friday both followed the same pattern: you got frustrated, you locked in, and you lifted heavier than any other day. You noticed it yourself and even wondered if you could use it on purpose. That's a self-management skill, and you're building it in real time."

   WRONG: "Six-week trajectory from self-referenced training ownership (Week 1) through intentional momentum with NBC derailer test passed (Week 2) to frustration-channeling self-regulation (Week 3)..."
   RIGHT: "Six weeks in, here's what your journey looks like. You started by owning your training on your own terms. Week 2 tested you when you weren't selected for the NBC group — and you responded by treating it as fuel, not a setback. Week 3 showed you could channel frustration into your best lifting sessions..."
