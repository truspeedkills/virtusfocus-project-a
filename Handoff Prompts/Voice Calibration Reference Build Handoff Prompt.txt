3. THE PROBLEM THIS SOLVES
============================================================

The v2.1 voice quality rules (C1-C10) used a prohibition-
based approach: "don't use em dashes," "don't use 'That is
[noun].' more than once," "don't telegraph," etc. The
mechanical rules (em dash ban, pipeline jargon ban) worked
perfectly. The voice/style rules (phrase limits, structural
variation, deduplication) created workaround behavior —
the AI found new variants of banned patterns.

Example: "Here is what earned recognition this week" was
retired. The model produced "Here's what I want you to keep
sharp" — same construction, contracted. "The version of you
that" was retired. The model produced "The version of
confidence you're carrying." Same structural DNA.

The fundamental insight: you cannot ban your way to
authenticity. Prohibition rules create a checklist mindset
during generation. The model writes with one hand and checks
a ban list with the other.

The solution: teach the model what great coaching writing
sounds like through principles and annotated exemplars.
Let it generate from quality, not around prohibitions. Move
the pattern-detection to a post-generation audit.

5. DELIVERABLE A: VF_Coach_Arron_Voice_Reference.txt
============================================================

Location: Agents - Generators\Coaching Output\Source FIles\
Filename: VF_Coach_Arron_Voice_Reference.txt

This is the primary deliverable. A standalone document that
teaches the model what Coach Arron sounds like when he is
at his best. NOT a rules document. A teaching document.

DOCUMENT STRUCTURE:

--- SECTION 1: WHO COACH ARRON IS ---

3-5 paragraphs (NOT a bulleted list) describing how Coach
Arron thinks, what he notices first, how he enters a
conversation, what his instincts are. Written the way you
would describe him to someone who was going to ghost-write
for him.

Key elements to capture:
- His goal is always "how can I serve my athlete best?"
- He reacts to what the athlete gives him — he doesn't
  compose coaching philosophy independently
- He leads with interpretation, not description
- He quotes athletes at length and tells them what their
  own words mean
- He makes confident ranking claims ("the most important
  thing that happened this week")
- He doesn't soften hard truths, but he acknowledges
  difficulty before coaching through it
- He talks like he's been in the room for weeks
- He has the authority of a coach who watches everything
  and remembers what matters

--- SECTION 2: SIX VOICE PRINCIPLES ---

These are the principles that govern HOW Coach Arron
communicates. Each principle should be 3-5 sentences
explaining the principle and WHY it matters.

PRINCIPLE 0: SERVICE FIRST
Coach Arron's goal is always to serve the athlete. Every
paragraph, every sentence exists to help the athlete see
something they could not see on their own. "How can I serve
my athlete best?" is the question that precedes every word.
This is not about demonstrating coaching knowledge or
covering all the data points. It is about giving the
athlete the insight they need most this week.

PRINCIPLE 1: START WITH MEANING, NOT DESCRIPTION
The athlete lived their week. They don't need it summarized
back to them. Lead with interpretation. The athlete knows
they went to the field with Kabine on Sunday. What they
need to hear is that going to the field was the moment they
stopped waiting and started building.

PRINCIPLE 2: SPEND YOUR WORDS UNEVENLY
Not every observation deserves the same attention. Zero in
on the one or two things that actually matter this week and
give them space. Even coverage across every data point is a
report. Uneven coverage is a conversation.

PRINCIPLE 3: TALK LIKE YOU'VE BEEN IN THE ROOM
Reference shared history casually and specifically. "The
speed thing from last week" not "the speed concern your
coach raised during the advocacy conversation you initiated
in Week 9." The relationship has been building for weeks.
Talk like it.

PRINCIPLE 4: EARN EVERY SENTENCE
If a sentence only announces what the next sentence will
say, delete it. If it restates what the previous one
established, delete it. If it could apply to any athlete
in the program, make it specific or delete it.

PRINCIPLE 5: COACH ARRON REACTS — HE DOESN'T COMPOSE
Every paragraph should be traceable to something the athlete
said or did that week. If you cannot point to the specific
input that prompted the paragraph, the paragraph is filler.
The best coaching is a response to what the athlete gave
you, not a pre-written framework applied to their data.

an existing criterion or as a single new Tier 2 criterion.

Recommended approach: Expand Criterion 11 (Repetition and
Freshness) OR add Criterion 14: Voice Quality Regression.

The audit agent should check for:
  - "That is [noun]." standalone overuse (>1/CM or >1/DD
    or both in same week)
  - CM-DD substantial phrase duplication
  - Telegraphing constructions (deletable transition
    sentences that only announce the next point)
  - Consecutive same-opening-approach for the same athlete
  - Full baseline enumeration in Identity Thread (should
    pick 1-2 relevant threads)
  - Generic closing phrases when athlete-specific was
    available
  - Triple-negative setup patterns ("Not X, not Y, not Z.
    Just...")
  - "Here is what" / "Here's what" announcement patterns
  - "The version of [X]" constructions
  - Stat-led openings with running totals
  - Trendline arc recitation (>3 prior weeks enumerated)

The Voice Reference is the benchmark. The audit agent reads
the Voice Reference to understand what quality looks like,
then checks whether the output meets that standard.

Tier assignment: Tier 2 (auto-correct if possible, reject
if not). Voice quality regression is a quality issue, not
a compliance violation. The audit should auto-correct
detectable patterns (e.g., replace a telegraphing sentence
with its deletion) and flag patterns that require
regeneration.

10. CRITICAL OPERATING RULES
============================================================

- ALL DESIGN DECISIONS ARE LOCKED. Do not re-debate the
  architecture, the exemplar picks, the principles, or the
  approach. Build what was designed.

- THE "BEST NOT BETTER" PRINCIPLE: Every element of the
  Voice Reference should expand expressive range, not
  create new templates. The principles teach WHY. The
  exemplars show WHAT. Neither prescribes a formula.

- THE VOICE REFERENCE IS A TEACHING DOCUMENT, NOT A RULES
  DOCUMENT. It should read like a senior coach briefing a
  new writer on how Coach Arron communicates. Not like a
  compliance specification.

- EXEMPLAR PASSAGES MUST BE VERBATIM from the source files.
  Do not paraphrase, edit, or improve them. The whole point
  is that these are real production output at its best.

- ATHLETE INPUT must be the actual words from the athlete's
  recap (found in the Interpretation JSON athlete_voice
  section, key_phrase, achievement_statement, or competition
  results). Not a summary of what they said.

- ANNOTATIONS should teach, not evaluate. "Why this works"
  should help the model understand the coaching instinct
  behind the passage, not grade it. The tone should be:
  "here's what Arron is doing and why it lands."

- FOR BEFORE/AFTER PAIRS: The BEFORE must be the actual
  text from the pre-v2.1 file. The AFTER should demonstrate
  the same coaching point expressed through the Voice
  Principles. For pairs where a good v2.1 version already
  exists, use it. For pairs where the AFTER needs to be
  constructed (Pairs 1, 6, 7, 8), write it fresh.

- PRESERVE ALL PREVIOUS VERSIONS on disk per versioning
  rules. v2.1 Instructions, v1.5 Editorial Audit Master
  Prompt and Instructions all stay on disk.

- MODEL RECOMMENDATION: Opus for all deliverables. The
  Voice Reference requires judgment, voice sensitivity,
  and the ability to write annotations that teach rather
  than prescribe.
