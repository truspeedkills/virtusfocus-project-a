THE CRITICAL STAGE 4 CONSTRAINT: execution_behavior_signals is INFORM-ONLY for Stage 4.

This is the established design principle from the Execution Signal Strategy (confirmed 2026-03-06):
- Raw execution data (completion rates, timing, backfill counts, reminder dependency, streak counts, component counts, question-level yes rates) NEVER reaches coaching staff in any form
- Composite scores and coach flags are consumed INTERNALLY by the engine to produce better compliance-safe sections — sharper Key Performance Signal, more accurate Composite Readiness, better Coach Context Cue
- The inform-only routing pattern is identical to how coach_insights subfields already work: they inform the output but their raw content is never reproduced
- This aligns with VF_Coach_Insights_Compliance_Framework.txt which prohibits: time spent in app, daily completion counts, last login timestamps, engagement scores, compliance or adherence ratings, streak counts

KEY DESIGN QUESTIONS REQUIRING USER INPUT:

DECISION 1 — COMPOSITE READINESS ENRICHMENT:
Currently Section 10 uses v9.3 fields only (weekly_tier, consistency_profile, recommitment_signal, primary_focus_ring, emotional_intensity via Stress Load). For App athletes, composite scores and coach flags could provide additional readiness data.

Options:
A) Additional modifier step (like Stress Load modifier): After the base determination (v9.3 fields) and Stress Load modifier, add a Step 2.5 "Execution Signal modifier" that can shift the readiness level for App athletes based on composite score patterns and coach flag severity.
B) Integrated into base determination: Composite score bands feed directly into the Step 1 determination rules alongside v9.3 fields.
C) Context-only: Composite scores and flags inform the readiness sentence but do not change the readiness level.

Suggested approach: Option A (additional modifier). This preserves the v9.3 base determination (no degradation for CF athletes), adds execution enrichment for App athletes, and keeps the modification logic clean and auditable. The modifier rules would be:
- If action-severity coach flags fire → shift one level toward Attention
- If 3+ composite scores in concern bands (same logic as compound_disengagement) → shift one level toward Attention
- Modifiers are NOT cumulative with each other (maximum one shift from execution signals)
- Modifiers ARE cumulative with Stress Load modifier (an athlete could shift twice: once from stress, once from execution)

DECISION 2 — KPS PRIORITY INTEGRATION:
Currently Section 8 has a 5-level priority list (outer-ring focus → consistency volatility → low recommitment → reactive despite Growth → derive from most_important_controllable). For App athletes, coach flags could be more urgent signals.

Options:
A) Prepend to priority list: Action flags at priority 0 (highest), attention flags between current priorities 1 and 2
B) Override: When action flags fire, they override the v9.3 priority list entirely
C) Inform only: Coach flags sharpen the KPS explanation but don't change priority selection

Suggested approach: Option A. This gives execution signals appropriate priority without discarding the v9.3 logic. Proposed updated priority:
0) action-severity coach flag fires → KPS derived from the flag's behavioral pattern (e.g., compound_disengagement → "Broad Engagement Shift")
1) primary_focus_ring = Cannot Control OR noise_fixation = Yes → Controllable Focus Alignment
1.5) attention-severity coach flag fires → KPS derived from the flag's behavioral pattern (e.g., drift_active → "Engagement Pattern Drift")
2) consistency_profile = Volatile or Reset Pattern → Routine Stability
3) recommitment_signal = Low → Adversity Response
4) Growth + reactive athlete_state → Composure Under Pressure
5) Derive from most_important_controllable

DECISION 3 — SELF-RATINGS ALIGNMENT IN CONFIDENCE MOMENTUM:
Currently Section 5 derives confidence trend from v9.3 fields (weekly_tier + consistency_profile + recommitment_signal + focus_ring). For App athletes, self_ratings_alignment.confidence_alignment provides a direct self-vs-execution comparison.

Options:
A) Confidence alignment as adjustment: Conflated → bump trend one level toward Variable; Undervalued → bump one level toward Building
B) Confidence alignment as override: When confidence is Conflated or Undervalued, it replaces the v9.3-derived trend
C) Context-only: Confidence alignment informs the section sentence but doesn't change the trend classification

Suggested approach: Option A. Conflated self-view (athlete rates confidence higher than execution supports) should temper the confidence trend. Undervalued self-view (execution exceeds self-rating) should boost it.

DECISION 4 — REACTIVITY RISK IN STRESS LOAD:
Section 6 uses emotional_intensity as primary input with a v9.3 secondary validation. For App athletes, Reactivity Risk Score provides execution-behavior-based reactivity data.

Options:
A) Reactivity Risk as additional secondary validation: If emotional_intensity = insufficient data AND v9.3 secondary validation = insufficient data, use Reactivity Risk Score band (Elevated → Elevated stress)
B) Reactivity Risk as tertiary validation (after v9.3 secondary)
C) No integration — emotional_intensity and the v9.3 validation are sufficient

Suggested approach: Option A or C. Reactivity Risk is conceptually related to stress load but measures execution volatility, not emotional load. If integrated, it should only serve as a final fallback, not a primary or secondary input.
