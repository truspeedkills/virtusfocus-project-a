4. ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK
============================================================

Analyze the output across these 5 dimensions. For each
dimension, compare Week 8 against the three prior weeks.
Use specific quotes and line references.

------------------------------------------------------------
DIMENSION 1: CRITERION 14 PATTERN PRESENCE
------------------------------------------------------------

Check all 8 files (4 CMs + 4 DDs) against each of the 12
Criterion 14 fail conditions. For each condition, note:
  - Present in Week 3? Week 5? Week 7? Week 8?
  - If present, quote the specific instance
  - If absent in Week 8 but present in prior weeks, note
    the improvement

The 12 fail conditions:
  14.1  Reusable Opening (could work for another athlete
        with surface edits)
  14.2  Telegraphing ("I'm going to be direct," "Here's
        what I want you to hear")
  14.3  Untraceable Paragraph (no specific behavioral
        evidence from this athlete's week)
  14.4  Category Coverage Over Response (hitting every data
        point vs coaching the athlete)
  14.5  Brand Copy Close (generic motivational ending not
        grounded in this week)
  14.6  Same Construction 3-Week Window (repeated rhetorical
        structure across recent weeks)
  14.7  DD as Expanded CM (Deep Dive reads like a longer
        version of the CM, not a different depth)
  14.8  "That is [Noun]." Standalone Overuse
  14.9  "Version of [X]" Construction
  14.10 Triple-Negative Setup ("Not X, not Y, but Z")
  14.11 Trendline Arc Recitation (full program history
        enumeration instead of 2-3 relevant inflection
        points)
  14.12 Pipeline-Adjacent Vocabulary ("approach-framed,"
        "pattern_shift_detected," "Growth weeks," etc.)

------------------------------------------------------------
DIMENSION 2: VOICE PRINCIPLE ALIGNMENT
------------------------------------------------------------

Assess how well Week 8 output embodies the 6 Voice
Principles from the Voice Reference, compared to prior
weeks. For each principle, note whether Week 8 demonstrates
it more naturally than prior output.

The 6 principles:
  P0: Service First ("How can I serve this athlete best
      this week?")
  P1: Start with Meaning, Not Description (lead with why
      it matters, not what happened)
  P2: Spend Your Words Unevenly (proportional attention to
      what matters most, not equal coverage)
  P3: Talk Like You've Been in the Room (specific behavioral
      knowledge, not generic coaching)
  P4: Earn Every Sentence (every sentence carries weight,
      no filler)
  P5: React, Don't Compose (respond to the athlete's week,
      don't construct a template around the data)

------------------------------------------------------------
DIMENSION 3: MECHANICAL COMPLIANCE
------------------------------------------------------------

Quick binary checks across all 8 files:
  - Em dashes: count per file (should be 0 in Week 8)
  - Contractions: present as natural register?
  - C3 retired constructions: "The question is [whether],"
    "Not X. Y. / not X, but Y," "The athletes who [X] are
    the ones who [Y]" — present in any file?
  - Pipeline classification language: any instances?
  - "That is [Noun]." standalone count per file

------------------------------------------------------------
DIMENSION 4: COACHING AUTHENTICITY
------------------------------------------------------------

This is the qualitative dimension. For each CM and DD,
assess:

  A. Does it sound like a real coach who knows this athlete,
     or like an AI processing a JSON?
  B. Is the opening grounded in a specific moment from this
     week, or could it work for any athlete?
  C. Does the coaching move (recognition, challenge, drift
     identification) feel reactive to what happened, or
     constructed around what the data contains?
  D. Does the Deep Dive add genuine analytical depth beyond
     the CM, or does it read like an expanded version of the
     same content?
  E. Is the language specific enough that you could identify
     which athlete and which week this was written for?

Rate each file: STRONG / ADEQUATE / WEAK on authenticity.
Quote the strongest and weakest passages.

------------------------------------------------------------
DIMENSION 5: EXPRESSIVE RANGE
------------------------------------------------------------

This maps to the "Best Not Better" principle established in
Session 92: fixes must expand expressive range, not swap one
template for another.

Compare the rhetorical variety across weeks:
  - Opening approaches: does Week 8 use a different approach
    than prior weeks, or does it follow a template?
  - Sentence rhythm: does Week 8 vary sentence length and
    structure more naturally?
  - Recognition moves: does Week 8 recognize athlete
    behavior in a way that feels fresh, or does it use the
    same recognition template as prior weeks?
  - Closing moves: does Week 8 close differently than prior
    weeks?

The goal is not that Week 8 is "better" in a single
direction. It is that the expressive RANGE is wider — the
output has more ways to coach, not just a cleaner version
of the same way.
