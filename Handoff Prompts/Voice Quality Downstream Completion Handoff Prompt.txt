TASK A: STAGE 4 VOICE QUALITY UPDATE
  Coach Insights Engine produces THREE types of human-facing
  output:
    1. Weekly Performance Insight (coach-facing)
    2. Weekly Team Snapshot (coach-facing)
    3. Parent Weekly Insight (parent-facing)

  All three reach human audiences (coaches or parents).
  None currently have voice quality rules from v2.1.

  The voice quality rules that APPLY to Stage 4 output:
    - Em dash ban (consistency across all pipeline output)
    - Pipeline classification language prohibition (coaches
      and parents should see behavioral descriptions, not
      JSON field names or pipeline terminology)
    - No-telegraphing (applies to any prose output)
    - Contraction default (Coach Arron voice in parent
      output; institutional register in coach output may
      be slightly more formal but still natural)

  The voice quality rules that DO NOT apply to Stage 4:
    - "That is [noun]." limits (athlete-facing construction)
    - CM opening variety (no CM structure in Stage 4)
    - CM-DD deduplication (no CM-DD pair)
    - Identity Thread variation (no identity thread section)
    - Athlete-specific closing phrases (no closing phrase)
    - Phrase frequency limits from C4 (the specific retired
      phrases like "Here is what" are Stage 3 constructions;
      Stage 4 has its own section structure)

  DELIVERABLES:

    - SOP_Coach_Insights_Project_Instructions_v1.8.txt (new
      version)

  SCOPE OF CHANGES:
    Master Prompt v1.4:
      - Remove any em dashes from prose

      - Add note that all human-facing output (coach and
        parent) must comply with pipeline classification
        language prohibition and em dash ban
      - Update parent output signature from "— Coach Arron"
        to "Coach Arron" if present

    Instructions v1.8:
      - Add a new section (after existing parent sections,
        before Final Rule) covering voice quality rules
        applicable to Stage 4 output
      - Em dash ban (zero tolerance in all output)
      - Pipeline classification language prohibition with
        Stage 4-specific WRONG/CORRECT examples:
          WRONG: "Emotional intensity returned to Low"
          RIGHT: "The emotional weight of the week was
          lighter compared to last week"
          WRONG: "Growth phase: Developing maintained"
          RIGHT: "The athlete continues to build foundational

          RIGHT: The Composite Readiness signal IS the

          Stage 4 output labels themselves.

        voice with natural contractions; coach-facing output
        uses institutional register (slightly more formal
        but still natural prose, not stiff academic writing)

      - Update any internal references to other specs that
        changed version (v2.0→v2.1 for Coaching Output
        Instructions, if referenced)

  IMPORTANT NUANCE — STAGE 4 COMPLIANCE-SAFE LABELS:
    Stage 4 already has its own compliance framework. Terms
    like "Composite Readiness: Positive (Green)," "Key
    Performance Signal," "Stress Load: Moderate," and the
    section names (Sections 1-10 for coach, T1-T7 for team,
    9 sections for parent) are the DESIGNED coach-facing
    translations. These are NOT pipeline classification
    language — they ARE the product.

    The prohibition targets:
    - Raw JSON field names used as labels ("emotional_
      intensity," "growth_phase_movement," "consistency_
      signal")
    - Stage 2 internal classifications surfaced verbatim
      ("Stable Positive," "Partially Aligned," "approach-
      framed," "pattern_shift_detected")
    - Execution signal internals ("Ownership Index: 72,"
      "drift_score.band = Early")
    - Pipeline metadata ("RQS 2," "v9.7 schema")

    The Stage 4 output structure and terminology (section
    names, Composite Readiness, KPS, stress categories,
    dashboard metrics) are compliance-safe by design.
