WHY THESE TWO SCRIPTS NEED TO EXIST
=====================================

The May 9 event has two blocks at the end of the day that
are currently un-scripted:

  2:15-2:30 PM   Pricing + Deployment Block (15 min, full-room)
  2:30-3:00 PM   Cross-Room Synthesis (30 min, full-room)

Both are signal-dense. Pricing + Deployment is the focused
full-room read on subscription tiers, institutional contract
structure, and white-label-vs-single-app architecture. Cross-
Room Synthesis is the only structured moment when each segment
shares findings to the full room AND the "what we missed"
prompt opens the floor.

Without scripts these blocks default to Arron's improvisation.
That works because he knows the territory, but it puts the
most strategic 45 minutes of the day on freestyle. Note-takers
don't know what to capture without anchored prompts. Timing
slips when there's no enforced structure (a 5-minute report
becomes 12 minutes).

Both scripts are lighter than the Pitch Script. Closer in
shape to Welcome_and_NDA_Flow.txt: structured outline, key
spoken language, prompts, stage directions in [BRACKETS],
contingency notes.

OPEN 1: PRICING + DEPLOYMENT BLOCK STRUCTURE

How to structure the 15-min block? Options:

  (a) Three sequential prompts, each ~4-5 min:
      P1: D2C subscription pricing read
      P2: Institutional contract structure read
      P3: White-label vs single-app architecture read
      No room for follow-up Q&A within the block.

  (b) One framing question + open discussion:
      "We need your honest read on three things: monthly
      D2C price ceiling, what an institutional contract
      should look like, and white-label vs single-app.
      What do you want to push back on first?"
      More organic but harder to control timing.

  (c) Hybrid:
      Arron names the three questions up front. Attendees
      pick which one they want to react to first. ~5 min
      per topic but order is attendee-driven.

  RECOMMEND option (c). Names all three so the room knows
  what's on the table; lets the room choose where to engage
  first; keeps timing tight.


OPEN 2: CROSS-ROOM SYNTHESIS STRUCTURE

30 minutes, full room, three breakouts to report. Options:

  (a) Round-robin segment reports:
      Each facilitator (Arron, Nicole, Michele) gives 5-min
      report from their breakout. 15 min total. Then 10 min
      open Q&A from the full room. Then 5 min "what we
      missed" prompt.

  (b) Theme-based synthesis:
      Arron names 3-4 themes that surfaced across rooms.
      Each facilitator chimes in on their room's read on
      each theme. ~6 min per theme. Skip individual room
      reports.

  (c) Quick reports + extended "what we missed":
      3 facilitators give 3-min reports each (9 min). Then
      Arron opens "what we missed" for 18 min. The "what we
      missed" prompt becomes the headline.

  RECOMMEND option (a). Each room gets full airtime. The
  open Q&A handles cross-room dynamics. The "what we
  missed" prompt closes the day with the highest-leverage
  unanswered question.


OPEN 3: "WHAT WE MISSED" FRAMING

Two phrasings worth considering:

  (a) "What did we not ask that we should have?"
      This is what the worksheets ended with. Repeating it
      on stage reinforces the question and gives attendees
      who answered it on paper a chance to surface it
      verbally.

  (b) "What are we wrong about?"
      Bolder framing. Pushes for direct contradiction
      rather than additive feedback. Higher signal but
      harder to get attendees to answer publicly.

  RECOMMEND (a). It matches the worksheets, gives a
  natural bridge from written answers to spoken
  surfacing, and is the lower-friction question to
  answer in front of the room.


OPEN 4: CLOSING REMARKS SCOPE

Cross-Room Synthesis closes the day. What should the
final 2-3 minutes cover? Candidates:

  - Thank-you (mandatory)
  - NDA continues to apply (mandatory)
  - Synthesis Report v1 timeline (May 16, 7 days post-

  - Digital artifact copies available on request as
