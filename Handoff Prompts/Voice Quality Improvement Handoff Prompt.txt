============================================================
2. PURPOSE OF THIS SESSION
============================================================

This is a COLLABORATIVE SESSION to improve the voice
quality of all athlete-facing coaching output in the
VirtusFocus pipeline. The audit identified specific AI-
detectable patterns that undermine authenticity. This
session fixes those patterns at the specification level
and then regenerates all athlete-facing output.

The core question: "If an athlete showed this message to
a teammate and said 'my coach wrote this,' would the
teammate believe it was written by a human coach?"

The pipeline's coaching CONTENT is strong — behaviorally
grounded, athlete-specific, analytically sound. The
problem is in the EXPRESSION — repetitive constructions,
formulaic transitions, predictable structures, and
punctuation habits that fingerprint the output as AI-
generated.

THIS SESSION HAS TWO PHASES:

PHASE 1 — SPECIFICATION CHANGES (collaborative)
  Review each audit recommendation with the user. Lock
  decisions. Update specification files. This phase
  requires user approval before proceeding.

PHASE 2 — OUTPUT REGENERATION (execution)
  Regenerate the last 4 weeks per athlete (48 files: 24
  CMs + 24 DDs) under the updated specifications. These
  are not being sent to athletes — they validate the
  voice improvements and set the standard for all future
  output.

============================================================
3. WHAT THE AUDIT FOUND — SUMMARY FOR CONTEXT
============================================================

Overall Assessment: ADEQUATE — individual paragraphs
frequently pass the human-coach test, but structural
patterns become detectable across multiple files.

TOP 3 AI-DETECTABLE PATTERNS:

1. "That is [noun]." standalone declarative — appears in
   EVERY file (~80+ instances). The #1 AI fingerprint.
   Example: "That is advocacy." "That is ownership."
   "That is discipline."

2. Trendline arc recitation — Deep Dives retell the full
   program history every week using hyphenated compound
   adjectives. Grace Wk9 has a 9-item enumeration in one
   sentence. Jasmine Wk6 and Wk7 trendlines are nearly
   verbatim for the first 5-6 paragraphs.

3. Cross-athlete structural cloning — All 7 athletes
   receive DDs with identical internal architecture.
   Same section order, same Strength Signal format
   (heading + paragraph + italicized capper at 100%
   consistency), same opening patterns.

TOP 3 STRENGTHS (preserve these):

1. Athlete-specific behavioral grounding — references
   specific days, events, and athlete's own words.

2. Domain and competitive level calibration — sport-
   specific language is precise across all 7 athletes.

3. High EI week handling — the most emotionally authentic
   writing occurs during disrupted or High EI weeks.

------------------------------------------------------------
ROUND 1: HIGH PRIORITY (4 recommendations)
------------------------------------------------------------

H1. CAP "THAT IS [NOUN]." STANDALONE DECLARATIVE
  Audit finding: 80+ instances across ~50 files. Every
  athlete. Every file type. Every week. The #1 AI-
  detectable pattern.
  Proposed rule: Maximum 2 per CM, maximum 2 per DD.
  Force variation: embed judgments mid-sentence, use
  different constructions, or let evidence speak without
  a declarative stamp.
  Example alternatives:
    BEFORE: "That is ownership."
    AFTER:  "You owned that moment."
    AFTER:  "Ownership, plain and simple."
    AFTER:  (delete — let the evidence stand)

  FILES TO CHANGE: SOP_Coaching_Output_Instructions
  (new frequency limit rule), VF_Coaching_Message_
  Framework (voice guidance), VF_Deep_Dive_Coaching_
  Analysis_Framework (voice guidance)

H2. ELIMINATE TRENDLINE ARC RECITATION (after Week 4)
  Audit finding: DD trendlines retell the athlete's
  entire program from Week 1 every week, growing longer.
  Grace Wk9 has 9 hyphenated compound adjectives in one
  sentence. Jasmine Wk6/Wk7 are nearly verbatim for
  their first 5-6 paragraphs.
  Proposed rule: After Week 4, the Trendline references
  2-3 key inflection points relevant to the current
  week. Never enumerate every prior week. Never use
  hyphenated compound-adjective arc labels. Replace
  percentage citations with qualitative descriptors
  ("you have won almost every day" not "96.4 percent").

  FILES TO CHANGE: VF_Deep_Dive_Coaching_Analysis_
  Framework (Trendline section rewrite)

H3. PHRASE FREQUENCY LIMITS
  Audit finding: Multiple phrases appear at template-
  detectable frequency across ALL athletes.
  Proposed caps:
    "The question is [whether]...": 0/CM, 1/DD max
    "Not X — Y" correction: 1/CM, 2/DD max
      (note: em dashes banned, so this becomes
       "Not X. Y." or "not X, but Y" constructions)
    "Here is what [verb]...": RETIRE entirely
    "The version of you that...": RETIRE entirely
    "I also want to name/acknowledge...": RETIRE from CMs
    "[N] weeks into this program...": 0/DD (retire as
      trendline opener — use specific behavioral opener)
    "The athletes who [X] are the ones who [Y]": max 1
      per DD, never in consecutive weeks for same athlete

  FILES TO CHANGE: SOP_Coaching_Output_Instructions
  (new frequency limit section)

H4. RESTORE CONTRACTIONS AS DEFAULT REGISTER
  Audit finding: Grace Wk1 CM uses contractions and
  sounds more natural. Later weeks shifted to formal
  no-contraction English ("did not," "it is," "That is")
  which sounds stiffer and more AI-like. Even Jasmine
  (Professional level) has 0% contractions across all CMs.
  Proposed rule: Contractions are the default register
  for Coach Arron. "Don't," "isn't," "wasn't," "you're,"
  "that's," "here's," "it's," "I'm" are standard speech.
  Reserve formal non-contraction prose for specific
  emphasis moments only (1-2 per CM max, 3-4 per DD max).
  Even Professional-level athletes receive 70-80%
  contraction usage — Coach Arron is still a human coach
  regardless of the athlete's competitive level.

  FILES TO CHANGE: VF_Coaching_Message_Framework (VOICE
  section), VF_Deep_Dive_Coaching_Analysis_Framework
  (VOICE section), brand_voice.txt or content_style_
  guide.txt (if register is specified there),
  SOP_Coaching_Output_Instructions (contraction rule)

ROUND 2: MEDIUM PRIORITY (5 recommendations)
------------------------------------------------------------

M1. DD STRUCTURAL VARIATION
  Audit finding: Every DD across all 7 athletes follows
  an identical internal template — same section order,
  same Strength Signal count (always 3-4), same Growth
  Area count (always 2-3), italicized capper on 100% of
  items.
  Proposed rules:
  - Strength Signal count may vary 2-5
  - Growth Area count may vary 1-4
  - Italicized observation frequency: 70-80% (1-2 items
    per DD may end without an italicized capper — let the
    evidence speak)
  - When used, italicized observations should vary in
    form: sometimes a question, sometimes a callback to
    athlete language, sometimes a concrete observation
    (not always a universal maxim like "The athletes who
    [X] are the ones who [Y]")
  - For disrupted weeks, permit section reordering
    (e.g., Growth Areas before Strength Signals when the
    disruption is the dominant story)

  FILES TO CHANGE: VF_Deep_Dive_Coaching_Analysis_
  Framework (section guidance rewrite)

M2. CM-DD DEDUPLICATION RULE
  Audit finding: Jasmine Wk7 has "That is not a small
  thing. That is a skill." verbatim in both CM and DD.
  John Wk1 has the same "The question is whether..."
  construction in both documents.
  Proposed rule: No phrase longer than 8 words should
  appear identically in both the CM and DD for the same
  week. The CM and DD should feel like two distinct
  pieces from the same coach, not two cuts from the same
  draft.

  FILES TO CHANGE: SOP_Coaching_Output_Instructions (new
  deduplication rule)

M3. CM OPENING FORMULA ROTATION
  Audit finding: Grace Wks 7-9 CMs use identical
  "[count]. [count]. And this week..." template. Tucker
  opens 3 of 4 CMs with Win/Missed day count.
  Proposed rule: Specify 5+ opening approaches:
    (a) Stat-led ("Sixty-three straight Win days.")
    (b) Quote-led ("'I spoke up.' Those three words...")
    (c) Moment-led ("Monday's pinch-hit strikeout...")
    (d) Challenge-led ("I want to push you on something.")
    (e) Context-led ("This was a recovery week, and...")
    (f) Callback-led ("Remember what you said in Week 3?")
  No athlete should see the same opening approach in
  consecutive weeks. The spec should define these as
  available tools, not a rotation mandate — the right
  opening depends on the week's character.

  FILES TO CHANGE: VF_Coaching_Message_Framework
  (opening guidance addition)

M4. TRANSITION ROTATION BANK
  Audit finding: "Here is what earned recognition" appears
  identically in 3 consecutive Grace CMs. "Here is what I
  want you to see" used across 5+ athletes.
  Proposed rule: Provide 6-8 alternative transitions for
  each coaching function (pivoting to recognition,
  pivoting to growth, framing challenges, bridging
  topics). Enforce variety against the prior 2-3 weeks of
  output for the same athlete. No transition phrase should
  repeat within a 3-week window for the same athlete.

  FILES TO CHANGE: SOP_Coaching_Output_Instructions
  (new transition variety section with example bank)

M5. BAN PIPELINE CLASSIFICATION LANGUAGE FROM DDs
  Audit finding: "Emotional intensity returned to Low,"
  "Consistent phase maintained," "Developing phase
  maintained," "Growth tier," "RQS [number]," "Stable
  Positive," "approach-framed" — all appear in DDs.
  "Approach-framed" is surfaced directly to athletes 3
  times. A college wrestler would not understand
  "approach-framed execution."
  Proposed rule: Add these terms to the prohibited
  vocabulary in the DD Framework and Coaching Output
  Instructions:
    "Emotional intensity [Low/Moderate/High]"
    "[Growth/Developing/Consistent] phase maintained"
    "Growth tier" / "Mixed tier" / "Reset tier"
    "RQS [number]" / "Reflection Quality Score"
    "Stable Positive" / "Partially Aligned" / "Aligned"
    "approach-framed"
    "pattern_shift_detected"
    "center-ring dominant" / "outer-ring dominant"
  Require behavioral description instead:
    "Your emotional weight was heavier this week" (not
     "Emotional intensity: Moderate")
    "You've been building steadily for four weeks" (not
     "Consistent phase maintained")
    "You competed to score, not to avoid losing" (not
     "approach-framed execution")

  FILES TO CHANGE: VF_Deep_Dive_Coaching_Analysis_
  Framework (prohibited vocabulary addition),
  SOP_Coaching_Output_Instructions (expanded prohibited
  list with behavioral replacements)

ROUND 3: LOW PRIORITY (3 recommendations)
------------------------------------------------------------

L1. VARY IDENTITY THREAD APPROACH
  Audit finding: Angelo's DDs enumerate all 4 baseline
  traits (goal-oriented, resilient, tenacious, sharp)
  every single week. Tucker's DDs repeat the "weekday
  version vs. weekend version" binary in Wks 4-6.
  Proposed rule: Do not enumerate all baseline traits
  every week. Pick 1-2 most relevant and go deeper.
  Reference the full set only every 3-4 weeks.

  FILES TO CHANGE: VF_Deep_Dive_Coaching_Analysis_
  Framework (Identity Thread guidance)

L2. ATHLETE-SPECIFIC CLOSING PHRASES
  Audit finding: "Control what's in your center ring" and
  "Lead yourself first" each used for 3+ athletes.
  Proposed rule: In addition to the standard rotation,
  develop 2-3 athlete-specific closings connected to each
  athlete's baseline identity claim or hinge habit.
  Example: Grace could receive "Show them what a chance
  looks like when you use it." Tucker could receive
  "Build the structure yourself." These supplement, not
  replace, the standard rotation.

  FILES TO CHANGE: VF_Coaching_Message_Framework (closing
  guidance), possibly individual athlete snapshots or a
  new reference section in the Instructions

L3. PRE-FIX DEEP DIVES (deferred)
  Audit finding: Session 69 regenerated 18 DDs (last 3
  weeks per athlete). 25+ older DDs still contain the
  pre-fix format: pipeline metadata headers, coach-facing

  Status: NOT in scope for this session. The regeneration

  govern all future generation. If a full historical

8. QUALITY VALIDATION PROTOCOL
============================================================

After each batch, validate a sample using these checks:

MECHANICAL CHECKS:
- Zero em dashes in any file
- "That is [noun]." standalone: ≤2 per CM, ≤2 per DD
- "Not X. Y." / "not X, but Y": ≤1 per CM, ≤2 per DD
- "The question is": 0 per CM, ≤1 per DD
- "Here is what [verb]": 0 (retired)
- "The version of you": 0 (retired)
- Contractions present (≥70% of contractable phrases)
- No trendline arc recitation past Week 4
- No pipeline classification language in athlete output

VOICE CHECKS (human judgment):
- Does the output sound like a real coach wrote it?
- Does the sentence rhythm vary within each paragraph?
- Do CMs for the same athlete vary in opening approach
  across consecutive weeks?
- Do DDs for the same athlete vary in Strength Signal
  count, Growth Area count, and structural approach?
- Is the coaching content accuracy preserved?

COMPLIANCE CHECKS:
- All 13 Editorial Audit criteria still pass
- No clinical language
- No pipeline data leakage
- No surveillance framing
- Format standards maintained (CM: header, separators,
  YOUR ACTION, focus question, closing, signature;
  DD: clean header, ALL CAPS, 850-1200 words)

9. WHAT NOT TO CHANGE
============================================================

The following are STRENGTHS identified in the audit and
must be preserved during all specification changes and
regeneration:

- Athlete-specific behavioral grounding (referencing
  specific days, events, and the athlete's own words)
- Domain and competitive level calibration (sport-
  specific language appropriate to each athlete)
- High EI week handling (empathy-first approach that
  produces the most natural, least formulaic writing)
- YOUR ACTION section quality (good variation in
  modality, specificity, and contextual subtitles)
- Week-type content differentiation (Gold Streak vs
  disrupted, competition vs recovery, High EI vs Low EI)
- Exemplar passages identified in the audit report
  (Section 8) — these represent the target voice quality.
  The goal is to make ALL output sound like these
  passages, not to change these passages.
