3. CONTEXT: WHY THIS IS HAPPENING
============================================================

The current v2.1 Stage 3 stack produces coaching output
that is polished, on-brand, and mechanically compliant, but
still shows detectable AI patterns at the rhetorical-move
level. The Voice Reference document was built in Sessions
96-97 to teach what Coach Arron sounds like at his best,
but it is not currently elevated as the top authority in
the active stack.

Three root causes were identified:

ROOT CAUSE 1: Authority-hierarchy conflict inside Stage 3
The Voice Reference presents itself as the primary voice
authority but is not operationally elevated. The active
stack gives high implicit authority to broad brand/content
documents (brand_voice.txt, brand_themes.txt,
content_style_guide.txt) that were NOT built for this
pipeline project. They are legacy marketing/social/email
documents dragged into Stage 3 as defaults.

ROOT CAUSE 2: Legacy document contamination
content_style_guide.txt contains structures like "Here's
the problem / Here's why / Here's the fix" and hook types
for viral posts, captions, CTAs. That rhetorical DNA leaks
into one-to-one coaching prose even when the exact phrases
are never reproduced. It teaches a posture of explanatory
branded content, not coach-athlete relationship.

brand_voice.txt mixes legitimate coaching voice principles
with audience-specific marketing tone adjustments and
brand phrases designed to function as marketing slogans.

brand_themes.txt is similar legacy content.

These files were built for a DIFFERENT project and got
absorbed into the pipeline as utility documents. They are
not purpose-built for Stage 3.

ROOT CAUSE 3: Internal contradictions in the stack
The CM Framework still legitimizes opening approaches
(stat-led, challenge-led) that the Voice Reference later
flags as AI tells. The model receives mixed instruction:
one file says this opening type is available, another
says the same move is a tell. When instructions contradict,
the model produces safe averages, not voice-authentic
prose.

The three-layer architecture locked in Session 96 (Voice
Reference as teacher during generation, Section 35 as
mechanical safety net, Stage 5 as post-generation audit)
requires the authority stack to be consistent. v2.1 got
the teacher and safety net parts right but did not
restructure the authority stack. v2.2 completes that work.

4. LOCKED DESIGN DECISIONS (DO NOT RE-DEBATE)
============================================================

DECISION 1: Purpose-Built Principle
Every document in the Stage 3 source stack must be
explicitly purpose-built for Stage 3 athlete-facing and
parent-facing weekly outputs. Legacy catch-all documents
do not belong in the authoritative source list.

DECISION 2: New Foundations Document (Not File Limitation)
Instead of limiting the legacy files' scope (which would
leave mixed authority), create a new pipeline-native
document that replaces their rhetorical authority for
Stage 3. Legacy files are REMOVED from Stage 3 authoritative
sources entirely. They remain on disk for whatever other
stages (Stage 4, Stage 6) currently reference them. Those
stages will be evaluated separately in future sessions.

DECISION 3: Voice Reference Stays at Top
VF_Coach_Arron_Voice_Reference.txt remains the primary
voice authority. Nothing in v2.2 changes that. Everything
else is subordinate for voice/rhetorical matters.

DECISION 4: Frameworks Define Coverage, Not Voice
CM Framework and DD Framework remain authoritative for:
  - Mandatory sections
  - Word targets
  - Output format (headers, separators, signatures)
  - Coverage requirements
They cede rhetorical/voice authority to the Voice Reference.
They are NOT converted to post-hoc validation layers (the
external reviewer's suggestion to do so was flagged as
incongruent with the pipeline architecture — Stage 5
already serves that function).

DECISION 5: Ban Moves, Not Just Phrases
Section 35 v2.2 explicitly adopts "ban moves, not just
phrases" as its framing principle. This matches what the
Voice Reference already teaches via exemplars.

DECISION 6: Uneven Coverage IS a Feature (with caveat)
Coaching prose should spend words proportionally to what
matters most in the week. A defining moment gets space.
A stable pattern gets acknowledgment. Uneven coverage is
deliberate.

CRITICAL CAVEAT: Uneven coverage means PROPORTIONAL
ATTENTION, not selective omission. Every significant
thread in the week (hinge habit status, derailer activation,
competition events, behavioral patterns, athlete voice)
must still be read and accounted for. The unevenness is
in how much space each gets, not in what gets covered.
The coach does not skip important data to make the
message sound more "real." The coach weights the data
according to what matters most THIS week.

DECISION 7: Exemplar Category Matching (No Retrieval Rebuild)
The external reviewer suggested building a retrieval system
to match exemplars to scenarios before generation. This
was flagged as architecturally incongruent — our pipeline
loads all source files at generation time, there is no
RAG layer. The simpler equivalent is to add explicit
guidance in v2.2: before generating, identify which Voice
Reference exemplar category best matches this week's
dominant coaching function (opening for hard week, handling
failure, reading a trendline, etc.) and use those specific
exemplars as primary calibration for tone, pacing, and
move selection. No architectural change needed.

DECISION 8: Reusable Opening Test
New audit test from the external reviewer, adopted:
"If the opening could be reused next week by changing
only the number, it is too templated."
Added to Section 35 v2.2 as guidance, added to Stage 5
v1.6 Criterion 14 as a fail condition.

DECISION 9: Criterion 14 Scope
Stage 5 v1.6 adds Criterion 14: Voice Quality Regression.
Tier 2 (auto-correct where possible, reject if not).
The fail conditions detect the patterns the old
C3/C5/C6/C7/C9/C10 rules tried to prevent at generation
time. Those generation rules are removed from v2.2 in
favor of audit-stage detection. The Voice Reference is
the quality benchmark the audit measures against.

DECISION 10: Phased Migration
Stage 3 is the first purpose-built migration. It becomes
the template. Stage 4 and Stage 6 legacy file evaluations
happen in SEPARATE FUTURE SESSIONS, not as part of this
build. Do not cascade changes into other stages during
this session.

    "OPENING SELECTION RULE

    Open with the most meaningful fact of the week, not
    the most available format. The right opening depends
    on what THIS week was about, not a menu of options.

    Preferred openings (in priority order):
      1. A defining moment from the week — the specific
         event, decision, or behavior that the week most
         turned on
      2. A defining meaning statement — an interpretive
         claim about what the week reveals, delivered
         before any data
      3. Athlete language that changes the reading of
         the week — a specific phrase or sentence from
         the athlete's recap that, when interpreted,
         reframes the week
      4. A difficult reality that must be acknowledged
         first — when the week contained genuine
         difficulty, lead with acknowledgment before any
         coaching

      - Stat-led openings that could be reused next week
        by changing only the number. If your opening is
        'X straight Win days' or 'Y consecutive Growth
        weeks,' it is templated. Use this only when the
        stat itself genuinely defines the week (a
        milestone, a record, a first).
      - Challenge-led openings that announce a coaching
        push instead of delivering it. 'I want to push
        you on something this week' is a setup sentence
        that does not belong. If you have a push, make
        it. Do not announce it.
      - Openings that could fit another athlete with
        only surface edits. If you could swap the name
        and the opening still works, it is too generic.

    REUSABLE OPENING TEST: If the opening of a new message
    could be reused next week by changing only the number
    or date, it is too templated. Rewrite it to lead with
    something specific to this week's meaning.

    See VF_Coach_Arron_Voice_Reference.txt Category A
    (Opening a Message) for 3 exemplars demonstrating
    strong openings across different scenarios."

CHANGE C1.3: Add Coverage vs Rhetorical Template Clarification
  In the STRUCTURE section (or a new note at the top of
  it), add:

    "STRUCTURE DEFINES COVERAGE, NOT RHETORIC. The

    covered in every Coaching Message. It does NOT

    appear in prose, how they must be transitioned

    "SECTION 14: CRITERION 14 — VOICE QUALITY REGRESSION
    Tier: 2 (SHOULD PASS, auto-correct where possible,
    reject if not)
    Applies to: Coaching Message AND Deep Dive

    PURPOSE:
    Detects AI-pattern regressions in voice and
    rhetorical moves that the Voice Calibration Reference
    is designed to prevent. Complements the Voice
    Reference by catching patterns that slipped through
    generation. The Voice Reference is the quality
    benchmark this criterion measures against.

    CHECK LOGIC:
    Scan both documents for the following fail
    conditions. Flag any detected.

    FAIL CONDITION 14.1: Reusable Opening
    The opening of the CM or DD could be reused next
    week by changing only the number, date, or surface
    detail. Common forms:
      - Stat-led openings with running totals (e.g.,
        'X straight Win days. Y consecutive Growth
        weeks.')
      - Template openings where substituting another
        athlete would still work
      - Generic scene-setters that do not reference
        anything specific from THIS week

    FAIL CONDITION 14.2: Telegraphing
    A sentence announces the coaching move instead of
    making it. Common forms:
      - 'Now I want to push you on something this week.'
      - 'Here is what I want you to see.'
      - 'There is something I want to talk about.'
      - 'I'm going to be direct about something.'
    The announcement is the tell. If the sentence can
    be deleted and the paragraph still works, it was
    scaffolding.

    FAIL CONDITION 14.3: Untraceable Paragraph
    A paragraph is not clearly traceable to a specific
    athlete behavior, statement, or moment from the
    week. Every paragraph in a CM or DD should be
    responding to something the athlete did or said.
    Paragraphs that apply generic coaching principles
    without specific athlete grounding fail this check.

    FAIL CONDITION 14.4: Category Coverage Over Response
    The message reads like it is covering required
    categories (recognition, strength, drift, identity,
    action) rather than responding to THIS week. Signs:
      - Evenly balanced paragraphs when the week was
        not evenly balanced
      - Transition sentences that announce section
        shifts
      - Coverage of every field in the JSON regardless
        of relevance
      - The message reads like a report, not a
        conversation

    FAIL CONDITION 14.5: Brand Copy Close
    The closing reads like marketing copy, a motivational
    slogan, or a generic brand phrase rather than
    athlete-specific language. The close should connect
    to something from THIS athlete's arc: identity
    claim, hinge habit, a specific moment, or their own
    language. Standard rotation phrases are acceptable
    as fallback only.

    FAIL CONDITION 14.6: Same Construction in 3-Week Window
    The same rhetorical construction appears in the
    current week's CM or DD and in either of the prior
    2 weeks for the same athlete. Common forms to check:
      - Same opening approach
      - Same transition phrase
      - Same italicized observation structure
      - Same closing phrase
      - Same reframe move

    FAIL CONDITION 14.7: DD as Expanded CM
    The Deep Dive reads like an expanded or repeated
    version of the Coaching Message instead of a
    different angle on the same week. The CM is the
    highlight reel. The DD is the full picture. They
    should feel like two complementary perspectives from
    the same coach, not the same draft at two lengths.
    Check for substantial phrase duplication between CM
    and DD for the same week (more than a few words of
    natural coaching vocabulary).

    FAIL CONDITION 14.8: 'That is [Noun].' Standalone Overuse
    'That is [noun].' standalone declaratives (e.g.,
    'That is ownership.') appearing more than once in
    a CM, more than once in a DD, or in both CM and DD
    for the same week. Occasional use as genuine
    emphasis is acceptable. Repeated use as a structural
    habit fails this check.

    FAIL CONDITION 14.9: 'Version of [X]' Construction
    Any use of 'the version of you,' 'the version of
    confidence,' 'the version of [athlete's quality]'
    constructions. These are retired entirely.

    FAIL CONDITION 14.10: Triple-Negative Setup
    'Not X, not Y, not Z. Just W.' constructions as
    openers or transitions. This is a recognizable AI
    pattern and should be replaced with direct positive
    framing.

    FAIL CONDITION 14.11: Trendline Arc Recitation
    DD Weekly Trendline section enumerates more than 3
    prior weeks or uses hyphenated compound-adjective
    arc labels ('outcome-reactive,' 'preparation-
    driven,' 'structure-sustained,' etc.). Trendline
    should reference 2-3 inflection points relevant to
    THIS week's story.

    FAIL CONDITION 14.12: Pipeline-Adjacent Vocabulary
    Terminology that is not a direct Category E/F/G
    violation (which would fail Criterion 13) but still
    reads as pipeline-adjacent:
      - 'Growth weeks' / 'Win days' as named categories
      - 'approach-framed' (in any form)
      - 'the system' as a reference to the VirtusFocus
        program (when it reads clinically)
      - Other jargon-adjacent terms that a real coach
        would not use

    AUTO-CORRECTION RULES FOR CRITERION 14:
    Auto-correct only when the fix is clear and
    mechanical. Examples:
      - Fail 14.2 (telegraphing): Delete the announcing
        sentence. If the following sentence now reads
        naturally, the correction is complete.
      - Fail 14.8 (That is [Noun] overuse): Convert
        extra instances to alternate constructions
        (fragment, embedded judgment, athlete callback).
      - Fail 14.9 (version of X): Replace 'the version
        of [X]' with '[X]' or direct language.
      - Fail 14.11 (trendline recitation): Trim to 2-3
        inflection points with plain-language
        descriptions.
      - Fail 14.12 (pipeline-adjacent): Replace with
        behavioral description.

    REJECT AND REGENERATE when:
      - Fail 14.1 (reusable opening): Requires full
        opening rewrite, not mechanical replacement.
      - Fail 14.3 (untraceable paragraph): Requires
        generation of new content grounded in athlete
        data.
      - Fail 14.4 (category coverage over response):
        Structural problem requiring full regeneration.
      - Fail 14.7 (DD as expanded CM): Requires DD to
        be rewritten as a different angle.
      - Multiple auto-correctable fails (3+): Reject
        rather than patch.

    REGENERATION INSTRUCTION TEMPLATE:
    When rejecting for Criterion 14, include in the
    regeneration instruction:
      - Which fail conditions triggered
      - The specific passage(s) that failed
      - Reference to the relevant Voice Reference
        exemplar category for calibration
      - Explicit instruction to re-read the Voice
        Reference before regenerating

    RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER CRITERIA:
    Criterion 11 (Brand Voice Tonal Scan) handles
    tonal register violations (hype, cliche, guilt,
    academic, preachy). Criterion 14 handles voice
    quality regression at the rhetorical-move level.
    They are distinct and both apply.

    Criterion 13 (Pipeline Data Leakage) handles raw
    pipeline data surfacing (Category E/F/G). Criterion
    14 handles pipeline-adjacent vocabulary that does
    not directly surface data but reads as jargon.
    They are distinct and both apply."
